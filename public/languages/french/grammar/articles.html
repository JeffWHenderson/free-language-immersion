French Articles — le, la, les, un, une

Every French noun has a grammatical gender — either masculine or feminine — and articles must agree with that gender and number. There is no neutral gender in French. Mastering articles is one of the first and most important steps in French grammar.

Definite Articles — "The"

Use definite articles when referring to a specific, known noun, or when speaking about something in general (e.g., "I like coffee" = likes coffee in general).

Gender / NumberArticleExample
Masculine singularlele café, le livre, le pain
Feminine singularlala table, la maison, la ville
Before a vowel or mute hl'l'eau, l'hôtel, l'ami, l'amie
All plurallesles livres, les tables, les amis

Indefinite Articles — "A / An / Some"

Use indefinite articles when introducing a noun for the first time, or when it is one of many.

Gender / NumberArticleExample
Masculine singularunun café, un livre, un ami
Feminine singularuneune table, une maison, une amie
All pluraldesdes livres, des tables, des amis

Examples

  • le café the coffee (masc.)
  • la table the table (fem.)
  • l'eau the water (fem., before vowel)
  • les enfants the children (plural)
  • un livre a book (masc.)
  • une pomme an apple (fem.)
  • des amis some friends (plural)
  • J'aime le chocolat. I like chocolate. (general liking → definite article)
  • Je veux un café. I want a coffee. (one specific coffee → indefinite)

Important Contractions

When le or les follow the prepositions à (at/to) or de (of/from), they contract:

Preposition + ArticleContractionExample
à + leauJe vais au café. (I'm going to the café.)
à + lesauxJe parle aux enfants. (I speak to the children.)
de + leduLe prix du café. (The price of the coffee.)
de + lesdesLe prix des livres. (The price of the books.)

Note: à + la and à + l' do NOT contract: à la maison, à l'école.

Common Pitfalls

  • After negation, un/une/des change to de (or d' before a vowel): Je n'ai pas de livre. (I don't have a book.)
  • Professions after être drop the article: Elle est médecin. (She is a doctor.)
  • General statements use the definite article in French but not in English: J'aime les chats. (I like cats.)
  • The gender of nouns must be memorized — there are patterns (nouns ending in -tion, -sion, -ure are usually feminine; nouns ending in -ment, -age are usually masculine) but many exceptions exist.
