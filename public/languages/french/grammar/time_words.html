Time Words & Days

Time expressions in French allow you to anchor any sentence in time. They are flexible in position — usually at the very start or end of a sentence — and many are used constantly in everyday speech.

Days are lowercase in French. Never capitalise them: lundi not Lundi.

"On Monday" — Habitual vs. Specific

le + day = habitual (every week)
  • Le lundi, je travaille. On Mondays, I work.
  • Elle fait du sport le vendredi. She exercises on Fridays.
day alone = this coming day
  • Lundi, je travaille. On Monday (this Monday), I work.
  • On se voit vendredi ? Shall we meet (this) Friday?

Position of Time Expressions

Unlike English, French time expressions can go either at the start or the end of a sentence. They are not placed between the subject and the verb.

Aujourd'hui je travaille. ✓ — Today I work.
Je travaille aujourd'hui. ✓ — I work today.
Je aujourd'hui travaille. ✗ — do not place between subject and verb.

Example Sentences

  • Hier soir, je suis allé au cinéma. Yesterday evening, I went to the cinema.
  • On se retrouve demain matin ? Shall we meet tomorrow morning?
  • Je travaille toujours le samedi. I always work on Saturdays.
  • Maintenant je comprends ! Now I understand!
