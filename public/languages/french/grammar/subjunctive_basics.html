Le Subjonctif — The Subjunctive Mood

The subjunctive expresses wishes, emotions, doubt, necessity, and hypothetical situations. It is used in a subordinate clause introduced by que, after certain triggers.

Common Triggers for the Subjunctive

CategoryExpressionEnglish
Wish / desirevouloir que, souhaiter que, préférer quewant that, wish that, prefer that
Necessityil faut que, il est nécessaire queit is necessary that
Emotionêtre content(e) que, être triste que, avoir peur que, regretter quebe glad that, be sad that, be afraid that, regret that
Doubtdouter que, ne pas croire que, ne pas penser quedoubt that, not believe that
Conjunctionsbien que, pour que, avant que, à moins quealthough, so that, before, unless

Two Subjects Required

The subjunctive is only used when the subject of the main clause differs from the subject of the subordinate clause. If the subjects are the same, use the infinitive.

  • Je veux que tu viennes. (two subjects) I want you to come.
  • Je veux venir. (same subject) I want to come.
  • J'ai peur qu'il soit malade. I'm afraid that he's sick.
  • Il faut qu'elle vienne. It's necessary that she come.
  • Je suis content que tu sois là. I'm glad that you're here.
