Y et EN — Location & Quantity Pronouns

y and en are adverbial pronouns that replace prepositional phrases. Like direct object pronouns, they are placed before the conjugated verb.

Y — Replacing à + Place or à + Thing

y replaces a location phrase (à, en, chez, dans, sur… + place) or à + an abstract thing.

Original sentenceWith yEnglish
Je vais au magasin.J'y vais.I'm going there.
Elle habite à Paris.Elle y habite.She lives there.
Nous allons en France.Nous y allons.We're going there.
Je pense à ce problème.J'y pense.I'm thinking about it.

EN — Replacing de + Noun (Quantity or Origin)

en replaces de + noun in partitive or quantity contexts, and de + place (origin).

Original sentenceWith enEnglish
Tu veux du pain ?Tu en veux ?Do you want some?
J'ai beaucoup de travail.J'en ai beaucoup.I have a lot of it.
Elle vient du marché.Elle en vient.She's coming from there.
Je parle de ce sujet.J'en parle.I'm talking about it.

Placement Rules

  • J'y vais. I'm going there. (before conjugated verb)
  • Je vais y aller. I'm going to go there. (before infinitive)
  • Je n'y vais pas. I'm not going there. (ne wraps y + verb)
  • Vas-y ! Go there! / Go ahead! (positive imperative: pronoun after verb + hyphen)
  • N'y va pas ! Don't go there! (negative imperative: pronoun before verb)

EN with Numbers

When a number or quantity word is used, en is still required even though the quantity is stated.

  • Tu as des enfants ? — J'en ai deux. Do you have children? — I have two (of them).
  • Il en reste beaucoup. There is a lot left.
  • Il n'en reste plus. There is none left.
