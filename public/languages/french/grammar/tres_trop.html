Intensifiers — très, trop, assez, vraiment, si

French has a rich set of adverbs that intensify or qualify adjectives and other adverbs. These small words make a huge difference to meaning and are essential for natural expression. They are placed directly before the adjective or adverb they modify.

Très — Very

The most common intensifier. Used with adjectives and adverbs, but not with past participles used as adjectives (use très carefully with nouns — use beaucoup for verbs):

  • C'est très bon. It's very good.
  • Je suis très fatigué. I'm very tired.
  • Il parle très vite. He speaks very quickly.
  • C'est très bien ! It's very good! / Well done!

Trop — Too / Too Much

Trop implies excess — more than is desired or acceptable:

  • C'est trop cher. It's too expensive.
  • Il y a trop de sel. There is too much salt.
  • Tu parles trop vite. You speak too fast.
  • J'ai trop mangé. I ate too much.

Note: In informal spoken French, "trop" is increasingly used to mean "very" (like English "so"): "C'est trop bien !" (It's so great!). This is slang — avoid it in formal contexts.

Assez — Quite / Enough

Assez has two meanings depending on position:

  • C'est assez difficile. It's quite difficult. (= fairly)
  • Je n'ai pas assez de temps. I don't have enough time. (= sufficient)
  • J'en ai assez ! I've had enough! (= I'm fed up)

Si vs Tellement — So

  • C'est si beau ! It's so beautiful! (emotional)
  • Je suis tellement content ! I'm so happy! (so/such)
  • Il mange tellement vite. He eats so fast.

With Nouns — Beaucoup de, Trop de, Assez de

When intensifying a quantity before a noun, use the intensifier + de (no article):

  • beaucoup de travail a lot of work
  • trop de bruit too much noise
  • assez de place enough space
  • un peu de café a little coffee

Common Pitfalls

  • Très vs Beaucoup: Très modifies adjectives and adverbs; beaucoup modifies verbs: Je travaille beaucoup (I work a lot) — NOT "je travaille très."
  • Trop de vs Trop: Before a noun, use trop de; before an adjective, use trop alone: trop de café / trop chaud.
  • Intensifiers do not change form in French — they are invariable adverbs. No gender or number agreement needed.
