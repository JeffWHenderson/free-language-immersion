La Voix Passive — Passive Voice

In the passive voice, the subject receives the action rather than performing it. In French, the passive is formed with être + past participle. The past participle agrees in gender and number with the subject, just like an adjective. The agent (who performs the action) is introduced by par.

Formation

ElementFormNote
Subjectnoun or pronoun receiving the actionDetermines agreement of participle
Auxiliaryêtre (conjugated in any tense)Tense of être = tense of the passive
Past Participleagrees with subject in gender & number-é / -ée / -és / -ées for -er verbs
Agentpar + agent (optional)Can be omitted if unknown

Active vs. Passive

ActivePassiveEnglish
Le directeur signe le contrat.Le contrat est signé par le directeur.The contract is signed by the manager.
L'équipe a terminé le projet.Le projet a été terminé par l'équipe.The project was finished by the team.
On embauchera deux ingénieurs.Deux ingénieurs seront embauchés.Two engineers will be hired.

Tense Shown on Être

Any tense can be used. The tense is carried entirely by the conjugation of être; the participle itself does not change tense:

TensePassive constructionEnglish
PrésentLa réunion est organisée.The meeting is organised.
Passé composéLa réunion a été organisée.The meeting was organised.
ImparfaitLa réunion était organisée chaque lundi.The meeting was organised every Monday.
Futur simpleLa réunion sera organisée demain.The meeting will be organised tomorrow.
ConditionnelLa réunion serait organisée.The meeting would be organised.

Agreement of the Past Participle

The past participle agrees with the grammatical subject (the noun receiving the action):

  • Le dossier est préparé. The file is prepared. (masc. sing.)
  • La facture est préparée. The invoice is prepared. (fem. sing.)
  • Les documents sont préparés. The documents are prepared. (masc. pl.)
  • Les lettres sont préparées. The letters are prepared. (fem. pl.)

Alternatives to the Passive

French often avoids the passive by using on (one / they / people) as a subject, or a reflexive verb (se + verb):

StrategyExampleEnglish
on + active verbOn embauche des développeurs ici.Developers are hired here. (lit. One hires…)
reflexive (se + verb)Ça se fait comme ça au bureau.That's done that way at the office.
reflexive (se + verb)Ce produit se vend très bien.This product sells very well.

More Example Sentences

  • Les candidats seront contactés par email. The candidates will be contacted by email.
  • Ce rapport a été rédigé par mon collègue. This report was written by my colleague.
  • On parle français ici. French is spoken here.
  • La décision a été prise par le directeur. The decision was made by the manager.
