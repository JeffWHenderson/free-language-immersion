Prepositions of Place

French prepositions of place describe where something or someone is located. Many of them require the article that follows them to contract, so it's important to learn them together with their usage patterns.

Core Prepositions of Place

FrenchEnglishExample
dansin, insideLe chat est dans la boîte. (The cat is in the box.)
suron, on top ofLe livre est sur la table. (The book is on the table.)

À — At / To

À is extremely versatile. It indicates location (at a place) or direction (to a place). Remember the mandatory contractions:

  • Je suis au café. I'm at the café. (à + le = au)
  • Elle est à la bibliothèque. She's at the library.
  • On va aux magasins. We're going to the shops. (à + les = aux)
  • Je vais à l'école. I'm going to school.

Chez — At Someone's Place

Chez means "at the home/place of" and is used before a person's name or pronoun:

  • Je suis chez Marie. I'm at Marie's (place).
  • On va chez toi ? Are we going to your place?
  • Je rentre chez moi. I'm going home. (lit. to my place)
  • chez le médecin at the doctor's (office)

En vs Dans

Both can mean "in" — but with different nuances:

PrepositionUseExample
dansInside a physical space or container; within a defined areadans le sac, dans la voiture, dans la pièce
enWith countries (fem.), materials, languages, time expressionsen France, en coton, en français, en cinq minutes

Examples in Context

  • Le restaurant est en face de la banque. The restaurant is across from the bank.
  • Les toilettes sont au fond à droite. The toilets are at the back on the right.
  • Ma chambre est à côté de la salle de bain. My room is next to the bathroom.
  • Il y a une fontaine au milieu de la place. There is a fountain in the middle of the square.

Common Pitfalls

  • Many place prepositions require de, which contracts: à côté du café (not "à côté de le"), près de l'école.
  • Dans la rue = on/in the street; sur la route = on the road. The prepositions don't always match English intuitions.
  • Chez cannot be used with places that are not homes or professional offices. Use à/au/dans for shops, buildings, etc.
