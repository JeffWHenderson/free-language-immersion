Pouvoir & Vouloir — Can & Want

Pouvoir (to be able to / can) and vouloir (to want) are two essential modal-like verbs in French. Both are irregular and both are typically followed by an infinitive. They are among the most frequently used verbs in everyday conversation.

Pouvoir — To Be Able To / Can

PronounPouvoir
jepeux
tupeux
il / elle / onpeut
nouspouvons
vouspouvez
ils / ellespeuvent

Pouvoir in Use

  • Je peux t'aider. I can help you.
  • Tu peux venir ce soir ? Can you come tonight?
  • Elle peut parler français. She can speak French.
  • On ne peut pas partir maintenant. We can't leave now.
  • Vous pouvez m'aider ? Can you help me? (formal/plural)

Vouloir — To Want

PronounVouloir
jeveux
tuveux
il / elle / onveut
nousvoulons
vousvoulez
ils / ellesveulent

Vouloir in Use

  • Je veux un café. I want a coffee.
  • Tu veux manger quelque chose ? Do you want to eat something?
  • Elle veut partir tôt. She wants to leave early.
  • On veut visiter le musée. We want to visit the museum.
  • Ils veulent rester ici. They want to stay here.

Je voudrais — Polite "I Would Like"

Je voudrais is the conditional form of vouloir and is much more polite than je veux. Use it when ordering in a restaurant, making a request, or addressing strangers.

  • Je voudrais un café, s'il vous plaît. I would like a coffee, please.
  • Je voudrais réserver une table. I would like to reserve a table.
  • Vous voudriez quelque chose d'autre ? Would you like anything else?

Both Verbs + Infinitive

Like English "can" and "want," both verbs are followed directly by an infinitive — no preposition needed:

  • Je peux parler. I can speak.
  • Je veux parler. I want to speak.
  • Il ne peut pas venir. He can't come.
  • Elle ne veut pas manger. She doesn't want to eat.

Common Pitfalls

  • Je veux vs je voudrais: Using je veux with strangers or in formal settings can sound blunt or rude. Prefer je voudrais in restaurants, shops, and any service context.
  • Puis-je is the formal/written inversion of je peux used in questions: Puis-je vous aider ? (May I help you?)
  • Both verbs have irregular stems throughout — notice that nous/vous forms (pouvons, voulez) look quite different from the other forms (peux/peut, veux/veut). This is called a "boot verb" pattern.
