Negation — ne...pas

To make a sentence negative in French, you wrap the verb with ne...pas. This two-part structure is the core of French negation. Unlike English, the "not" is split into two pieces placed around the verb.

Basic Structure

Subject + ne + verb + pas + (rest of sentence)

  • Je mange. → Je ne mange pas. I eat. → I don't eat.
  • Il parle. → Il ne parle pas. He speaks. → He doesn't speak.
  • Vous comprenez. → Vous ne comprenez pas. You understand. → You don't understand.

Elision Before Vowels

Ne shortens to n' before a verb beginning with a vowel or mute h:

  • Je n'ai pas faim. I'm not hungry.
  • Il n'est pas là. He's not here.
  • On n'habite pas ici. We don't live here.

Negation with Modal Verbs (pouvoir, vouloir, etc.)

When a modal verb is followed by an infinitive, ne...pas wraps only the modal:

  • Je ne peux pas venir. I can't come.
  • Elle ne veut pas manger. She doesn't want to eat.
  • On ne va pas partir. We're not going to leave.

Other Negative Expressions

Negation with Articles

After ne...pas, the indefinite articles (un, une, des) and partitive articles (du, de la, de l') change to de / d':

  • J'ai un chien. → Je n'ai pas de chien. I have a dog. → I don't have a dog.
  • Je bois du lait. → Je ne bois pas de lait. I drink milk. → I don't drink milk.
  • Elle a des amis. → Elle n'a pas d'amis. She has friends. → She has no friends.

The definite article (le, la, les) does NOT change after negation: "Je n'aime pas le café."

Spoken French — Dropping "ne"

In informal spoken French, the ne is very commonly dropped. Only pas (or the other negative word) is kept:

  • C'est pas vrai ! That's not true! (informal)
  • J'sais pas. I don't know. (informal; "je ne sais pas")
  • T'es pas là ? Aren't you there? (informal)

While this is natural in speech, always write the full ne...pas in formal written French.

Common Pitfalls

  • Beginners often place "pas" before the verb instead of after it. Remember: ne before, pas after: Je ne mange pas.
  • Personne and rien can also be subjects: Personne ne sait. (Nobody knows.) Rien n'est parfait. (Nothing is perfect.) — here ne is still required.
