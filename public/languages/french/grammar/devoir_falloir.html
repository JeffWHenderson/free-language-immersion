Devoir & Il Faut — Must / Have To

devoir (conjugated) and il faut (impersonal) both express obligation or necessity. They are followed by an infinitive.

Devoir — Conjugation (Present)

SubjectDevoirExample
jedoisJe dois partir. — I must leave.
tudoisTu dois manger. — You must eat.
il / elledoitIl doit rester. — He must stay.
nousdevonsNous devons y aller. — We must go.
vousdevezVous devez signer. — You must sign.
ils / ellesdoiventIls doivent partir. — They must leave.

Devoir in Other Tenses

TenseFormMeaningExample
Passé composéj'ai dûI had toJ'ai dû appeler le médecin.
Imparfaitje devaisI was supposed to / I had to (ongoing)Je devais travailler.
Conditionnelje devraisI should (advice)Tu devrais te reposer.

Il Faut — Impersonal Obligation

il faut + infinitive = it is necessary to / one must. It has no specified subject and applies generally.

  • Il faut manger des légumes. One must eat vegetables. / You need to eat vegetables.
  • Il faut que + subjunctive It is necessary that… (specifies a person — triggers subjunctive)
  • Il ne faut pas fumer ici. You must not smoke here. (prohibition)

Devoir vs. Il Faut

DevoirIl faut
SubjectSpecific (je dois, tu dois…)Impersonal (no subject)
TonePersonal obligation or expectationGeneral rule or necessity
Negationne pas devoir = must not / not supposed toil ne faut pas = must not (strong prohibition)

More Examples

  • Je dois me reposer. I must rest.
  • Tu devrais voir un médecin. You should see a doctor. (advice, softer)
  • Il faut prendre ce médicament deux fois par jour. You must take this medicine twice a day.
  • J'ai dû aller aux urgences. I had to go to the emergency room.
