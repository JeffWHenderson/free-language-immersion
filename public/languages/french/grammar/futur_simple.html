Le Futur Simple — Simple Future

The futur simple expresses future actions, predictions, and certainty about what will happen. It is formed by adding endings directly to the infinitive (dropping the final -e for -re verbs). It is more formal and confident-sounding than the futur proche.

Formation — Regular Endings

For all verbs, attach these endings to the infinitive stem (infinitive minus final -e for -re verbs):

Futur Simple vs. Futur Proche

Both tenses talk about the future, but with different nuances:

Futur Proche (aller + infinitive)Futur Simple
FeelingNear / planned / personal intentionMore distant / certain / formal / predicted
RegisterConversational, everyday speechWritten, formal, or for emphasis
ExampleJe vais partir demain.Je partirai demain.
MeaningI'm going to leave tomorrow.I will leave tomorrow.

Example Sentences

  • Demain, je parlerai avec mon chef. Tomorrow, I will speak with my boss.
  • Elle finira le projet avant vendredi. She will finish the project before Friday.
  • Nous irons à Paris l'été prochain. We will go to Paris next summer.
  • Tu verras, ce sera fantastique. You'll see, it will be fantastic.
  • Ils auront besoin d'aide. They will need help.
  • Quand tu arriveras, appelle-moi. When you arrive, call me. (Note: French uses future after quand, unlike English.)
