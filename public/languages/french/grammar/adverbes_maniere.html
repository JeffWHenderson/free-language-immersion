Les Adverbes en -ment — Adverbs of Manner

French adverbs of manner are typically formed by adding -ment to the feminine form of an adjective. They describe how an action is performed, equivalent to English "-ly" adverbs.

Adjectives Ending in a Vowel (Masculine Form)

When the masculine adjective already ends in a vowel, add -ment directly to the masculine form:

Adjectives Ending in -ant / -ent

Adjectives ending in -ant form adverbs with -amment; those ending in -ent use -emment. Both endings are pronounced the same: like "amman".

Position in the Sentence

The placement of adverbs varies by length:

  • Elle a bien chanté. (short adverb) She sang well. — short adverbs (bien, mal, vite, souvent) come before the past participle.
  • Il a parlé tristement. (long -ment adverb) He spoke sadly. — long adverbs typically come after the past participle.
  • Heureusement, il est arrivé à l'heure. Fortunately, he arrived on time. — sentence adverbs may open the sentence.

Common Adverbs of Manner

  • bien / mal well / badly (irregular — not formed from -ment)
  • vite quickly (also irregular — no -ment)
  • fort loudly / hard (adverb = same as masculine adjective)
  • ensemble together
