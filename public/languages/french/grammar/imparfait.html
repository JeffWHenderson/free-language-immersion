L'Imparfait — The Imperfect Tense

The imparfait describes ongoing states, habitual actions, and background descriptions in the past. It contrasts with the passé composé, which describes completed, punctual events.

Formation

Take the nous form of the present tense, remove -ons, and add the imparfait endings:

SubjectEndingparler (stem: parl-)
je-aisje parlais
tu-aistu parlais
il / elle / on-aitil parlait
nous-ionsnous parlions
vous-iezvous parliez
ils / elles-aientils parlaient

Exception: être has an irregular stem: ét- → j'étais, tu étais, il était, nous étions, vous étiez, ils étaient.

When to Use the Imparfait

UseExampleEnglish
Habitual / repeated actionJe courais tous les jours.I used to run every day.
Ongoing background stateIl faisait beau.The weather was nice.
Feelings / mental statesJ'étais fatigué.I was tired.
Age, time, descriptionsElle avait vingt ans.She was twenty.
Ongoing action interrupted by an eventJe dormais quand il est arrivé.I was sleeping when he arrived.

Imparfait vs. Passé Composé

This distinction is one of the most important in French. Use this as a guide:

  • J'avais mal. (imparfait) I was in pain / I had an ache. (ongoing state)
  • J'ai eu mal. (passé composé) I felt a sudden pain. (completed event)
  • Elle chantait. (imparfait) She was singing / She used to sing. (background)
  • Elle a chanté. (passé composé) She sang. (one completed performance)

Signal Words for the Imparfait

  • tous les jours / chaque jour every day
  • souvent / toujours / d'habitude often / always / usually
  • autrefois / à l'époque in the past / back then
  • quand j'étais jeune when I was young
