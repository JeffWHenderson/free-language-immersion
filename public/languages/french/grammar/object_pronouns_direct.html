Direct Object Pronouns — le, la, les, me

Direct object pronouns replace a noun that directly receives the action of the verb. They avoid repetition when the noun has already been mentioned.

The Pronouns

PronounMeaningExample
me (m')meTu me connais. — You know me.
te (t')youJe te vois. — I see you.
le (l')him / it (masc.)Je le prends. — I take it.
la (l')her / it (fem.)Il la cherche. — He's looking for her.
noususIls nous invitent. — They invite us.
vousyou (pl./formal)Je vous appelle. — I'll call you.
lesthemElle les lit. — She reads them.

Position Rules

The pronoun goes directly before the conjugated verb. With infinitives, it goes before the infinitive.

  • Je le vois. I see him/it. (before conjugated verb)
  • Je veux le voir. I want to see him/it. (before infinitive)
  • Je ne le vois pas. I don't see him/it. (ne...pas wraps the pronoun + verb)

In Passé Composé

The pronoun goes before the helper verb (avoir/être).

  • Je l'ai vu. I saw him/it.
  • Tu les as pris. You took them.
  • Elle ne m'a pas appelé. She didn't call me.
