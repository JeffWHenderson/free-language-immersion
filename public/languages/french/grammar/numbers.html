Numbers 0–100

French numbers follow regular patterns for 0–69, then take a unique additive/multiplicative approach for 70–99. The key irregularities are in the 70s, 80s, and 90s.

Gender: 1 has two forms — un (masculine) and une (feminine). Use un livre (a book, masc.) but une table (a table, fem.). Teens 17–19 are compound forms: dix-sept = ten-seven, dix-huit = ten-eight, dix-neuf = ten-nine.

Compound numbers 21–69 are formed as tens + et + un (for x1) or tens + hyphen + digit (for x2–x9): vingt et un, vingt-deux, trente et un, trente-cinq.

Key rules for 80s and 90s:
  • quatre-vingts (80) has an s when standing alone, but loses it in compounds: quatre-vingt-un, quatre-vingt-dix.
  • No et is used in the 80s or 90s — only in 21, 31, 41, 51, 61, and 71.

"Et" Rule Summary

Use et
  • vingt et un (21)
  • trente et un (31)
  • quarante et un (41)
  • cinquante et un (51)
  • soixante et un (61)
  • soixante et onze (71)
No et — use hyphen
  • vingt-deux (22), vingt-trois (23)…
  • quatre-vingt-un (81)
  • quatre-vingt-onze (91)

Example Sentences

  • J'ai vingt-cinq ans. I am twenty-five years old.
  • Mon numéro de téléphone est le zéro-six-soixante-dix-sept-quatre-vingt-douze. My phone number is 06-77-92.
  • Ça coûte quatre-vingt-dix-neuf euros. That costs ninety-nine euros.
  • Il y a trente et un jours en janvier. There are thirty-one days in January.
