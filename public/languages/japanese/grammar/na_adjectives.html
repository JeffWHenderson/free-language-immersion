な Adjectives

な-adjectives (ナ形容詞), also called adjectival nouns, are the second major class of Japanese adjectives. Unlike い-adjectives, they do not end in い in their dictionary form, and they require な (na) when placed before a noun. As predicates, they use です just like nouns do — their conjugation mirrors the noun + です pattern.

Examples in Sentences

  • かのじょはとてもきれいです。 She is very beautiful. (Kanojo wa totemo kirei desu.)
  • しずかなばしょでよみたいです。 I want to read in a quiet place. (Shizuka na basho de yomitai desu.)
  • かれはにほんごがじょうずです。 He is good at Japanese. (Kare wa nihongo ga jōzu desu.)
  • このまちはにぎやかでべんりです。 This town is lively and convenient. (Kono machi wa nigiyaka de benri desu.) — で connects な-adjectives.
  • しずかにはなしてください。 Please speak quietly. (Shizuka ni hanashite kudasai.) — に makes an adverb.

な-Adjectives vs. い-Adjectives — Quick Test

If you remove です from the predicate form and the word still ends in い, it might be an い-adjective. If it doesn't end in い (or ends in a particle like な in attributive use), it's a な-adjective. Exception: きれい, きらい, and ゆうめい end in い but are な-adjectives — they must be memorized.

Common Mistakes

  • きれい is not an い-adjective. Even though it ends in い, its negative is きれいではない, not きれくない. Conjugate it like a noun.
  • Don't omit な before nouns. Say しずかなへや (a quiet room), not しずかへや.
  • Adverb form uses に, not く. い-adjectives use く for adverbs; な-adjectives use に. しずかに (quietly), not しずかく.
