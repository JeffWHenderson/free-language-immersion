Plain Form — Casual Speech

Japanese has two main registers: polite speech (ます/です forms) and plain speech (dictionary/casual forms). Knowing when to use each — and how to switch between them — is one of the most important skills in Japanese. Plain form is used with friends and family, but it also appears inside sentences in both registers as an embedded clause.

Polite vs. plain — when to use each

Polite form (〜ます / 〜です)Plain form (dictionary / casual)
With strangersYes — default in publicToo casual; sounds rude
At workYes — professional standardOnly with very close colleagues
With close friendsSounds stiff or distantNatural and warm
With familySounds formalDefault register
Inside embedded clauses (〜と思う etc.)Sounds unnatural in inner clausesRequired regardless of outer register

Plain form in everyday casual speech

  • 今日は疲れた。 Kyō wa tsukareta. — I'm tired today. (casual)
  • ちょっと待って。 Chotto matte. — Wait a moment. (casual)
  • 明日、どこ行く？ Ashita, doko iku? — Where are you going tomorrow? (casual)
  • この映画、面白くなかった。 Kono eiga, omoshiroku nakatta. — This movie wasn't interesting. (casual)

Plain form embedded in larger structures

Even in polite sentences, the plain form is required inside embedded clauses. The most common patterns are 〜と思う (I think that…) and 〜と言った (said that…). Notice how the inner clause stays in plain form even when the outer sentence is polite.

StructureMeaningExample
plain form ＋ と思いますI think that…明日は雨だと思います。
plain form ＋ と言いましたsaid that…彼は行かないと言いました。
plain form ＋ と聞きましたheard that…店が閉まったと聞きました。
plain form ＋ と知っていますknow that…日本語が難しいと知っています。
  • 明日は雨だと思います。 Ashita wa ame da to omoimasu. — I think it will rain tomorrow.
  • 彼は仕事を辞めると言いました。 Kare wa shigoto wo yameru to iimashita. — He said he would quit his job.
  • そのお店がおいしいと聞きました。 Sono omise ga oishii to kikimashita. — I heard that restaurant is delicious.

Same idea — polite vs. casual register

  • 今日は疲れています。（丁寧） Kyō wa tsukarete imasu. (polite) — I'm tired today.
  • 今日は疲れてる。（ふつう） Kyō wa tsukareteru. (casual) — I'm tired today.
  • コーヒーを飲みますか？（丁寧） Kōhī wo nomimasu ka? (polite) — Would you like coffee?
  • コーヒー飲む？（ふつう） Kōhī nomu? (casual) — Want coffee?
  • どこに行きますか？（丁寧） Doko ni ikimasu ka? (polite) — Where are you going?
  • どこ行くの？（ふつう） Doko iku no? (casual) — Where are you going?

Usage notes

  • Casual speech also drops particles more freely: コーヒー飲む？ instead of コーヒーを飲む？
  • The の at the end of a casual question (どこ行くの？) softens it and seeks explanation — it is the casual equivalent of のですか.
  • Using plain form with people you just met or in formal contexts is considered rude. When in doubt, use polite forms.
  • Inside reported speech (〜と言った, 〜と思う), always use plain form regardless of the politeness of the rest of the sentence.
  • Female speakers in casual speech often replace plain だ with no copula or just rising intonation: 元気？ instead of 元気だ？
