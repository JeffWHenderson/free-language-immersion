〜すぎる — Too Much / Too...

〜すぎる (sugiru) means "too much" or "excessively." It attaches to adjectives and verb stems and is one of the most natural ways to express that something is over the top — too sad, too tired, too much work. The polite form is すぎます.

Formation

Word typeRuleExampleMeaning
い-adjectiveDrop い → + すぎる悲しすぎるtoo sad
な-adjectiveDrop な → + すぎる静かすぎるtoo quiet
Verb (ます stem)Drop ます → + すぎる食べすぎるeat too much

Emotion & Mood Examples

  • 悲しすぎます。 kanashi sugimasu — It's too sad. / I'm too sad.
  • 嬉しすぎて泣いてしまいました。 ureshisugite naite shimaimashita — I was so happy I ended up crying.
  • 心配しすぎないでください。 shinpai shi suginaide kudasai — Please don't worry too much.
  • 緊張しすぎています。 kinchō shi sugite imasu — I'm way too nervous.
  • 考えすぎですよ。 kangae sugi desu yo — You're overthinking it.

Everyday Examples

  • 疲れすぎています。 tsukare sugite imasu — I'm too tired.
  • 食べすぎました。 tabe sugimashita — I ate too much.
  • 高すぎます。 taka sugimasu — It's too expensive.
  • 難しすぎます。 muzukashi sugimasu — It's too difficult.
  • 静かすぎて不安です。 shizuka sugite fuan desu — It's so quiet it's unsettling.

〜すぎて — Connecting to a Result

Use 〜すぎて to connect the "too much" clause to its consequence.

  • 疲れすぎて何もできません。 tsukare sugite nani mo dekimasen — I'm too tired to do anything.
  • 忙しすぎて休めません。 isogashi sugite yasumemasen — I'm too busy to rest.
  • 嬉しすぎて言葉が出ません。 ureshisugite kotoba ga demasen — I'm so happy I can't find words.

Negation

To say something is "not too" much, use すぎない or soften with あまり〜すぎない.

  • あまり心配しすぎないでください。 amari shinpai shi suginaide kudasai — Please don't worry too much.
  • 考えすぎないようにします。 kangae suginai yō ni shimasu — I'll try not to overthink it.

Usage Notes

  • すぎる is a Group 2 (ichidan) verb itself, so it conjugates fully: すぎます, すぎた, すぎて, すぎない.
  • It can express positive excess too: 嬉しすぎる (I'm overwhelmingly happy), but context usually makes the tone clear.
  • Very commonly heard in casual conversation: 食べすぎた！疲れすぎ！
