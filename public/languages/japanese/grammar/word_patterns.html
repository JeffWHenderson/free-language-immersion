Word Patterns — Spotting How Japanese Works

Japanese builds meaning by attaching small suffixes to words. Once you recognise these recurring patterns you can decode — and produce — hundreds of new words without memorising each one individually. This lesson covers five of the most useful building blocks.

Pattern 1 — 〜さ: Turning い-Adjectives into Nouns

Drop the final い from an い-adjective and add さ to make a noun meaning "the degree / amount of" that quality. The result works exactly like any other noun.

AdjectiveRomajiNounRomajiMeaning
大きいookii大きさookisasize / bigness
長いnagai長さnagasalength
高いtakai高さtakasaheight / level
美しいutsukushii美しさutsukushisabeauty
難しいmuzukashii難しさmuzukashisadifficulty
  • この川の長さはどれくらいですか？ How long is this river? (Kono kawa no nagasa wa dore kurai desu ka?)
  • 彼女の美しさに驚きました。 I was amazed by her beauty. (Kanojo no utsukushisa ni odorokimashita.)
  • この問題の難しさが分かりますか？ Do you understand the difficulty of this problem? (Kono mondai no muzukashisa ga wakarimasu ka?)
Note: 〜さ produces a noun, so it can take particles like の, が, and を. Use it when you want to talk about the degree of a quality as a thing in itself — e.g. measuring, comparing, or marvelling at it.

Pattern 2 — 〜すぎる: Too Much

Attach すぎる (sugiru) to express that something is excessive — "too ___." For verbs, attach to the ます-stem (drop ます). For い-adjectives, drop the final い. For な-adjectives, use the plain stem.

Base word+ すぎるRomajiMeaning
食べる (taberu)食べすぎるtabesugirueat too much
飲む (nomu)飲みすぎるnomisugirudrink too much
大きい大きすぎるookisugirutoo big
高い高すぎるtakasugirutoo expensive / too high
難しい難しすぎるmuzukashisugirutoo difficult
  • 昨日、食べすぎました。 I ate too much yesterday. (Kinō, tabesugimashita.)
  • このバッグは高すぎます。 This bag is too expensive. (Kono baggu wa takasugimasu.)
  • その映画は難しすぎて、分かりませんでした。 That film was too difficult; I didn't understand it. (Sono eiga wa muzukashisugite, wakarimasen deshita.)
Note: すぎる conjugates as a regular る-verb: すぎます (polite), すぎて (te-form), すぎた (plain past). The て-form is especially common: 食べすぎて気持ち悪い (I ate too much and feel sick).

Pattern 3 — 〜やすい / 〜にくい: Easy / Hard to Do

Attach やすい (yasui) or にくい (nikui) to the ます-stem of a verb (the part before ます) to describe how easy or difficult something is to do. The result is an い-adjective and conjugates like one.

〜やすい — easy to do
  • 食べやすい easy to eat (tabeyasui)
  • 読みやすい easy to read (yomiyasui)
  • 使いやすい easy to use (tsukaiyasui)
〜にくい — hard to do
  • 読みにくい hard to read (yominikui)
  • 分かりにくい hard to understand (wakarinikui)
  • この箸は使いやすいです。 These chopsticks are easy to use. (Kono hashi wa tsukaiyasui desu.)
  • この地図は分かりにくいですね。 This map is hard to understand, isn't it. (Kono chizu wa wakarinikui desu ne.)
  • 彼の字は読みにくいです。 His handwriting is hard to read. (Kare no ji wa yominikui desu.)
Note: Because やすい / にくい are い-adjectives, they conjugate: 読みやすかった (was easy to read), 読みにくくない (not hard to read).

Pattern 4 — Verb Stem + 始める / 続ける / 終わる: Starting, Continuing, Finishing

Attach one of these three verbs to the ます-stem to describe the phase of an action.

SuffixRomajiMeaningExampleRomajiEnglish
〜始める〜hajimerustart doing食べ始めるtabe-hajimerustart eating
〜続ける〜tsuzukerukeep doing / continue話し続けるhanashi-tsuzukerukeep talking
〜終わる〜owarufinish doing書き終わるkaki-owarufinish writing
  • 雨が降り始めました。 It started to rain. (Ame ga ori-hajimemashita.)
  • 彼は二時間話し続けました。 He kept talking for two hours. (Kare wa ni-jikan hanashi-tsuzukemashita.)
  • レポートを書き終わりましたか？ Have you finished writing the report? (Repōto wo kaki-owarimashita ka?)
  • 子供たちが走り始めた。 The children started running. (Kodomotachi ga hashiri-hajimeta.)
Note: 始める and 続ける are る-verbs; 終わる is a う-verb. All three conjugate normally after the stem. You can combine them: 食べ始めてから食べ続けた (started eating and kept eating).

Pattern 5 — 〜そう: Looks Like / Seems Like

Add そう (sou) to express that something appears or seems a certain way, based on what you can see right now. The formation rule depends on word type.

Formation rules:
  • い-adjective: drop い → + そう　　例: おいしい → おいしそう
  • な-adjective: drop な → + そう　　例: 元気な → 元気そう
  • Verb: ます-stem → + そう　　例: 降り（ます）→ 降りそう
Base〜そう formRomajiMeaning
おいしい (oishii)おいしそうoishisoulooks delicious
難しい (muzukashii)難しそうmuzukashisouseems difficult
疲れる (tsukareru)疲れそうtsukare-soulooks tired / about to get tired
雨が降る (ame ga furu)雨が降りそうame ga furi-soulooks like rain
  • このケーキ、おいしそうですね！ This cake looks delicious! (Kono kēki, oishisou desu ne!)
  • 空を見て — 雨が降りそうです。 Look at the sky — it looks like rain. (Sora wo mite — ame ga furisou desu.)
  • 田中さんは疲れそうな顔をしています。 Tanaka-san looks like she has a tired face. (Tanaka-san wa tsukare-sou na kao wo shite imasu.)
  • この試験は難しそうです。 This exam seems difficult. (Kono shiken wa muzukashisou desu.)
Note: そう acts like a な-adjective: おいしそうな料理 (food that looks delicious), 難しそうに話す (speak in a way that seems difficult). Do not use this そう to report what someone told you — that is a different そう (plain form + そうです).
