い Adjectives

Japanese adjectives fall into two classes. い-adjectives (イ形容詞) are adjectives whose dictionary form ends in the hiragana い. They conjugate directly — no helper verb needed — and can describe nouns or act as predicates on their own.

Using い-Adjectives

い-adjectives can appear in two positions: before a noun (attributive) or at the end of a sentence as a predicate. Both positions use the plain い form.

PositionPatternExampleMeaning
Attributive (before noun)[い-adj] + nounおおきいいぬa big dog
Predicate (polite)[Topic] は [い-adj] ですこのいぬはおおきいです。This dog is big.
Predicate (plain)[Topic] は [い-adj]このいぬはおおきい。This dog is big. (casual)

Examples in Sentences

  • きょうはいそがしいです。 Today is busy. (Kyō wa isogashii desu.)
  • このえいがはおもしろくないです。 This movie is not interesting. (Kono eiga wa omoshiroku nai desu.)
  • きのうはたのしかったです。 Yesterday was fun. (Kinō wa tanoshikatta desu.)
  • やすくておいしいレストランがすきです。 I like cheap and delicious restaurants. (Yasukute oishii resutoran ga suki desu.) — て-form connects adjectives.
  • かのじょはやさしくなりました。 She became kind. (Kanojo wa yasashiku narimashita.) — く + なる = become.

Common Mistakes

  • いい (good) is irregular. The negative/past forms come from よい: よくない (not good), よかった (was good), よくなかった (was not good). Never say いくない or いかった.
  • Don't add な before a noun. い-adjectives modify nouns directly: おいしいたべもの (delicious food), not おいしいなたべもの.
  • Don't drop い from the dictionary form as a predicate. Say おおきいです, not おおきです.
