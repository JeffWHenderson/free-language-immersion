Time Words & Days

Japanese time expressions are placed before the verb, typically near the beginning of a sentence. Learning the core time words and days of the week unlocks a wide range of everyday conversations about schedules, plans, and routines.

Word-Order Rule: Time → Place → Verb

In Japanese, time expressions come before the verb and typically before place expressions. This T-P-V order is the standard pattern.

  • 今日は学校に行きます。 Today I go to school. (Kyō wa gakkō ni ikimasu.)
  • 明日、図書館で勉強します。 Tomorrow I'll study at the library. (Ashita, toshokan de benkyō shimasu.)
Tip: Time words like 今日 and 明日 do not require a particle (は, に, etc.) and can be dropped at the front of the sentence. Specific times and days of the week typically take に: 月曜日に会いましょう (Let's meet on Monday).

Note: あまり and 全然 require a negative verb form at the end: あまり食べません (don't eat much), 全然わかりません (don't understand at all).

Examples

  • 今日は何曜日ですか？ What day is today? (Kyō wa nanyōbi desu ka?)
  • 昨日、映画を見ました。 Yesterday I watched a movie. (Kinō, eiga wo mimashita.)
  • 毎日日本語を勉強します。 I study Japanese every day. (Mainichi nihongo wo benkyō shimasu.)
  • 時々コーヒーを飲みます。 I sometimes drink coffee. (Tokidoki kōhī wo nomimasu.)
