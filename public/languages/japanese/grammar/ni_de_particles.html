に vs で — Location Particles

Both に (ni) and で (de) can translate as "at," "in," or "on" in English, which makes them a classic source of confusion. The key distinction is simple: に marks the location where something exists or the destination you are going to, while で marks the location where an action takes place.

Core Rule

ParticleUseKey VerbsExample
に (ni)Location of existenceいる, ある, すむえきにいます。(I am at the station.)
に (ni)Destination of movementいく, くる, かえるとうきょうにいきます。(I go to Tokyo.)
で (de)Location of actionたべる, よむ, はたらくカフェでよみます。(I read at the café.)
で (de)Means / methodany action verbバスできます。(I come by bus.)

に — Existence & Destination

Think of に as pointing to a specific point in space where something is or arrives. No activity needed — just presence.

  • ねこはソファのうえにいます。 The cat is on the sofa. (Neko wa sofa no ue ni imasu.)
  • かぎはテーブルのうえにあります。 The key is on the table. (Kagi wa tēburu no ue ni arimasu.)
  • びょういんにいきます。 I am going to the hospital. (Byōin ni ikimasu.)
  • ちかくにすんでいます。 I live nearby. (Chikaku ni sunde imasu.)

で — Action Location & Means

Think of で as the stage on which an activity happens, or the tool/means used to do something.

  • としょかんでべんきょうします。 I study at the library. (Toshokan de benkyō shimasu.)
  • レストランでごはんをたべます。 I eat at the restaurant. (Resutoran de gohan wo tabemasu.)
  • にほんごではなします。 I speak in Japanese. (Nihongo de hanashimasu.) — で marks means.
  • でんしゃでいきます。 I go by train. (Densha de ikimasu.) — で marks transport.

Side-by-Side Comparison

に sentenceで sentence
こうえんにいます。(I am in the park.)こうえんであそびます。(I play in the park.)
うちにかえります。(I return home.)うちではたらきます。(I work at home.)
カフェにいます。(I am at the café.)カフェでコーヒーをのみます。(I drink coffee at the café.)

Common Mistakes

  • いる and ある always use に. These existence verbs pinpoint a location, never an action scene. Never use で with them.
  • Movement verbs use に for destination. Say としょかんにいく (go to the library), not としょかんでいく.
  • で also marks means. バスできた (came by bus), はさみできる (cut with scissors) — when a noun is the tool or method, use で.
