〜ます Verb Form

The 〜ます (masu) form is the polite, non-past form of Japanese verbs. It is the first verb form learners use and remains appropriate in most everyday situations: speaking with teachers, strangers, coworkers, shopkeepers, and in formal contexts. Understanding how to build the ます form and its conjugations gives you a complete toolkit for polite conversation.

Three Verb Groups

Japanese verbs are divided into three groups based on how they conjugate.

GroupAlso CalledDictionary EndingMasu Form RuleExample
Group 1 (五段)U-verbs / Godan-u (く, ぐ, す, つ, ぬ, ぶ, む, る*)Change last vowel to い-sound + ますかく → かきます
Group 2 (一段)Ru-verbs / Ichidan-iru or -eruDrop る, add ますたべる → たべます
Group 3Irregularする, くるMemorizeする → します, くる → きます

Examples in Sentences

  • まいにちにほんごをべんきょうします。 I study Japanese every day. (Mainichi nihongo wo benkyō shimasu.)
  • あしたともだちにあいます。 Tomorrow I will meet a friend. (Ashita tomodachi ni aimasu.)
  • きのうはすしをたべませんでした。 Yesterday I didn't eat sushi. (Kinō wa sushi wo tabemasen deshita.)
  • いっしょにいきましょう！ Let's go together! (Issho ni ikimashō!)
  • コーヒーをのみますか？ Do you drink coffee? (Kōhī wo nomimasu ka?)

Common Mistakes

  • ます is non-past, not just present. たべます can mean "I eat" (habitual) or "I will eat" (future). Japanese does not have a separate future tense.
  • Some -る verbs are Group 1. かえる (to return) → かえります, not かえます. These must be memorized.
  • Don't add ます to adjectives. Adjectives conjugate differently. たかいです is correct; たかいます is not a word.
