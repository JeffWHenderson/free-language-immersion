〜たい — Want To Do

To express "I want to do something," use the masu-stem of a verb plus たい. This conjugates like an い-adjective and is used only for the speaker's own desires. For third-person wants, use 〜たがっています.

Formation: masu-stem + たい

Dictionary formMasu-stemたい formEnglish
食べる食べ食べたいwant to eat
飲む飲み飲みたいwant to drink
行く行き行きたいwant to go
作る作り作りたいwant to make
見る見見たいwant to see

Polite form, negative, and past

FormJapaneseRomajiEnglish
Polite食べたいですtabetai desuI want to eat
Negative食べたくない(です)tabetakunai (desu)don't want to eat
Past食べたかった(です)tabetakatta (desu)wanted to eat

Object particle: が or を

With たい, the object can take either が or を. Both are acceptable; が is slightly more natural when expressing a craving.

  • ラーメンが食べたい。 Rāmen ga tabetai. — I want to eat ramen. (が, craving feel)
  • ラーメンを食べたい。 Rāmen wo tabetai. — I want to eat ramen. (を, also fine)

Third person: 〜たがっています

  • 彼はすしが食べたがっています。 Kare wa sushi ga tabetagai imasu. — He wants to eat sushi.

Food examples

  • 今日はコーヒーが飲みたいです。 Kyō wa kōhī ga nomitai desu. — I want to drink coffee today.
  • 新しいレストランに行きたいです。 Atarashii resutoran ni ikitai desu. — I want to go to a new restaurant.
  • 甘いものが食べたくないです。 Amai mono ga tabetakunai desu. — I don't want to eat sweets.
