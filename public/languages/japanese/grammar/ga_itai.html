〜が痛い — Expressing Pain

In Japanese, pain is not described the way it is in English. Instead of "I have a headache" or "My leg hurts," Japanese uses a subject-predicate structure where the body part becomes the subject and 痛い (itai) — an い-adjective meaning "painful" or "hurts" — is the predicate. The body part takes the subject particle が.

Core Pattern

PatternJapaneseRomajiMeaning
[Body part] が 痛い(です)あたまがいたいです。Atama ga itai desu.My head hurts. / I have a headache.
[Body part] が 痛い(です)はがいたいです。Ha ga itai desu.My tooth hurts. / I have a toothache.
[Body part] が 痛い(です)おなかがいたいです。Onaka ga itai desu.My stomach hurts.

Degrees of Pain

Add an adverb before 痛い to express intensity.

  • すこしいたいです。 It hurts a little. (Sukoshi itai desu.)
  • とてもいたいです。 It hurts very much. (Totemo itai desu.)
  • すごくいたいです。 It hurts a lot / really hurts. (Sugoku itai desu.)

At the Doctor — Useful Phrases

  • どこがいたいですか？ Where does it hurt? (Doko ga itai desu ka?)
  • いつからいたいですか？ Since when has it hurt? (Itsu kara itai desu ka?)
  • ここをおすといたいです。 It hurts when you press here. (Koko wo osu to itai desu.)
  • かぜをひきました。 I caught a cold. (Kaze wo hikimashita.)
  • ねつがあります。 I have a fever. (Netsu ga arimasu.) — fever uses ある, not いたい.
  • きぶんがわるいです。 I feel sick / unwell. (Kibun ga warui desu.)

Common Mistakes

  • The body part is the subject (が), not the object. Don't use を. Say あしがいたい, not あしをいたい.
  • Don't translate word-for-word from English. "I have a headache" is not わたしはあたまをもっています. It is simply あたまがいたいです.
  • 痛い conjugates like any い-adjective. Past: いたかったです (it hurt). Negative: いたくないです (it doesn't hurt).
