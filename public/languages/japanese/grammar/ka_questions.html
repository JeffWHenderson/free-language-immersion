Questions with か

Forming a yes/no question in Japanese is remarkably simple: add the question particle か (ka) to the end of a statement. There is no need to rearrange word order the way English does. Rising intonation is optional in formal speech — the か does the work.

Basic Pattern

StatementQuestionMeaning
これはほんです。(This is a book.)これはほんですか？Is this a book?
かれはがくせいです。(He is a student.)かれはがくせいですか？Is he a student?
たべものはおいしいです。(The food is delicious.)たべものはおいしいですか？Is the food delicious?

Question Words + か

Combine か with a question word (疑問詞, gimonshi) to form information questions. Unlike English, word order stays the same — you just slot in the question word and add か at the end.

Answering Yes/No Questions

はい (hai) = yes. いいえ (iie) = no. In natural conversation, Japanese speakers often echo part of the question in their answer rather than just saying yes or no alone.

  • A: にほんごをはなしますか？ A: Do you speak Japanese? (Nihongo wo hanashimasu ka?)
  • B: はい、はなします。 B: Yes, I do. (Hai, hanashimasu.)
  • B: いいえ、はなしません。 B: No, I don't. (Iie, hanashimasen.)

More Examples

  • おてあらいはどこですか？ Where is the restroom? (Otearai wa doko desu ka?)
  • なまえはなんですか？ What is your name? (Namae wa nan desu ka?)
  • きょうはなんようびですか？ What day of the week is it today? (Kyō wa nan'yōbi desu ka?)
  • これはいくらですか？ How much is this? (Kore wa ikura desu ka?)
  • でんしゃはなんじにでますか？ What time does the train leave? (Densha wa nanji ni demasu ka?)

Casual Speech

In casual conversation, the sentence-final か can sound blunt or overly formal. Instead, speakers often use rising intonation alone, or add the softer の (no) or かな (kana).

  • たべる？ Are you going to eat? (Taberu?) — casual rising intonation
  • どこにいくの？ Where are you going? (Doko ni iku no?) — casual

Common Mistakes

  • Don't add a question mark and omit か in writing. In formal Japanese writing, か signals the question. A question mark (？) is added in casual contexts but does not replace か.
  • Word order stays the same. Unlike English ("Are you a student?" inverts subject and verb), Japanese simply appends か: がくせいですか？
  • なに vs. なん. Use なん before です, な-adjectives, and counters (なんにん — how many people). Use なに before particles like を and が.
