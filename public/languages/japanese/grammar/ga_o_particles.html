が & を — Subject & Object Particles

Japanese uses small particles after nouns to show their grammatical role in the sentence. Two of the most essential are が (ga), which marks the grammatical subject, and を (wo/o), which marks the direct object of an action verb. Mastering these two particles unlocks the ability to construct real sentences.

が — Subject Particle

が identifies who or what performs the action or is described by the predicate. It is also used to introduce new or emphasized information, and with stative verbs expressing want, ability, and emotion.

PatternJapaneseRomajiMeaning
[Subject] が [Verb]ねこがねている。Neko ga nete iru.The cat is sleeping.
[Subject] が [Adjective] ですそらがあおいです。Sora ga aoi desu.The sky is blue.
[X] が すき / きらい / ほしいコーヒーがすきです。Kōhī ga suki desu.I like coffee.
[X] が できますにほんごができます。Nihongo ga dekimasu.I can speak Japanese.

を — Object Particle

を marks the direct object — the thing that receives the action of a transitive verb. It is written を but almost always pronounced o.

PatternJapaneseRomajiMeaning
[Object] を [Verb]パンをたべます。Pan wo tabemasu.I eat bread.
[Object] を [Verb]みずをのみます。Mizu wo nomimasu.I drink water.
[Object] を [Verb]えいごをべんきょうします。Eigo wo benkyō shimasu.I study English.

Examples

  • わたしがりんごをたべます。 I eat an apple. (Watashi ga ringo wo tabemasu.) — が marks "I" as subject; を marks "apple" as object.
  • かのじょがほんをよみます。 She reads a book. (Kanojo ga hon wo yomimasu.)
  • いぬがボールをおいかけます。 The dog chases the ball. (Inu ga bōru wo oikakemasu.)
  • なにがほしいですか？ What do you want? (Nani ga hoshii desu ka?)
  • なにをたべますか？ What will you eat? (Nani wo tabemasu ka?)
  • おんがくがすきです。 I like music. (Ongaku ga suki desu.) — emotion verb takes が.

が vs. は — Key Distinction

は marks the topic (often already known); が marks the subject (often new information or emphasis). Compare:

  • たなかさんはがくせいです。 As for Tanaka, he is a student. (は = topic, already known)
  • だれがたなかさんですか？ — たなかさんがわたしです。 Who is Tanaka? — I am Tanaka. (が = new identification)

Common Mistakes

  • Don't use を with intransitive verbs. 走る (hashiru, to run) is intransitive — say こうえんをはしる (run through the park, を marks the space traversed), not こうえんがはしる.
  • Emotion and ability verbs take が, not を. すき (like), きらい (dislike), ほしい (want), できる (can) all use が for their object: コーヒーがすき, not コーヒーをすき.
  • を is unambiguous. Unlike は, を always and only marks the direct object. If you see を, you know the noun before it is the thing being acted on.
