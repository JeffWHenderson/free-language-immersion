〜に行く / 来る / 帰る — Going To Do Something

To express going, coming, or returning somewhere in order to do something, attach に to the masu-stem of the purpose verb, then add the movement verb. The に here marks purpose, not destination.

Formation: masu-stem + に + 行く / 来る / 帰る

JapaneseRomajiEnglish
食べに行くtabe ni ikugo to eat
見に来るmi ni kurucome to see
買い物に行くkaimono ni ikugo shopping
飲みに行くnomi ni ikugo (out) for drinks
休みに帰るyasumi ni kaerureturn home to rest

に for purpose vs. に for destination

The same particle に is used for destinations (駅に行く = go to the station). The key difference: when に follows a masu-stem verb, it expresses purpose.

  • 駅に行く。 Eki ni iku. — Go to the station. (destination)
  • 電車を見に行く。 Densha wo mi ni iku. — Go to see the train. (purpose)

Examples with places

  • レストランに食べに行きます。 Resutoran ni tabe ni ikimasu. — I'll go to the restaurant to eat.
  • 図書館に本を返しに行きました。 Toshokan ni hon wo kaeshi ni ikimashita. — I went to the library to return a book.
  • 映画館に映画を見に来ました。 Eigakan ni eiga wo mi ni kimashita. — I came to the cinema to watch a movie.
  • スーパーに買い物に行きませんか？ Sūpā ni kaimono ni ikimasen ka? — Shall we go shopping at the supermarket?
  • 公園に散歩しに行きましょう。 Kōen ni sanpo shi ni ikimashō. — Let's go to the park for a walk.
