〜から / 〜ので — Because / Reason

When you want to explain why something is true or give a reason for an action, Japanese uses 〜から or 〜ので. Both translate roughly as "because" or "so", but they differ in tone: 〜から is direct and assertive, while 〜ので is softer and more considerate. Choosing the right one shapes how your statement comes across to the listener.

At a glance

FormStructureTone
〜からPlain or polite form + からDirect, assertive; the speaker states their reason confidently
〜のでPlain form + ので (な-adj / noun + なので)Soft, considerate; implies the reason is a natural, objective fact

〜から — giving a direct reason

Attach から directly after a verb, adjective, or noun in plain or polite form. It is the most common way to give a reason in conversation and can feel emphatic or even slightly pushy if overdone in formal settings.

  • 疲れたから、早く寝ます。 Tsukareta kara, hayaku nemasu. — I'm tired, so I'll go to bed early.
  • 雨が降っているから、傘を持っていこう。 Ame ga futte iru kara, kasa wo motte ikō. — It's raining, so let's take an umbrella.
  • お腹が空いたから、何か食べたい。 Onaka ga suita kara, nanika tabetai. — I'm hungry, so I want to eat something.
  • 忙しいから、後で電話します。 Isogashii kara, ato de denwa shimasu. — I'm busy, so I'll call later.
  • 日本語が好きだから、毎日練習しています。 Nihongo ga suki da kara, mainichi renshū shite imasu. — Because I like Japanese, I practice every day.

〜ので — giving a polite, considerate reason

Attach ので after the plain form. For な-adjectives and nouns, add な before ので (e.g. 静かなので, 学生なので). 〜ので feels more indirect and considerate — it presents the reason as an objective circumstance rather than a personal assertion, making it ideal for formal speech and polite requests.

  • 体の調子が悪いので、休ませてください。 Karada no chōshi ga warui node, yasumasete kudasai. — Since I'm not feeling well, please let me rest.
  • 会議があるので、少し遅れます。 Kaigi ga aru node, sukoshi okuremasu. — I have a meeting, so I'll be a little late.
  • 静かなので、集中しやすいです。 Shizuka na node, shūchū shi yasui desu. — Because it's quiet, it's easy to concentrate.
  • 初めてなので、教えてもらえますか？ Hajimete na node, oshiete moraemasu ka? — Since it's my first time, could you teach me?

Common everyday patterns

  • 遅くなるから、先に食べてて。 Osoku naru kara, saki ni tabete te. — I'll be late, so go ahead and eat. (casual)
  • 遅くなるので、先に食べていてください。 Osoku naru node, saki ni tabete ite kudasai. — I'll be late, so please go ahead and eat. (polite)
  • 電車が遅れたから、間に合わなかった。 Densha ga okureta kara, ma ni awanakatta. — The train was late, so I didn't make it. (casual explanation)
  • 電車が遅れたので、遅刻してしまいました。 Densha ga okureta node, chikoku shite shimaimashita. — Because the train was delayed, I ended up being late. (formal apology)

Usage notes

  • 〜から can follow polite ます/です forms as well as plain forms. 〜ので almost always follows the plain form (even in otherwise polite sentences).
  • Use 〜ので when making requests of superiors, clients, or strangers — it softens the imposition.
  • 〜から at the very end of a sentence (without a result clause) means "because, that's why" as a trailing explanation: 好きだから。(Because I like it.)
  • 〜ので cannot stand alone at the end of a sentence the way 〜から can.
  • In very formal written Japanese, 〜ため(に) (tame ni) is an even more formal alternative to 〜ので.
