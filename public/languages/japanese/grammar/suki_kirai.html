好き & 嫌い — Like & Dislike

好き (suki) and 嫌い (kirai) are among the most commonly used expressions in Japanese. Unlike English, they are na-adjectives, not verbs. This means you say "X is liked" rather than "I like X." The thing you like or dislike takes the subject particle が.

Core Pattern

MeaningPatternExampleRomaji
I like XX が 好きですコーヒーがすきです。Kōhī ga suki desu.
I dislike XX が 嫌いですたまねぎがきらいです。Tamanegi ga kirai desu.
I love XX が 大好きですすしがだいすきです。Sushi ga daisuki desu.
I hate XX が 大嫌いですからいものがだいきらいです。Karai mono ga daikirai desu.
I don't like XX が 好きじゃない/好きではないスポーツがすきじゃないです。Supōtsu ga suki ja nai desu.

Liking an Action (Verb + のが好き)

To say you like doing something, nominalize the verb with の (no) or こと (koto), then use が好き.

  • およぐのがすきです。 I like swimming. (Oyogu no ga suki desu.)
  • りょうりをするのがすきです。 I like cooking. (Ryōri wo suru no ga suki desu.)
  • まつのがきらいです。 I dislike waiting. (Matsu no ga kirai desu.)

More Examples

  • にほんごがだいすきです。 I love Japanese. (Nihongo ga daisuki desu.)
  • かのじょはやさいがきらいです。 She dislikes vegetables. (Kanojo wa yasai ga kirai desu.)
  • なにがすきですか？ What do you like? (Nani ga suki desu ka?)
  • すきなたべものはなんですか？ What is your favorite food? (Suki na tabemono wa nan desu ka?)
  • かれはそとであそぶのがすきです。 He likes playing outside. (Kare wa soto de asobu no ga suki desu.)

Common Mistakes

  • 好き and 嫌い take が, not を. The object of liking/disliking is grammatically the subject of the adjective. Say コーヒーがすきです, not コーヒーをすきです.
  • 好き is an adjective, not a verb. You cannot conjugate it the way you conjugate a verb. Use です to be polite: すきです (not すきます).
  • 大好き is stronger than すき. Saying だいすき carries real emotional weight — it can mean "love." Reserve it accordingly.
  • Topic vs. subject. The person who likes/dislikes is often the topic (は), and the thing liked/disliked is the subject (が): わたしはすしがすきです (As for me, sushi is liked).
