Navigation — Directions Grammar

Giving and following directions requires specific verbs and spatial expressions. The particles で (location of action) and に (destination/direction) are essential here.

Core direction verbs

右に曲がるmigi ni magaruturn right
左に曲がるhidari ni magaruturn left
まっすぐ行くmassugu ikugo straight
渡るwatarucross (a road/bridge)

Particles: で vs. に in directions

で marks where an action (like turning) takes place. に marks direction or destination.

  • 角で右に曲がってください。 Kado de migi ni magatte kudasai. — Please turn right at the corner.
  • 交差点で左に曲がります。 Kōsaten de hidari ni magarimasu. — Turn left at the intersection.

Direction sequences

  • まっすぐ行って、橋を渡ってください。 Massugu itte, hashi wo watatte kudasai. — Go straight, then cross the bridge.
  • コンビニを過ぎて、左に曲がります。 Konbini wo sugite, hidari ni magarimasu. — Pass the convenience store, then turn left.
  • 銀行の前で止まってください。 Ginkō no mae de tomatte kudasai. — Please stop in front of the bank.
  • 駅はこの道をまっすぐ行くとあります。 Eki wa kono michi wo massugu iku to arimasu. — If you go straight along this road, you'll find the station.
