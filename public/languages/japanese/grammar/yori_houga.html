〜より / 〜のほうが — Comparisons

To compare two things in Japanese, you use the pattern A より B のほうが — "B is more [adjective] than A". This is the opposite order from English: the thing you are comparing against (A) comes first marked by より, and the thing you prefer or rate higher (B) comes before のほうが. With practice, this structure becomes very natural.

Core comparison pattern

Japanese patternMeaning
A より B のほうが ＋ adjectiveB is more [adjective] than A
A より B のほうが好きですI like B more than A
B のほうが ＋ adjective (casual, dropping より A)B is better / more [adjective] (when context is clear)

Basic examples

  • この仕事より、あの仕事のほうが楽しい。 Kono shigoto yori, ano shigoto no hō ga tanoshii. — That job is more fun than this job.
  • 電車よりバスのほうが安いです。 Densha yori basu no hō ga yasui desu. — The bus is cheaper than the train.
  • サッカーより野球のほうが好きです。 Sakkā yori yakyū no hō ga suki desu. — I like baseball more than soccer.
  • 夏より冬のほうが好きです。 Natsu yori fuyu no hō ga suki desu. — I like winter more than summer.

Dropping のほうが in casual speech

In everyday conversation, のほうが is often dropped when context makes the comparison obvious. より alone can then act as a simple "more than" marker.

  • 昨日より今日のほうが気分がいい。→ 昨日より気分がいい。 Kinō yori kyō no hō ga kibun ga ii. → Kinō yori kibun ga ii. — I feel better today than yesterday.
  • これよりあっちがいいな。 Kore yori acchi ga ii na. — That one over there is better than this. (casual)

一番 — superlatives

To say something is "the most" or "the best", place 一番 (ichiban) before the adjective. No special conjugation is needed.

  • この趣味が一番楽しいです。 Kono shumi ga ichiban tanoshii desu. — This hobby is the most fun.
  • 彼女が一番上手です。 Kanojo ga ichiban jōzu desu. — She is the most skilled.
  • この仕事が一番やりがいがあります。 Kono shigoto ga ichiban yarigai ga arimasu. — This job is the most rewarding.

どちらのほうが — asking "which is more?"

Use どちらのほうが (dochira no hō ga) to ask which of two options is more [adjective]. Use どれが一番 (dore ga ichiban) when choosing from three or more.

  • 料理と写真、どちらのほうが好きですか？ Ryōri to shashin, dochira no hō ga suki desu ka? — Which do you like more, cooking or photography?
  • この三つの仕事の中でどれが一番大変ですか？ Kono mittsu no shigoto no naka de dore ga ichiban taihen desu ka? — Among these three jobs, which is the most demanding?

Job and hobby context examples

  • フリーランスより会社員のほうが安定しています。 Furīransu yori kaishain no hō ga antei shite imasu. — Being a company employee is more stable than freelancing.
  • 室内の仕事より屋外の仕事のほうが体を使います。 Shitsunai no shigoto yori okugai no shigoto no hō ga karada wo tsukaimasu. — Outdoor work uses the body more than indoor work.
  • 読書より映画を見るほうが好きです。 Dokusho yori eiga wo miru hō ga suki desu. — I prefer watching movies to reading.
  • 一人でやるより、チームでやるほうが楽しいです。 Hitori de yaru yori, chīmu de yaru hō ga tanoshii desu. — Doing it as a team is more fun than doing it alone.

Usage notes

  • より marks the thing being compared against (the "loser" of the comparison). のほうが marks the preferred or higher-rated item.
  • You can use より with adjectives alone for a general sense of "more than expected": 思ったより難しかった (It was harder than I thought).
  • 一番 works with both い-adjectives (一番高い — most expensive) and な-adjectives (一番便利な — most convenient).
  • どちら (dochira) is the polite form of どっち (docchi); both mean "which of the two".
