حروف الجر — Prepositions

Arabic prepositions (حروف الجر, huruf al-jarr) are small but powerful words that express relationships between nouns. When a noun follows a preposition, it takes the genitive case — in practice, this mainly affects the final vowel in formal speech. For beginners, focus on memorizing the prepositions and their usage patterns.

Attached Prepositions

The prepositions بـ (bi-) and لـ (li-) are written attached directly to the following word with no space:

  • بالعربية in Arabic (bil-arabiyya)
  • للمطعم to/for the restaurant (lil-mat'am)
  • بالسيارة by car (bis-sayyara)

Examples in Context

  • المفاتيح على الطاولة. The keys are on the table. (al-mafatih 'ala at-tawila)
  • أنا من الأردن. I am from Jordan. (ana min al-urdunn)
  • نذهب إلى المطعم. We are going to the restaurant. (nadhhab ila al-mat'am)
  • البنك بجانب المقهى. The bank is next to the cafe. (al-bank bi-janib al-maqha)
  • القطة تحت الطاولة. The cat is under the table. (al-qitta taht at-tawila)
  • جئت مع صديقي. I came with my friend. (ji'tu ma' sadiqi)
  • هذا لك. This is for you. (hatha lak)
  • أتكلم بالعربية. I speak in Arabic. (atakallam bil-arabiyya)

Common Pitfalls

  • في vs على: في (fi) = inside or at a location. على ('ala) = on top of a surface. "I'm at the office" = أنا في المكتب. "The book is on the desk" = الكتاب على المكتب.
  • من for "some of": من can mean "some of" or "from." Context determines meaning: من الخبز = some bread, من مصر = from Egypt.
  • Genitive after prepositions: In written/formal Arabic, nouns after prepositions take a kasra (ـِ) ending. In spoken Arabic this is usually dropped — don't worry about it at first.
