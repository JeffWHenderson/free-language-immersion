Question Words — أدوات الاستفهام

Arabic has a rich set of question words. They typically come at the beginning of the question. Some question words work with different verbs than their English equivalents — pay attention to how Arabic structures each question type. Questions can also be introduced with هَل (hal) or أَ (a-) to form yes/no questions.

Question Words in Sentences — Locations

  • أَيْنَ المَحَطَّة؟ ayna al-mahatta? — Where is the station?
  • مَتَى يَصِل القِطَار؟ mata yasil al-qitar? — When does the train arrive?
  • كَيْفَ أَذهَب إلى المطار؟ kayfa adhhab ila al-matar? — How do I get to the airport?
  • مَن يَعرِف الطريق؟ man ya'rif at-tariq? — Who knows the way?
  • مَاذَا يُوجَد في المَركَز؟ madha yujad fi al-markaz? — What is there in the centre?
  • مَا اسمُ هذا الشارع؟ ma ismu hadha ash-shari'? — What is the name of this street?
  • كَمِ المَسَافَة إلى الفندق؟ kami al-masafa ila al-funduq? — How far is the hotel? (lit. 'how much the distance?')
  • لِمَاذَا المَحَطَّة مُغلَقَة؟ limadha al-mahatta mughlaqah? — Why is the station closed?
  • أَيّ حافِلَة تَذهَب إلى المدينة؟ ayy hafila tadhhab ila al-madina? — Which bus goes to the city?

مَاذَا vs. مَا

  • مَاذَا تَفعَل؟ madha taf'al? — What are you doing? (verb follows — use ماذا)
  • مَا هذا؟ ma hadha? — What is this? (noun / identity — use ما)
