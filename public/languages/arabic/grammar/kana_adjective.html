كَانَ + صِفَة — Past States with كَانَ

كَانَ (kāna) is the Arabic verb "to be" in the past tense. When combined with an adjective, it describes a past state: what someone or something was like. The adjective that follows كَانَ is in the accusative case (خَبَر كَانَ منصوب) and agrees with the subject in gender and number.

Adjective Agreement with كَانَ

The predicate adjective (خَبَر) must agree with the subject in gender and takes the accusative indefinite (tanwin -an / -atan):

SubjectAdjective FormExampleMeaning
هو (m.s.)masculine acc. (-an)كَانَ سَعِيدًاHe was happy
هي (f.s.)feminine acc. (-atan)كَانَتْ سَعِيدَةًShe was happy
هم (m.pl.)masculine pl. acc. (-īna)كَانُوا سُعَدَاءَThey were happy

Negation of Past States

To negate a past state, use لَمْ يَكُنْ (lam yakun) + adjective in the accusative — or كَانَ غَيْرَ + adjective:

  • لَمْ يَكُنْ سَعِيدًا lam yakun saʿīdan — He was not happy (lam + jussive of كَانَ)
  • كَانَ غَيْرَ مُرْتَاح kāna ghayra murtāḥ — He was not comfortable (lit. 'was other-than comfortable')

Example Sentences

  • كَانَ سَعِيدًا جِدًّا. kāna saʿīdan jiddan — He was very happy.
  • كَانَتْ حَزِينَةً بَعْدَ الأَخْبَار. kānat ḥazīnatan baʿda l-akhbār — She was sad after the news.
  • كُنْتُ خَائِفًا فِي الْبِدَايَة. kuntu khāʾifan fī l-bidāya — I was afraid at first.
  • كَانُوا مُتْعَبِينَ بَعْدَ الْعَمَل. kānū mutʿabīna baʿda l-ʿamal — They were tired after work.
