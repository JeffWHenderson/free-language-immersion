المضارع المنفي — Negating Present Tense Verbs

Arabic has several ways to negate present tense verbs and nominal sentences, and the choice depends on whether you are using Modern Standard Arabic (MSA) or a colloquial dialect, and whether you are negating a verb or a noun/adjective. The two most important negation words for the present are لَا (lā) and مَا (mā), with the colloquial مِش / مُش (mish/mush) used widely in Egyptian and Levantine speech.

لَا — Standard Negation of Present Tense Verbs (MSA)

لَا directly precedes the present tense (مضارع) verb. The verb does not change its form:

AffirmativeNegative with لَاEnglish
أَشْعُرُ بِخَوْفٍ.لَا أَشْعُرُ بِخَوْفٍ.I don't feel fear.
يَفْهَمُ مَشَاعِرَهَا.لَا يَفْهَمُ مَشَاعِرَهَا.He doesn't understand her feelings.
نُحِبُّ الانْتِظَارَ.لَا نُحِبُّ الانْتِظَارَ.We don't like waiting.
تَقْلَقُ كَثِيرًا.لَا تَقْلَقُ كَثِيرًا.She doesn't worry too much.

لَا vs. مَا — Regional and Stylistic Differences

NegatorUsageExampleEnglish
لَا (lā)MSA present tense; prohibitive commands (لَا تَفْعَلْ)لَا أَفْهَمُ.I don't understand.
مَا (mā)Gulf and Levantine colloquial present & past; also classicalمَا أَعْرِفُ. / مَا أَعْرِف.I don't know.
مِش / مُش (mish/mush)Egyptian & Levantine colloquial — negates nouns, adjectives, and sometimes verbsأنا مِش تعبان.I'm not tired.

مِش — Colloquial Negation of Adjectives and Nouns

In Egyptian Arabic and Levantine dialects, مِش (mish) is placed before a noun, adjective, or nominal predicate to negate it. It does not attach to verbs the same way لَا does:

PositiveNegative with مِشEnglish
أنا حزين.أنا مِش حزين.I'm not sad.
هو سعيد.هو مِش سعيد.He's not happy.
هي قلقانة.هي مِش قلقانة.She's not anxious.
ده مشكلة.ده مِش مشكلة.That's not a problem.

لَا for Prohibition (Imperative Negation)

لَا followed by the jussive (مضارع مجزوم) form negates a command:

  • لَا تَقْلَقْ. lā taqalaq — Don't worry.
  • لَا تَكُنْ حَزِينًا. lā takun ḥazīnan — Don't be sad.

Example Sentences with Emotion Vocabulary

  • لَا أَشْعُرُ بِالسَّعَادَةِ الآنَ. lā ashʿuru bis-saʿādati l-ān — I don't feel happy right now.
  • لَا يُحِبُّ أَحَدٌ الحُزْنَ. lā yuḥibbu aḥadun al-ḥuzna — Nobody likes sadness.
  • أنا مِش خايف، أنا قلقان. ana mish khāyif, ana qalqān — I'm not scared, I'm anxious. (Egyptian colloquial)
  • مَا أَعْرِفُ كَيْفَ أَتَصَرَّفُ. mā aʿrifu kayfa ataṣarrafa — I don't know how to act.
  • لَا تَكْتُمِ مَشَاعِرَكَ. lā taktumi mashāʿirak — Don't suppress your feelings.
  • هُوَ مِش مبسوط من الوضع. huwa mish mabsūṭ min al-waḍʿ — He's not happy with the situation. (Levantine)
