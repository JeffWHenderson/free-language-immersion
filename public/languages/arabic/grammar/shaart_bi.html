الجملة الشرطية — Conditional Sentences

Arabic has two main types of conditional sentences. إِذَا (idhā) introduces real conditions — things that could genuinely happen. لَوْ (law) introduces counterfactual (unreal) conditions — things contrary to reality or hypothetical. The verb tenses in the two clauses follow specific patterns.

إِذَا — Real Conditions

إِذَا + past tense verb → result clause (often future or imperative). Despite using a past-tense form in the condition, the meaning is present or future:

Condition (إِذَا + past)ResultMeaning
إِذَا كُنْتَ حَزِينًاتَكَلَّمْ مَعِيIf you are sad, talk to me.
إِذَا كُنْتَ مُتْعَبًااِسْتَرِحْIf you are tired, rest.
إِذَا شَعَرْتَ بِتَحَسُّنٍسَنَخْرُجIf you feel better, we will go out.

لَوْ — Counterfactual Conditions

لَوْ + past tense → لَـ + past tense. The prefix لَـ (la-) on the result clause is a marker of the counterfactual and is required:

Condition (لَوْ + past)Result (لَـ + past)Meaning
لَوْ كُنْتُ سَعِيدًالَغَنَّيْتُIf I were happy, I would sing.
لَوْ كُنْتُ غَنِيًّالَسَافَرْتُIf I were rich, I would travel.
لَوْ عَلِمْتُلَمَا جِئْتُIf I had known, I wouldn't have come.

لَوْ كُنْتُ مَكَانَكَ — If I Were You

The standard Arabic idiom for "if I were you" is لَوْ كُنْتُ مَكَانَكَ (law kuntu makānaka), literally "if I were in your place."

كُلَّمَا — Whenever

كُلَّمَا (kullamā) = whenever / every time. Related to conditional expressions, used for repeated events. It takes a past tense verb and expresses habitual patterns:

  • كُلَّمَا رَأَيْتُهُ ابْتَسَمَ. kullamā raʾaythu ibtasama — Every time I saw him, he smiled.

Example Sentences

  • لَوْ كُنْتُ سَعِيدًا لَغَنَّيْتُ. law kuntu saʿīdan la-ghannaytu — If I were happy, I would sing.
  • إِذَا كُنْتَ حَزِينًا تَكَلَّمْ مَعِي. idhā kunta ḥazīnan takallam maʿī — If you are sad, talk to me.
  • لَوْ كُنْتُ مَكَانَكَ لَاعْتَذَرْتُ. law kuntu makānaka lāʿtadhārtu — If I were you, I would apologize.
  • إِذَا كُنْتَ غَاضِبًا لَا تَتَّخِذْ قَرَارَات. idhā kunta ghāḍiban lā tattakhidh qarārāt — If you are angry, don't make decisions.
