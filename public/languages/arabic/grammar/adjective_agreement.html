Adjective Agreement

In Arabic, adjectives must agree with the noun they describe in four properties: gender (masculine/feminine), number (singular/plural), definiteness (definite/indefinite), and case (nominative/accusative/genitive — mainly relevant in formal/written Arabic). This agreement is one of the most important rules in Arabic grammar.

Gender Agreement

Add ة (ta marbuta) to make a masculine adjective feminine:

Masculine Noun + Adj.Trans.Feminine Noun + Adj.Trans.
رجل سعيدrajul sa'idامرأة سعيدةimra'a sa'ida
طالب جديدtalib jadidطالبة جديدةtaliba jadida
مطعم كبيرmat'am kabirغرفة كبيرةghurfa kabira
يوم جميلyawm jamilحديقة جميلةhadiqa jamila

Definiteness Agreement

When the noun takes ال (the definite article), the adjective must also take ال:

  • رجل سعيد a happy man (both indefinite) (rajul sa'id)
  • الرجل السعيد the happy man (both definite) (ar-rajul as-sa'id)
  • الرجل سعيد. The man is happy. (predicate — adjective stays indefinite) (ar-rajul sa'id)

Attributive vs Predicate Position

This is the key distinction beginners must learn:

TypeExampleTrans.Meaning
Attributive (modifies noun directly)الطعام اللذيذat-ta'am al-ladhidhthe delicious food
Predicate (equational sentence)الطعام لذيذ.at-ta'am ladhidh.The food is delicious.
Attributive (modifies noun directly)امرأة جميلةimra'a jamilaa beautiful woman
Predicate (equational sentence)المرأة جميلة.al-mar'a jamila.The woman is beautiful.

Plural Agreement

For human plurals, the adjective takes its plural form. For non-human plurals (objects, animals), Arabic treats them as feminine singular — a very useful shortcut:

  • الطلاب مجتهدون. The students (m.) are diligent. (at-tullab mujtahadun) — human plural
  • الكتب جديدة. The books are new. (al-kutub jadida) — non-human plural treated as fem. sg.
  • الشوارع مزدحمة. The streets are crowded. (ash-shawari' muzdahima) — non-human plural
  • المدرّسات متعبات. The (female) teachers are tired. (al-mudarrisat muta'abat) — human fem. plural

More Agreement Examples

  • أنا سعيد اليوم. I am happy today. (masc. speaker) (ana sa'id al-yawm)
  • أنا سعيدة اليوم. I am happy today. (fem. speaker) (ana sa'ida al-yawm)
  • مزاجها جيد. Her mood is good. (mizajuha jayyid — mood/مزاج is masc.)
  • هي نشيطة جداً. She is very energetic. (hiya nashita jiddan)

Common Pitfalls

  • Adjective AFTER the noun: Arabic adjectives always follow the noun, unlike English where adjectives precede. "A big house" = بيت كبير (bayt kabir — lit. "house big").
  • Non-human plural rule: This is counterintuitive but very consistent. Whenever you describe a group of things (not people), use the feminine singular adjective form: السيارات جديدة (the cars are new) NOT السيارات جدد.
  • Predicate adjectives are indefinite: In an equational sentence "The house is big," the adjective بيت كبير has no ال: البيت كبير (not البيت الكبير, which means "the big house" as a noun phrase).
