الفعل الماضي — The Past Tense

Arabic past tense verbs are built on the root system. The most common Form I pattern is فَعَلَ (fa'ala) — three root letters with the vowels a-a-a. To conjugate in the past, attach suffixes to this base. The verb ذَهَبَ (dhahaba, he went) is the base form — the third-person masculine singular needs no suffix.

Negation of the Past Tense

There are two main ways to negate the past in Arabic:

  • مَا + past verb ma + past verb — colloquial / literary negative past: ما ذَهَبتُ (I did not go)
  • لَم + present (jussive) lam + present — formal literary negative past: لم أَذْهَب (I did not go)

Example Sentences

  • أَكَلتُ في المطعم أمس. akaltu fi al-mat'am ams — I ate at the restaurant yesterday.
  • شَرِبَت القهوة. sharibat al-qahwa — She drank the coffee.
  • ذَهَبنا إلى البيت. dhahabna ila al-bayt — We went home.
  • مَا قُلتُ شيئاً. ma qultu shay'an — I didn't say anything.
  • لَم يَذْهَب إلى العمل. lam yadhhab ila al-'amal — He did not go to work.
