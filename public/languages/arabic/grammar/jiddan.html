جداً — Intensifiers

Arabic has a rich set of words for intensifying adjectives and verbs — the equivalents of "very," "really," "extremely," and "so." The most common and universal is جداً (jiddan = very), but several others appear frequently in both formal and colloquial speech.

جداً (jiddan) — Very

جداً always comes after the adjective (or sometimes after a predicate), unlike English "very" which precedes the adjective. The nunation (ً) on the ا is characteristic of this adverb.

ArabicTransliterationMeaning
سعيد جداًsa'id jiddanvery happy
كبير جداًkabir jiddanvery big
جميل جداًjamil jiddanvery beautiful
متعب جداًmuta'ab jiddanvery tired
لذيذ جداًladhidh jiddanvery delicious
قريب جداًqarib jiddanvery close / very near

كثيراً (kathiran) — A Lot / Very Much

كثيراً is used with verbs to mean "a lot" or "very much," and sometimes interchangeably with جداً after adjectives:

  • أحبك كثيراً. I love you very much. (uhibbuk kathiran)
  • شكراً كثيراً. Thank you very much. (shukran kathiran)
  • ضحكنا كثيراً. We laughed a lot. (dahikna kathiran)

Examples in Context

  • أنا متعب جداً اليوم. I am very tired today. (ana muta'ab jiddan al-yawm)
  • هي مبدعة جداً. She is very creative. (hiya mubdi'a jiddan)
  • هذه الكعكة حلوة جداً. This cake is very sweet. (hadhihi al-ka'ka hulwa jiddan)
  • الملح أكثر من اللازم. There is too much salt. (al-milh akthar min al-lazim)
  • أنا قلق جداً بشأن ذلك. I am very worried about that. (ana qaliq jiddan bi-sha'n dhalik)
  • الحساء شهيّ جداً. The soup is very tasty. (al-hisa' shahiyy jiddan)
  • هذا صحيح تماماً. That is completely correct. (hatha sahih tamaman)
  • المدينة مزدحمة للغاية. The city is extremely crowded. (al-madina muzdahima lil-ghaya)

Colloquial Intensifiers

In Levantine Arabic, you will also encounter these informal intensifiers:

  • كتير ktir — very (Levantine equivalent of كثيراً/جداً): كتير كويس = very good
  • مرّة marra — very/super (Levantine): مرة حلو = super nice

Common Pitfalls

  • Position matters: جداً always comes AFTER the adjective. Saying "جداً سعيد" (jiddan sa'id) is incorrect — it should be "سعيد جداً" (sa'id jiddan).
  • جداً doesn't change form: Unlike Arabic adjectives, جداً doesn't change for gender, number, or definiteness. It's an adverb and stays the same.
  • كثيراً with verbs: Use كثيراً after verbs (أحب كثيراً = I like a lot) and جداً after adjectives (سعيد جداً = very happy). They can overlap but this distinction is clean and natural.
