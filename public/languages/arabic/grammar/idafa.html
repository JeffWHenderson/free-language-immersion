الإضافة — The Construct State

الإضافة (al-idafa, "the addition") is Arabic's way of expressing possession and close relationships between nouns — equivalent to "of" in English or the apostrophe-s ('s). Two nouns are placed side by side: the first noun (المُضاف, al-mudaf) and the second noun (المُضاف إليه, al-mudaf ilayhi, in the genitive case). The result is a tight grammatical unit.

The Core Rule

  • First noun: NEVER has ال The mudaf (first noun) loses its article and its nunation (tanwin). It can only be definite through the following noun.
  • Second noun: MAY have ال The mudaf ilayhi (second noun) is in the genitive case. It may or may not have ال. If it does, the whole construction is definite.

Chained Idafa (More Than Two Nouns)

  • مَفتَاحُ بَابِ البَيْتِ miftahu babi al-bayti — the key of the door of the house. Only the last noun in the chain may have ال.
  • مَديرُ فُندُقِ المَدِينَةِ mudiru funduqi al-madinati — the manager of the city hotel.

More Examples in Context

  • أين مَحَطَّةُ الحافِلَة؟ ayna mahattatu al-hafila? — Where is the bus station?
  • هذا مَطعَمُ الفندق. hadha mat'amu al-funduq — This is the hotel's restaurant.
  • غُرفَةُ النَّوم كبيرة. ghurfatu an-nawm kabira — The bedroom is large. (lit. 'room of sleep')
  • جَامِعَةُ القَاهِرَة jami'atu al-qahira — Cairo University (the university of Cairo)
