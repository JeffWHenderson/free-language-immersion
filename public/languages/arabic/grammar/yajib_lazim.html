يَجِبُ / لَازِم — Expressing Obligation

Arabic has several ways to express obligation and necessity. The most common are يَجِبُ أَنْ (yajib an — it is necessary that), لَازِمٌ (lāzim — must/necessary), and يَنْبَغِي (yanbaghī — should/ought to). The verb following these expressions takes the subjunctive mood (المضارع المنصوب).

The Subjunctive (المضارع المنصوب)

After يَجِبُ أَنْ and يَنْبَغِي أَنْ, the following verb is in the subjunctive mood. The subjunctive is formed by changing the final ـُ (-u) of the present tense to ـَ (-a):

Present (Indicative)SubjunctiveMeaning

يَذْهَبُيَذْهَبَ(he) go
آخُذُآخُذَ(I) take
تَأْكُلُتَأْكُلَ(you) eat

يَجِبُ — Does Not Change

يَجِبُ (yajib) is an impersonal verb — it stays in the same form regardless of who must do the action. The person is conveyed by the conjugated verb following أَنْ:

  • يَجِبُ أَنْ أَسْتَرِيحَ yajib an astarīḥa — I must rest
  • يَجِبُ أَنْ تَسْتَرِيحَ yajib an tastarīḥa — You must rest
  • يَجِبُ أَنْ يَسْتَرِيحَ yajib an yastarīḥa — He must rest

Negation

  • لَا يَجِبُ أَنْ lā yajib an — must not / it is not necessary (lā + yajib)
  • يَنْبَغِي أَلَّا yanbaghī allā — should not (yanbaghī + allā, where allā = an + lā contracted)

Example Sentences

  • يَجِبُ أَنْ تَسْتَرِيحَ. yajib an tastarīḥa — You must rest.
  • يَجِبُ أَنْ آخُذَ دَوَاءً. yajib an ākhudha dawāʾan — I should take medicine.
  • لَازِمٌ تَذْهَبَ إِلَى الطَّبِيب. lāzim tadhhaba ilā l-ṭabīb — You should go to the doctor.
  • يَنْبَغِي أَلَّا تُدَخِّنَ. yanbaghī allā tudakhkhina — You should not smoke.
  • مِن الضَّرُورِيّ أَنْ تَنَامَ مُبَكِّرًا. min al-ḍarūriyy an tanāma mubakkiran — It is necessary to sleep early.
