Quantity Expressions with Food — أسماء الكميات

Arabic has specific words and constructions for expressing quantities of food and drink. Many follow the pattern quantity word + مِن (min, of) + noun, while some use بَعْض + definite noun. Learning these makes ordering food and shopping much more natural.

Questions About Quantity

ArabicRomanizedEnglish
كَمْ تريد؟kam turid?How much/many do you want?
كَمْ كيلو؟kam kilu?How many kilos?
كَمِ السعر؟kami as-si'r?How much is the price?

Example Phrases with Food

  • بَعض الخبز ba'd al-khubz — some bread (note: definite article on the noun)
  • كَثِير من الأكل kathir min al-akl — a lot of food
  • قَلِيل من السكر qalil min as-sukkar — a little sugar
  • نِصف كيلو دجاج nisf kilu dajaj — half a kilo of chicken
  • رُبع كيلو جبن rub' kilu jubn — a quarter kilo of cheese
  • كُوب حليب kub halib — a cup of milk
  • كيلو طماطم kilu tamatim — a kilo of tomatoes
  • أريد بعض الأرز، من فضلك. urid ba'd al-aruzz, min fadlak — I'd like some rice, please.
