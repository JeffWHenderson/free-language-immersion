Numbers & Counting — الأعداد

Arabic numbers are essential for everyday life — shopping, telling time, giving your phone number. One important quirk sets Arabic apart: numbers 3–10 use the opposite gender of the noun they count.

Teens and Twenty

Teens are formed by combining the unit with عشر (ʿashar = ten). The pattern is unit + ʿashar. Twenty is عشرون (ʿishrūn) in formal Arabic, عشرين (ʿishrīn) in colloquial speech.

The Gender Reversal Rule (3–10)

Numbers 3–10 take the opposite gender of the noun they count. This is one of the most famous quirks of Arabic grammar. Numbers 1 and 2 agree with the noun normally; numbers 11 and above follow different rules.

Masculine noun + feminine number
ثلاثة كتب thalātha kutub — three books (كتاب, book, is masc. → number is fem. ثلاثة)
Feminine noun + masculine number
ثلاث بنات thalāth banāt — three girls (بنات, girls, is fem. → number is masc. ثلاث, without ة)

The masculine form of numbers 3–10 drops the ة (tāʾ marbūṭa): ثلاثة (fem.) → ثلاث (masc.). The counted noun appears in the plural.

Larger Numbers

ArabicRomanizedEnglish
واحد وعشرونwāḥid wa-ʿishrūntwenty-one (lit. "one and twenty")
مئةmiʾaone hundred
ألفalfone thousand

Compound numbers above 20 are formed with و (wa = and): واحد وعشرون = one and twenty = 21. Units come before tens.

Telling Time

Time uses ordinal numbers (second, third, etc.) with الساعة (al-sāʿa = the hour/clock).

ArabicRomanizedEnglish
الساعة الثانيةal-sāʿa al-thāniya2 o'clock
الساعة الثالثةal-sāʿa al-thālitha3 o'clock
الساعة الثالثة والنصفal-sāʿa al-thālitha wa-n-niṣf3:30 (three and a half)
الساعة الرابعة والربعal-sāʿa al-rābiʿa wa-r-rubʿ4:15 (four and a quarter)

Example Sentences

  • عندي خمسة أصدقاء. ʿindī khamsa aṣdiqāʾ. — I have five friends. (أصدقاء, friends, is masc. pl. → fem. number خمسة)
  • أريد ثلاث تذاكر من فضلك. urīd thalāth tadhākir min faḍlak. — I want three tickets, please. (تذاكر, tickets, is fem. → masc. number ثلاث)
  • الساعة الآن الثانية والنصف. al-sāʿa al-ān al-thāniya wa-n-niṣf. — It is now 2:30.
  • المتجر يفتح الساعة التاسعة. al-matjar yaftaḥ al-sāʿa al-tāsiʿa. — The store opens at 9 o'clock.
