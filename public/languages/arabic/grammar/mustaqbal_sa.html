المستقبل — The Future Tense

Arabic does not have a separate future tense verb form. Instead, the future is expressed by attaching the prefix سَـ (sa-) or the word سَوْفَ (sawfa) to the present tense verb. The present tense verb form remains unchanged.

سَـ vs. سَوْفَ

PrefixRomanizedRegisterExampleMeaning
سَـsa-everyday speech and writingسَأَذْهَبُI will go
سَوْفَsawfaformal, written, emphaticسَوْفَ أَذْهَبُI will go

Both mean the same thing. سَـ is more common in everyday speech and news. سَوْفَ appears in formal writing, official announcements, and the Quran. They are not combined — choose one or the other.

Future Negation — لَنْ

The future is negated with لَنْ (lan), not with لَا or مَا. لَنْ is followed by the subjunctive mood (the final ـُ changes to ـَ). Do not use سَـ or سَوْفَ with لَنْ.

  • لَنْ أَذْهَبَ lan adhhaba — I will not go (emphatic)
  • لَنْ تَسْتَسْلِمَ lan tastaslima — She will not give up
  • لَنْ نَنْسَى lan nansā — We will never forget

Yes/No Questions about the Future

Add هَلْ (hal) before the future verb to form a yes/no question:

  • هَلْ سَتَأْتِي؟ hal sa-taʾtī? — Will you come?
  • هَلْ سَيَكُونُ هُنَاك؟ hal sa-yakūnu hunāk? — Will he be there?

Example Sentences

  • سَأَعْمَلُ غَدًا. saʾaʿmalu ghadan — I will work tomorrow.
  • سَتَدْرُسُ فِي الْجَامِعَة. sa-tadrusu fī l-jāmiʿa — She will study at university.
  • سَوْفَ تَفْتَحُ الشَّرِكَةُ فَرْعًا جَدِيدًا. sawfa taftaḥu l-sharikatu farʿan jadīdan — The company will open a new branch.
  • لَنْ أَذْهَبَ إِلَى هُنَاك. lan adhhaba ilā hunāk — I will not go there.
