كَانَ — Was / Were (Past of "To Be")

Arabic has no present-tense verb "to be" — sentences like "the food is delicious" use no verb at all. But for the past, Arabic uses the verb كَانَ (kana, was/were). كان is a full verb that conjugates for person and gender. The predicate adjective or noun that follows takes the accusative case (often shown with tanwin -an or -atan on indefinite adjectives).

Conjugation of كَانَ

PronounArabicRomanizedMeaning
هو (he)كَانَkanahe was
هي (she)كَانَتkanatshe was
أنا (I)كُنتُkuntuI was
أنتَ (you, m.)كُنتَkuntayou were (m.)
أنتِ (you, f.)كُنتِkuntiyou were (f.)
نحن (we)كُنَّاkunnawe were

كَانَ + Adjective (Food and Drink Context)

  • كَانَ الطَّعَامُ لَذِيذًا. kana at-ta'am ladhidhan — The food was delicious. (adjective in accusative: لذيذًا)
  • كَانَتِ القَهْوَةُ بَارِدَةً. kanat al-qahwa baridatan — The coffee was cold. (feminine adjective: بارِدةً)
  • كَانَ الحَسَاءُ سَاخِنًا. kana al-hisa' sakhianan — The soup was hot.
  • كَانَ المطعمُ مزدَحِمًا. kana al-mat'am muzdahiman — The restaurant was crowded.
  • كُنتُ جائعًا. kuntu ja'i'an — I was hungry.
  • كُنَّا عطشانِين. kunna 'atshaniyn — We were thirsty.

Key Grammar Note

  • Accusative predicate After كان, the predicate (adjective or noun) takes the accusative case. Indefinite adjectives add tanwin: لذيذ → لَذِيذًا (ladhidhan), بارِدة → بَارِدَةً (baridatan). In everyday speech this distinction is often dropped, but it is standard in MSA writing.
