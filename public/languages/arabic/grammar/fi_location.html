في + Location

The preposition في (fi) is one of the most frequently used words in Arabic. It means "in," "at," or "inside" and is used to express where something or someone is located, when something happens, and membership in a category or group.

Expressing Location

The pattern is simple: Subject + في + Place (with no verb "to be" needed in the present tense).

ArabicTransliterationMeaning
أنا في البيت.ana fi al-baytI am at home.
هو في المكتب.huwa fi al-maktabHe is at the office.
هي في المطبخ.hiya fi al-matbakhShe is in the kitchen.
نحن في المطعم.nahnu fi al-mat'amWe are at the restaurant.
الكتاب في الحقيبة.al-kitab fi al-haqibaThe book is in the bag.
المفاتيح في الدرج.al-mafatih fi ad-durjThe keys are in the drawer.

في with Time Expressions

في is also used to say "in the morning," "at night," and other time expressions:

  • في الصباح in the morning (fi as-sabah)
  • في المساء in the evening (fi al-masa')
  • في الليل at night (fi al-layl)
  • في الأسبوع in the week (fi al-usbu')
  • في الصيف in summer (fi as-sayf)

في Answering "Where?" Questions

When someone asks أين؟ (ayna? = where?), the answer typically uses في:

  • أين الحمام؟ — في النهاية على اليسار. Where is the bathroom? — At the end on the left. (ayna al-hammam? — fi an-nihaya 'ala al-yasar)
  • أين تعيش؟ — أعيش في دمشق. Where do you live? — I live in Damascus. (ayna ta'ish? — a'ish fi dimashq)
  • أين السيارة؟ — في الموقف. Where is the car? — In the parking lot. (ayna as-sayyara? — fi al-mawqif)

Common Phrases with في

  • أدرس في الجامعة. I study at the university. (adrus fi al-jami'a)
  • أعمل في مستشفى. I work in a hospital. (a'mal fi mustashfa)
  • يلعبون في الحديقة. They play in the park. (yal'abun fi al-hadiqa)
  • الشطيرة فيها جبن. The sandwich has cheese in it. (ash-shatira fiha jubn) — فيها = "in it" (fem.)
  • في أمان الله. Goodbye (lit. "in God's protection"). (fi aman allah)
  • أحب القهوة في الصباح. I like coffee in the morning. (uhibb al-qahwa fi as-sabah)

فيه / فيها — "In It"

When في is combined with a pronoun suffix, it becomes فيه (fihi = in it, masc.) or فيها (fiha = in it, fem.):

  • الحساء فيه ملح كثير. The soup has a lot of salt in it. (al-hisa' fihi milh kathir)
  • المدينة فيها ناس كثيرون. The city has many people in it. (al-madina fiha nas kathirun)

Common Pitfalls

  • في vs عند: في (fi) = physically inside a place. عند ('inda) = at/near someone. أنا في المتجر = I'm inside the store. أنا عند المتجر = I'm at/by the store (outside).
  • Attached pronouns: في + ي (me) = فيّ (fiyya), في + ك (you) = فيك (fik), في + نا (us) = فينا (fina). These contracted forms are very common in speech.
  • No verb needed: Remember, present-tense "to be" is dropped. أنا في البيت (I am at home) has no verb — that's correct Arabic.
