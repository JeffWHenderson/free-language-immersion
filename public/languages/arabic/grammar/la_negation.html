لا & ما — Negation

Arabic has several ways to say "no" or "not," each used in specific contexts. The two most essential for beginners are لا (la) and ما (ma). Choosing between them depends on what you are negating: a verb, a noun, an adjective, or a habitual statement.

لا (la) — Negating Verbs and Nouns

لا is placed directly before a present-tense verb to negate it:

AffirmativeNegative with لاMeaning
أفهملا أفهمI don't understand (la afham)
يأكل اللحملا يأكل اللحمHe doesn't eat meat (la ya'kul al-lahm)
أحب البصللا أحب البصلI don't like onions (la uhibb al-basal)
تعرفلا أعرفI don't know (la a'rif)

لا is also used as a standalone "no" in response to yes/no questions, and to negate nouns/adjectives in equational sentences:

  • لا مشكلة. No problem. (la mushkila)
  • لا، شكراً. No, thank you. (la, shukran)
  • لا وقت عندي. I have no time. (la waqt 'indi)

ما (ma) — Negating Past & Equational Sentences

ما is commonly used in colloquial Arabic (including Levantine) to negate both past-tense verbs and equational sentences. It is very frequent in everyday speech:

  • ما أكلت. I didn't eat. (ma akalt) — Levantine
  • ما عرفت. I didn't know. (ma 'araft) — Levantine
  • ما هو صعب. It is not difficult. (ma huwa sa'b) — Levantine
  • ما في مشكلة. There's no problem. (ma fi mushkila) — Levantine colloquial

لم (lam) — Formal Past Negation

In Modern Standard Arabic, past-tense verbs are negated with لم + the present-tense form of the verb (which then has a short vowel ending):

  • لم أذهب. I did not go. (lam adhhab)
  • لم يأكل. He did not eat. (lam ya'kul)
  • لم أفهم الدرس. I did not understand the lesson. (lam afham ad-dars)

لن (lan) — Negating the Future

لن + present verb = will not:

  • لن أذهب غداً. I will not go tomorrow. (lan adhhab ghadan)
  • لن يأتي. He will not come. (lan ya'ti)

Common Pitfalls

  • لا vs ما in colloquial speech: In Levantine Arabic, ما is extremely common for negation across many contexts. You'll hear ما بعرف (ma b'arif = I don't know) more often than لا أعرف in conversation.
  • لم + present form: After لم, the verb looks like a present tense form but means past. لم يذهب looks like "he goes" but means "he did not go."
  • أبداً for "never": لا + verb + أبداً = never. لا أتأخر أبداً = I never arrive late. أبداً alone in answer to a question means "never/absolutely not."
