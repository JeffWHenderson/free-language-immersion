المقارنة — Comparisons

Arabic uses two main methods for comparison. The first uses أَكْثَر مِن (akthara min — more than) and أَقَلّ مِن (aqalla min — less than) with nouns or adjectives. The second uses the أَفْعَل (elative) pattern, which is a special form derived from adjectives for comparatives and superlatives.

أَكْثَر / أَقَلّ — Analytical Comparison

These words are used before nouns or before adjectives (especially those that don't have a common elative form):

StructureMeaningExampleEnglish
أَكْثَر + adj/noun + مِنmore ... thanهَذَا العَمَلُ أَكْثَرُ إِثَارَةً مِنْ ذَاكَ.This job is more exciting than that one.
أَقَلّ + adj/noun + مِنless ... thanهِوَايَتِي أَقَلُّ إِجْهَادًا مِنْ عَمَلِي.My hobby is less tiring than my work.
بِقَدْرِas much asأَعْمَلُ بِقَدْرِ مَا تَعْمَلُ.I work as much as you work.

The Elative Form — أَفْعَل Pattern

Many common adjectives have a special comparative/superlative form following the أَفْعَل (af'al) root pattern. This form is diptote (it does not take tanwīn and only takes ḍamma or fatḥa, never kasra):

AdjectiveMeaningElative (أَفْعَل)Meaning
كَبِيرbigأَكْبَرbigger / the biggest
صَغِيرsmallأَصْغَرsmaller / the smallest
جَمِيلbeautifulأَجْمَلmore beautiful / the most beautiful
سَرِيعfastأَسْرَعfaster / the fastest
قَرِيبnearأَقْرَبnearer / the nearest
صَعْبdifficultأَصْعَبmore difficult / the most difficult
سَهْلeasyأَسْهَلeasier / the easiest
طَوِيلtall / longأَطْوَلtaller / the tallest
قَصِيرshortأَقْصَرshorter / the shortest
كَثِيرmuch / manyأَكْثَرmore / the most

Comparative: Elative + مِن

To compare two things, use the elative form followed by مِن (than):

ExampleTransliterationEnglish
عَمَلِي أَكْبَرُ مِنْ عَمَلِكَ.ʿamalī akbaru min ʿamalik.My company is bigger than yours.
هَذَا المَشْرُوعُ أَصْعَبُ مِنَ الأَوَّلِ.hādhā l-mashrūʿu aṣʿabu min al-awwal.This project is harder than the first.
الرِّيَاضَةُ أَسْهَلُ مِنَ البَرْمَجَةِ بِالنِّسْبَةِ لِي.ar-riyāḍatu ashal min al-barmaja bin-nisba lī.Sports is easier than programming for me.

Superlative: Definite Article + Elative

To form the superlative ("the most..."), add the definite article الـ before the elative. The elative form itself does not change:

ExampleTransliterationEnglish
هُوَ الأَفْضَلُ فِي الفَرِيقِ.huwa l-afḍalu fi l-farīq.He is the best on the team.
هَذَا الأَصْعَبُ فِي مِهْنَتِي.hādhā l-aṣʿabu fī mihnatī.This is the hardest part of my profession.
هِيَ الأَجْمَلُ فِي المَكْتَبِ.hiya l-ajmalu fi l-maktab.She is the most beautiful in the office.

الأَفْضَل and الأَحْسَن — The Best

أَفْضَل (afḍal, from فَاضِل — virtuous/excellent) and أَحْسَن (aḥsan, from حَسَن — good) both mean "better / the best" and are among the most common irregular elatives:

  • هَذَا أَفْضَلُ عَمَلٍ فِي حَيَاتِي. hādhā afḍalu ʿamalin fī ḥayātī — This is the best job of my life.
  • الصِّحَّةُ أَحْسَنُ مِنَ الثَّرْوَةِ. aṣ-ṣiḥḥatu aḥsanu min ath-tharwa — Health is better than wealth.

Example Sentences in Job and Hobby Context

  • هِوَايَتِي أَجْمَلُ مِنْ عَمَلِي. hiwāyatī ajmalu min ʿamalī — My hobby is more enjoyable than my job.
  • هُوَ أَسْرَعُ مُبَرْمِجٍ فِي الشَّرِكَةِ. huwa asraʿu mubarammijin fi sh-sharika — He is the fastest programmer in the company.
  • أَعْمَلُ أَكْثَرَ مِنْ ثَمَانِي سَاعَاتٍ يَوْمِيًّا. aʿmalu akthara min thamānī sāʿātin yawmiyyan — I work more than eight hours a day.
  • الطَّبِيبُ أَقَلُّ وَقْتًا فَرَاغًا مِنَ المُعَلِّمِ. aṭ-ṭabību aqallu waqtan farāghan min al-muʿallim — The doctor has less free time than the teacher.
  • هَذَا المَشْرُوعُ أَكْبَرُ مِنَ الَّذِي أَنْجَزْنَاهُ العَامَ الماضِي. hādhā l-mashrūʿu akbaru min alladhī anjaznaahu l-ʿāma l-māḍī — This project is bigger than the one we completed last year.
