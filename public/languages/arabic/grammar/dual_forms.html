المثنى — The Arabic Dual

Arabic has a special grammatical number called the dual (المثنى, al-muthannā) for expressing exactly two of anything. It sits between singular and plural. Body parts that come in pairs — eyes, hands, feet, ears — naturally use the dual. The dual is formed by adding a suffix to the singular noun.

Feminine Nouns Ending in ة

When a feminine noun ends in ة (taa' marbuta), the ة changes to ت before the dual suffix is added:

  • رِئَة + ـَانِ rʾa + ānī → رِئَتَانِ (riʾatāni) — two lungs. The ة opens to ت.
  • مَرَّة + ـَانِ marra + āni → مَرَّتَانِ (marratāni) — two times.

Definite Dual

To make the dual definite, simply add الـ before the noun; the dual suffix remains:

  • الأُذُنَانِ al-ʾudhunāni — the two ears / both ears
  • الْيَدَيْنِ (after preposition) al-yadayni — in the two hands: فِي الْيَدَيْنِ (fī l-yadayni)

كِلَا / كِلْتَا — Both

كِلَا (kilā) = both (masculine dual), كِلْتَا (kiltā) = both (feminine dual). Both are always followed by a definite dual noun in the genitive:

  • كِلَا الطَّبَقَيْنِ kilā l-ṭabaqayni — both dishes (masc.)
  • كِلْتَا الْيَدَيْنِ kiltā l-yadayni — both hands (fem.)

Example Sentences

  • عِنْدِي عَيْنَانِ. ʿindī ʿaynāni — I have two eyes.
  • يَدَاهُ كَبِيرَتَانِ. yadāhu kabīratāni — His two hands are large. (dual adjective agrees in gender)
  • آلَمَتْنِي قَدَمَايَ. ālamatnī qadamāya — My two feet hurt me.
  • سَمِعْتُ بِأُذُنَيَّ. samiʿtu bi-ʾudhunayya — I heard with my own two ears. (genitive after bi-)
