Possessive Suffixes — الضمائر المتصلة للملكية

In Arabic, possession is expressed by attaching a suffix directly to the end of a noun — no separate word like "my" or "your" is needed. These suffixes are called ضمائر الملكية (possessive pronouns). The noun's final vowel may shift slightly when ـي (my) is added.

Key Notes

  • ـي (my) — vowel shift When adding ـي, the noun's final short vowel is replaced by a kasra (i): كِتَابٌ → كِتَابِي (kitabun → kitabi). The nunation (ـٌ) disappears.
  • أُمِّي (ummi) Nouns ending in ـة (ta marbuta) drop the ta marbuta before most suffixes: أُمّ + ي = أُمِّي. Similarly: غُرفة → غُرفتي (my room).
  • No article with possessives A noun with a possessive suffix is already definite — you cannot add ال (al-). كِتَابِي = my book (definite). You would not say الكتابي.

Example Sentences

  • اسمِي أحمد. ismi Ahmad — My name is Ahmad.
  • بَيتُنا قريب. baytuna qarib — Our house is nearby.
  • هذا كتابُهَا. hadha kitabuha — This is her book.
  • أين مفتاحُكَ؟ ayna miftahuka? — Where is your key? (m.)
