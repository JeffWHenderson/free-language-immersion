Equational Sentences — No Verb "To Be"

One of the most distinctive features of Arabic is that the present-tense verb "to be" does not exist. To say "The coffee is hot" or "I am a doctor," you simply place the subject next to the predicate — no linking verb needed. These are called equational sentences (الجملة الاسمية, al-jumla al-ismiyya).

The Basic Pattern

Definiteness Rule

In equational sentences, the predicate adjective or noun is typically indefinite (no ال). This distinguishes a predicate from a modifier:

  • الرجل كبير. The man is old. (predicate — indefinite adjective) (ar-rajul kabir)
  • الرجل الكبير the old man (noun phrase — definite adjective modifying noun) (ar-rajul al-kabir)

More Examples

  • أنا جائع. I am hungry. (ana ja'i')
  • هي طبيبة. She is a doctor. (hiya tabiba)
  • البيت قريب من هنا. The house is close to here. (al-bayt qarib min huna)
  • العربية جميلة. Arabic is beautiful. (al-arabiyya jamila)
  • الماء بارد. The water is cold. (al-ma' barid)
  • أختي طالبة في الجامعة. My sister is a student at the university. (ukhti taliba fi al-jami'a)
  • مزاجي جيد اليوم. My mood is good today. (mizaji jayyid al-yawm)

Past and Future: When "To Be" Appears

The verb كان (kana) = "was/were" is used in the past tense. For the future, سيكون (sayakun) = "will be" is used. Only the present tense drops the verb.

  • كان الطقس جميلاً. The weather was beautiful. (kana at-taqis jamilan)
  • سيكون الطقس جميلاً. The weather will be beautiful. (sayakun at-taqis jamilan)

Common Pitfalls

  • Don't insert "is": New learners often want to add a word for "is." There is no such word needed in the present tense. الطعام لذيذ (at-ta'am ladhidh) is complete — "The food [is] delicious."
  • Word order: Arabic equational sentences are typically Subject + Predicate. Reversing this can shift emphasis or change meaning.
  • Pronoun as subject: When the subject is a pronoun and the predicate is a definite noun, a pronoun "separator" هو/هي is inserted: أنا هو المدير (ana huwa al-mudir = I am the manager). This avoids confusion with a definite noun phrase.
