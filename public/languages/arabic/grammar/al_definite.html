ال — The Definite Article

Arabic has one definite article: ال (al-), equivalent to English "the." It is always attached to the front of the noun with no space. Unlike English, Arabic has no indefinite article — "a book" is simply كتاب (kitab), while "the book" is الكتاب (al-kitab).

Sun Letters vs Moon Letters

This is one of the most important pronunciation rules in Arabic. When ال precedes certain letters called sun letters (الحروف الشمسية), the "l" of ال is assimilated — it sounds like the first letter of the word instead.

TypeWrittenPronouncedMeaning
Moon letter (no change)الكتابal-kitabthe book
Moon letter (no change)البيتal-baytthe house
Sun letter (l → n)النورan-nur (not al-nur)the light
Sun letter (l → sh)الشمسash-shams (not al-shams)the sun
Sun letter (l → t)الطعامat-ta'am (not al-ta'am)the food
Sun letter (l → d)الدجاجad-dajaj (not al-dajaj)the chicken
Sun letter (l → r)الرزar-ruzz (not al-ruzz)the rice

Tip: The spelling never changes — it's always written ال. Only the pronunciation changes. Sun letters include: ت ث د ذ ر ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ل ن.

When to Use ال

  • أريد قهوة. I want a coffee. (urid qahwa) — indefinite, first mention
  • القهوة ساخنة. The coffee is hot. (al-qahwa sakhina) — definite, already known
  • أحب الخبز. I like bread. (uhibb al-khubz) — generic/category use
  • الخبز طازج. The bread is fresh. (al-khubz tazij)
  • هل عندك وصفة جيدة؟ Do you have a good recipe? (hal indaka wasfa jayyida?) — indefinite

ال with Adjectives

When an adjective modifies a definite noun, the adjective also takes ال:

  • الخبز الطازج the fresh bread (al-khubz at-tazij) — both definite
  • خبز طازج fresh bread (khubz tazij) — both indefinite
  • الخبز طازج. The bread is fresh. (equational sentence — adjective stays indefinite)

Common Pitfalls

  • Generic categories: Unlike English ("I like coffee"), Arabic often uses ال for generic references: أحب القهوة (I like coffee/the coffee). Both are correct; the definite form is very common.
  • No indefinite article: Arabic has no word for "a/an." An unadorned noun is indefinite by default.
  • Pronunciation, not spelling: Don't try to change the spelling for sun letters. Always write ال, just pronounce the "l" as the following letter.
