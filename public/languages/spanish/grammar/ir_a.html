Ir + a — Going Places & Near Future

Ir (to go) is one of the most common Spanish verbs. It's irregular — you just have to memorize it. It has two main uses: going to a place, and talking about the near future.

Going to a place — ir + a + lugar

  • Voy al mercado. I'm going to the market.
  • Vamos a la escuela. We're going to school.
  • ¿A dónde vas? Where are you going?

Note: a + el = al. You always contract these: voy al mercado (not a el).

Near future — ir + a + infinitive

Like English "going to," use ir a + infinitive for things you plan to do soon:

  • Voy a comer. I'm going to eat.
  • ¿Vas a venir? Are you going to come?
  • Vamos a estudiar. We're going to study.
