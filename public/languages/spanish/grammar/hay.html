Hay — There Is / There Are

Hay comes from the verb haber and means "there is" or "there are." It never changes — use the same form for singular and plural.

Basic uses

SpanishEnglish
Hay un banco.There is a bank.
Hay muchas tiendas.There are many stores.
No hay hospital aquí.There is no hospital here.
¿Hay un supermercado?Is there a supermarket?

Hay vs. Está / Están

Use hay to announce the existence of something. Use está/están to say where a specific thing is:

  • Hay un banco en la calle. There is a bank on the street. (announcing it exists)
  • El banco está en la calle principal. The bank is on the main street. (locating a specific bank)

Hay que + infinitive

Use hay que + infinitive for impersonal obligation — "one must" or "you have to":

  • Hay que ir al centro. You have to go downtown. / One must go downtown.
  • Hay que reservar con anticipación. You have to book in advance.
