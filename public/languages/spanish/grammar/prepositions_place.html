Prepositions of Place

Spanish prepositions of place describe where something or someone is located relative to another thing. Many are compound prepositions built with de. When de is followed by el, they contract to del — this contraction is mandatory.

The de + el → del contraction

Whenever de precedes the masculine singular article el, they must contract to del. There is no contraction with la, los, or las.

  • La farmacia está al lado del banco. The pharmacy is next to the bank. (del = de + el)
  • El hotel está enfrente del parque. The hotel is across from the park.
  • La parada está cerca de la plaza. The stop is near the square. (no contraction — la)

Examples with location vocabulary

  • El banco está detrás del hotel. The bank is behind the hotel.
  • La farmacia está enfrente de la estación. The pharmacy is across from the station.
  • El restaurante está entre el banco y la biblioteca. The restaurant is between the bank and the library.
  • El aeropuerto está lejos del centro. The airport is far from the city centre.
  • La salida está a la derecha del ascensor. The exit is to the right of the elevator.
  • El mapa está encima de la mesa. The map is on top of the table.
  • La maleta está debajo de la cama. The suitcase is under the bed.
