Frequency Adverbs — siempre, a veces, nunca

Frequency adverbs tell us how often something happens. In Spanish they are flexible in placement — they can go before the verb, at the start of the sentence, or at the end — but they most commonly appear before the main verb or at the start of the sentence.

Placement examples

  • Siempre llego puntual al trabajo. I always arrive on time to work.
  • Normalmente trabajo desde casa los viernes. I usually work from home on Fridays.
  • A veces salgo a correr antes del trabajo. Sometimes I go for a run before work.
  • De vez en cuando voy al gimnasio. From time to time I go to the gym.
  • Casi nunca como en restaurantes entre semana. I almost never eat in restaurants on weekdays.
  • Nunca llego tarde a las reuniones. I never arrive late to meetings.

Double negative with nunca

Nunca can come before the verb (standard) or after the verb if no is added before the verb. Spanish double negatives are grammatically correct.

  • Nunca trabajo los domingos. I never work on Sundays.
  • No trabajo nunca los domingos. I never work on Sundays. (double negative — also correct)
