Quisiera — Polite Ordering

Quisiera is the past subjunctive of querer (to want), but in modern Spanish it functions as a polite "I would like." It softens requests and is the standard register in restaurants and shops. Me gustaría (from gustar) works the same way.

Politeness register contrast

SpanishEnglishRegister
Quiero un café.I want a coffee.Blunt / casual
Quisiera un café.I would like a coffee.Polite / standard
Me gustaría un café.I would like a coffee.Polite / slightly softer

Quisiera + noun (ordering food)

  • Quisiera la ensalada, por favor. I would like the salad, please.
  • Quisiera un vaso de agua. I would like a glass of water.
  • Quisiera el menú del día. I would like the daily special.

Quisiera + infinitive (polite requests)

  • Quisiera pedir la cuenta. I would like to ask for the bill.
  • Quisiera reservar una mesa. I would like to reserve a table.
  • Quisiera probar el postre. I would like to try the dessert.

Me gustaría + infinitive

  • Me gustaría ver el menú. I would like to see the menu.
  • Me gustaría pedir algo de postre. I would like to order something for dessert.
  • Me gustaría saber qué recomienda. I would like to know what you recommend.

Other forms of quisiera

Quisiera works for all persons without changing form in practice — you can also say ¿quisiera usted...? to politely offer something to another person.

  • ¿Quisiera usted algo más? Would you like anything else? (formal)
  • ¿No quisiera probar el vino? Wouldn't you like to try the wine?
