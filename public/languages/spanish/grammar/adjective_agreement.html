Adjective Agreement — Gender & Number

In Spanish, adjectives must agree in gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural) with the noun they describe. This is one of the most consistent rules in the language.

The -o / -a pattern

Most adjectives end in -o (masculine) and change to -a for feminine. Add -s for plural.

Invariable adjectives (no gender change)

Adjectives that end in -e or a consonant usually have the same form for masculine and feminine. Only number changes.

Position — adjectives usually come after the noun

Unlike English, descriptive adjectives in Spanish normally follow the noun. A few common adjectives (bueno, malo, grande) can come before and may change form.

  • El pan fresco The fresh bread (adjective after noun)
  • La sopa caliente The hot soup
  • Los tacos fritos The fried tacos (plural, masculine)
  • Las papas fritas The fried potatoes (plural, feminine)
  • La salsa picante The spicy sauce (invariable: same for f/m)
  • El pollo delicioso / La ensalada deliciosa The delicious chicken / The delicious salad

Using ser vs. estar with food adjectives

Ser is used for inherent or defining qualities; estar is used for current states. Both are common with food.

  • El chile es picante. (ser) Chili is spicy. (it's always that way)
  • La sopa está fría. (estar) The soup is cold. (right now)
  • El pan está fresco. (estar) The bread is fresh. (current condition)
