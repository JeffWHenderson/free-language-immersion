Voz Pasiva — Ser + Participio

Spanish has three ways to express passive meaning. The ser + past participle passive is the most formal and emphasizes the action being done to the subject. The agent (who did it) can be expressed with por. Two other common alternatives — estar + participle and reflexive se — each carry a different nuance.

Structure: ser + past participle

The past participle must agree in gender and number with the subject, just like an adjective.

SubjectSer (conjugated)Past participleEnglish
El informefueescritoThe report was written
La cartafueescritaThe letter was written
Los contratosfueronfirmadosThe contracts were signed
Las propuestasfueronaprobadasThe proposals were approved

Adding the agent with por

  • El proyecto fue diseñado por el equipo. The project was designed by the team.
  • El contrato fue firmado por el gerente. The contract was signed by the manager.
  • El premio fue ganado por ella. The prize was won by her.

Ser vs. estar + past participle

Ser + participle describes the action (something happening). Estar + participle describes the resulting state (how something ends up).

Ser (action / process)Estar (resulting state)
La tienda fue cerrada a las seis.La tienda está cerrada.
The store was closed at six (by someone).The store is closed (it's in a closed state).
El trabajo fue terminado ayer.El trabajo está terminado.
The work was finished yesterday (action).The work is finished (current state).

Reflexive passive — se + verb

In everyday speech, the reflexive passive with se is far more common than the formal ser passive. It is used when there is no specific agent, or when talking about general practices.

ExampleMeaning
Se habla español aquí.Spanish is spoken here.
Se venden pisos en este edificio.Apartments are sold in this building.
Se busca programador.Programmer wanted. (literally: a programmer is sought)
¿Cómo se dice esto?How is this said? / How do you say this?

More examples in work and professional contexts

  • El informe fue presentado por la directora. The report was presented by the director.
  • Los empleados fueron contratados el año pasado. The employees were hired last year.
  • Se requiere experiencia previa. Previous experience is required.
  • El problema ya está resuelto. The problem is already solved. (state — estar)
  • El problema fue resuelto por el equipo. The problem was solved by the team. (action — ser)
