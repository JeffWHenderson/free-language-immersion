Imperfecto — Was / Used To

The imperfect describes past states, ongoing or habitual actions, and background context — things that "were happening" or that you "used to do." It contrasts with the preterite, which covers completed, one-time events.

Imperfect vs. preterite — when to use each

ImperfectPreterite
Ongoing / background stateCompleted, one-time event
Habitual action (used to, would always)Single action at a specific moment
Era niño cuando... (I was a child when...)...llegó la carta. (...the letter arrived.)

Era and estaba for states

Era (from ser) describes inherent character or identity in the past. Estaba (from estar) describes a temporary emotional or physical state in the past — both are common with emotion words.

Examples with emotion words

  • De niño, era muy feliz. As a child, I was very happy.
  • Estaba triste cuando me enteré. I was sad when I found out.
  • Tenía miedo de hablar en público. I used to be afraid of speaking in public.
  • Solía preocuparme por todo. I used to worry about everything.
  • Siempre estábamos contentos juntos. We were always happy together.
  • Era una persona muy tranquila. She was a very calm person.
  • Cuando era joven, no tenía estrés. When I was young, I had no stress.

Time markers that signal the imperfect

  • cuando era niño/a — when I was a child
  • siempre — always (habitual)
  • todos los días — every day
  • antes — before / in the past
  • solía + infinitive — used to (do something)
