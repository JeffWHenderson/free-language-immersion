Tener que — Obligation & Necessity

Tener que + infinitive expresses obligation or necessity: "have to" or "must." The verb tener is conjugated to match the subject; the infinitive never changes.

Pattern

[subject] + tener (conjugated) + que + infinitive

Tener — conjugation

Tener que — examples

  • Tengo que trabajar. I have to work.
  • Tienes que estudiar. You have to study.
  • Ella tiene que ir al médico. She has to go to the doctor.
  • Tenemos que salir ahora. We have to leave now.

Comparing obligation verbs

tener que Tengo que llamar. I have to call. — strong obligation, external or necessary
deber Debo comer más. I should eat more. — moral duty or advice
necesitar Necesito descansar. I need to rest. — practical need

All three follow the same structure: subject + verb + infinitive. Tener que is the strongest obligation; deber expresses moral duty or advice; necesitar conveys practical need.

More examples

  • ¿Tienes que trabajar mañana? Do you have to work tomorrow?
  • No tengo que ir si no quiero. I don't have to go if I don't want to.
  • Debes hablar con ella. You should talk to her.
