Imperatives — Commands

The imperative is used to give commands, instructions, or advice. Spanish has different command forms for tú (informal singular) and usted (formal/medical register). Negative commands use the subjunctive form for both.

Tú affirmative imperative

The tú affirmative imperative is identical to the él/ella present tense form. Simply drop the subject pronoun.

Usted affirmative imperative (formal / medical)

The usted command uses the present subjunctive form: take the yo present tense → drop -o → add opposite vowel ending (-e for -ar verbs, -a for -er/-ir verbs).

Negative commands (tú and usted)

Both tú and usted negative commands use the subjunctive. For tú, add no before the subjunctive form (not the same as the affirmative).

  • No hables. / No hable usted. Don't speak. (informal / formal)
  • No comas eso. / No coma eso. Don't eat that.
  • No se mueva. Don't move. (usted, reflexive)

Medical and healthcare commands in context

  • Abra la boca, por favor. Open your mouth, please. (doctor to patient)
  • Respira hondo. Breathe deeply. (informal)
  • Descanse y tome mucha agua. Rest and drink plenty of water. (formal advice)
  • Tome este medicamento dos veces al día. Take this medication twice a day.
  • No se mueva, por favor. Please don't move.
  • Levante el brazo izquierdo. Raise your left arm.
  • No coma nada antes de la operación. Don't eat anything before the operation.
