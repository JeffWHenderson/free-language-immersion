Comparaciones — más/menos que, tan...como

Spanish comparisons follow consistent, predictable patterns. Once you know the three core structures, you can compare almost anything — jobs, hobbies, abilities, and qualities.

The three main structures

StructureMeaningExample
más + adjective/adverb + quemore ... thanmás interesante que
menos + adjective/adverb + queless ... thanmenos estresante que
tan + adjective/adverb + comoas ... astan creativo como

Superlatives — the most / the least

Add the definite article before más or menos: el/la/los/las más + adjective. The article agrees with the noun being described.

ExampleMeaning
el trabajo más interesantethe most interesting job
la hobby menos costosathe least expensive hobby
el mejor candidatothe best candidate

Irregular comparative forms

AdjectiveComparativeSuperlative
bueno (good)mejor (better)el/la mejor (the best)
malo (bad)peor (worse)el/la peor (the worst)
grande (big / old)mayor (older / greater)el/la mayor (the oldest)
pequeño (small / young)menor (younger / lesser)el/la menor (the youngest)

Examples with jobs and hobbies

  • Mi trabajo es más estresante que el tuyo. My job is more stressful than yours.
  • Correr es menos costoso que el gimnasio. Running is less expensive than the gym.
  • Ella es tan creativa como él. She is as creative as he is.
  • Este puesto es mejor que el anterior. This position is better than the previous one.
  • La fotografía es el pasatiempo más relajante. Photography is the most relaxing hobby.
  • Mi hermana es mayor que yo, pero gana menos. My sister is older than me, but she earns less.
  • Tocar la guitarra es tan difícil como aprender un idioma. Playing guitar is as difficult as learning a language.

Comparing quantities with nouns

When comparing amounts of things (nouns rather than adjectives), use más/menos de before numbers, and más/menos + noun + que otherwise.

  • Tengo más experiencia que él. I have more experience than him.
  • Gana más de cien mil al año. She earns more than a hundred thousand a year.
