Demonstratives — este, ese, aquel

Spanish demonstratives correspond to English "this," "that," and "that over there." They agree in gender and number with the noun they modify. There are also neuter forms (esto, eso, aquello) used when the gender of something is unknown or when referring to an idea.

Este / esta / esto — near the speaker

  • Este hotel es muy caro. This hotel is very expensive. (near me)
  • Esta calle es muy larga. This street is very long.
  • ¿Qué es esto? What is this? (neuter — gender unknown)

Ese / esa / eso — near the listener

  • Ese restaurante está lleno. That restaurant is full. (near you)
  • ¿Esa es la parada de autobús? Is that the bus stop?
  • Eso no me gusta. I don't like that. (neuter — referring to an idea)

Aquel / aquella / aquello — far from both

  • Aquel edificio es el museo. That building over there is the museum.
  • ¿Ves aquella torre? Do you see that tower over there?
  • Aquello fue un problema. That (thing over there / back then) was a problem.

Using demonstratives to point at places

  • Dobla en esa esquina. Turn at that corner.
  • El taxi está en este lado de la calle. The taxi is on this side of the street.
  • Aquella estación es la central. That station over there is the main one.
