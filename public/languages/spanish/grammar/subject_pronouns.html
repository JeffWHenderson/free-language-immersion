Spanish Subject Pronouns

Subject pronouns tell you who is performing the action. Spanish has a key difference from English: you can usually drop them — the verb ending already shows who the subject is.

Pronouns are usually dropped

The verb ending already encodes the subject, so pronouns are omitted in normal speech:

  • Hablo español. I speak Spanish. (yo is implied by -o)
  • ¿Hablas inglés? Do you speak English? (tú is implied by -as)
  • Somos amigos. We are friends. (nosotros is implied by -mos)

When to keep the pronoun

  • Contrast or emphasis: Yo no sé, pero tú sí sabes. I don't know, but you do.
  • Avoid ambiguity with él/ella/usted: Él trabaja; ella estudia. He works; she studies.

Formal vs. informal

Use usted with strangers, elders, or in professional settings. Use tú with friends, family, and peers.
