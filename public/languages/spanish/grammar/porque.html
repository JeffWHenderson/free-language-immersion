Porque / Por qué — Because & Why

Three forms sound similar but have completely different roles. Getting these right instantly improves your writing and comprehension.

The three forms at a glance

FormMeaning / roleExample
porquebecause (conjunction)Estoy feliz porque me ascendieron.
por quéwhy? (question word, two words)¿Por qué estás triste?
el porquéthe reason (noun)No entiendo el porqué de su enojo.

Porque — because (conjunction)

Porque introduces the reason. It links two clauses and is never capitalized mid-sentence.

  • Me siento mal porque estoy cansado. I feel bad because I'm tired.
  • Estoy nervioso porque tengo una reunión importante. I'm nervous because I have an important meeting.
  • Lloro porque estoy emocionado. I'm crying because I'm moved.

Por qué — why? (question word)

Two words, both carry an accent. Used in direct and indirect questions.

  • ¿Por qué estás triste? Why are you sad?
  • No sé por qué está enojado. I don't know why he's angry. (indirect question)
  • ¿Por qué no me llamaste? Why didn't you call me?
