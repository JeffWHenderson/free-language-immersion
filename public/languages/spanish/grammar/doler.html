Doler — To Hurt

Doler works exactly like gustar — the body part is the subject, and you are the indirect object. The verb agrees with the body part, not with you.

Structure

Indirect object pronoun + duele/duelen + body part

  • Me duele la cabeza. My head hurts. (lit: the head hurts to me)
  • Me duelen los pies. My feet hurt. (pies is plural → duelen)

Indirect object pronouns

PronounMeaning
me duele / duelenmy ___ hurts / hurt
te duele / duelenyour ___ hurts / hurt
le duele / duelenhis / her ___ hurts / hurt
nos duele / duelenour ___ hurts / hurt

Duele vs. duelen

UseExample
singular body partMe duele la cabeza / el estómago / la espalda.
plural body partMe duelen los pies / los ojos / las rodillas.

Useful questions

  • ¿Dónde te duele? Where does it hurt?
  • ¿Te duele mucho? Does it hurt a lot?
  • ¿Te duele algo? Does something hurt?
