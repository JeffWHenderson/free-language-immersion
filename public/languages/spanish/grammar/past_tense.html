The Spanish Past Tense (Preterite)

The preterite is used for completed past actions — things that happened once, at a specific time, or that are clearly finished.

Regular -AR verbs

Example: cocinar (to cook)

PersonEndingExample
yo-écociné (I cooked)
tú-astecocinaste (you cooked)
él/ella-ócocinó (he/she cooked)
nosotros-amoscocinamos (we cooked)
ellos-aroncocinaron (they cooked)

Regular -ER and -IR verbs

Example: comer (to eat) / beber (to drink)

PersonEnding-ER example-IR example
yo-ícomí (I ate)bebí (I drank)
tú-istecomiste (you ate)bebiste (you drank)
él/ella-iócomió (he/she ate)bebió (he/she drank)
nosotros-imoscomimos (we ate)bebimos (we drank)
ellos-ieroncomieron (they ate)bebieron (they drank)

Stem-changing verbs

Some -IR verbs change their stem in the third-person preterite. Servir (to serve) is common in food contexts:

PersonServir
yoserví
túserviste
él/ellasirvió (stem changes: e → i)
nosotrosservimos
ellossirvieron (stem changes: e → i)

Common phrases

  • Comí en un restaurante anoche. I ate at a restaurant last night.
  • ¿Qué bebiste? What did you drink?
  • Ella cocinó la cena. She cooked dinner.
  • Nosotros comimos juntos. We ate together.
  • El camarero nos sirvió rápido. The waiter served us quickly.

Time markers that signal the preterite

  • ayer — yesterday
  • anoche — last night
  • la semana pasada — last week
  • hace dos días — two days ago
