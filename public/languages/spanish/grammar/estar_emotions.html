Estar + Emotions

Spanish has two verbs for "to be": ser and estar. For emotions and temporary states, you always use estar.

Why estar?

Emotions are temporary — they can change from moment to moment. Estar is used for conditions that change over time. Ser is for permanent identity and characteristics.

Ser (permanent)

Soy alto. I am tall.

Ella es inteligente. She is intelligent.

Estar (temporary)

Estoy feliz. I am happy.

Ella está triste. She is sad.

Examples in sentences

  • Estoy muy feliz hoy. I am very happy today.
  • Él está nervioso por el examen. He is nervous about the exam.
  • Estamos muy cansados. We are very tired.
  • ¿Estás bien? Are you okay?
  • Ella está emocionada por el viaje. She is excited about the trip.

A useful tip

If you can imagine the emotion changing throughout the day, use estar. "You weren't angry this morning, but you are now" — that's temporary, so: estás enojado/a.
