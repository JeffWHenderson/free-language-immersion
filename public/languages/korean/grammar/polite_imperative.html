-(으)세요 — Polite Imperative

-(으)세요 is the standard polite way to make a request or give an instruction in Korean. Attach 세요 after vowel-ending stems, and 으세요 after consonant-ending stems. This form is also used as a polite honorific statement ("please do…"). It is much softer than the casual imperative -아/어라, which is only used with close friends or children.

Formation

Stem endingEndingExampleMeaning
Vowel (마시-)세요마시세요please drink
Vowel (기다리-)세요기다리세요please wait
Vowel (주문하-)세요주문하세요please order
Consonant (앉-)으세요앉으세요please sit down
Honorific eat (드-)세요드세요please eat (honorific)

주세요 — Please Give Me (Ordering)

주세요 (juseyo, from 주다 "to give") is the standard phrase for ordering food or requesting items in a restaurant.

KoreanRomanizationEnglish
물 주세요mul juseyoWater, please.
메뉴 주세요menyu juseyoThe menu, please.
이거 주세요igeo juseyoThis one, please.

Casual Contrast

The casual imperative -아/어라 (e.g., 먹어라, 가라) is used only in informal speech with close friends, younger people, or in written commands. Always use -(으)세요 in restaurants and public settings.

Example Sentences

  • 여기 앉으세요. yeogi anjeuseyo — Please sit here.
  • 천천히 드세요. cheoncheonhi deuseyo — Please eat slowly.
  • 잠깐 기다리세요. jamkkan gidariseyo — Please wait a moment.
  • 비빔밥 주세요. bibimbap juseyo — Bibimbap, please.
