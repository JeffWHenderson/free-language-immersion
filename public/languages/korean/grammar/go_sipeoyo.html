-고 싶어요 — Want To

-고 싶어요 expresses a desire to do something — "I want to (verb)." Attach it directly to the verb stem with no vowel harmony changes needed. This pattern is one of the most useful in everyday Korean. For wanting a noun (thing), use 원해요 or say the item is good with 이/가 좋아요.

Formation

VerbStem-고 싶어요 formMeaning
먹다먹-먹고 싶어요want to eat
마시다마시-마시고 싶어요want to drink
주문하다주문하-주문하고 싶어요want to order
가다가-가고 싶어요want to go
쉬다쉬-쉬고 싶어요want to rest

Negation — Don't Want To

Replace 싶어요 with 싶지 않아요 to say you don't want to do something.

KoreanRomanizationEnglish
먹고 싶지 않아요meokgo sipji anayodon't want to eat
마시고 싶지 않아요masigo sipji anayodon't want to drink

Wanting a Thing vs. Wanting to Do

KoreanRomanizationEnglish
커피를 원해요keopireul wonhaeyoI want a coffee (noun)
커피를 마시고 싶어요keopireul masigo sipeoyoI want to drink coffee (action)

3rd Person Note

-고 싶어요 is naturally used for "I want." For third person ("he/she wanted"), use -고 싶어했어요 (e.g., 먹고 싶어했어요 — he/she wanted to eat). This distinction keeps the speaker's own desire clear.

Example Sentences

  • 삼겹살을 먹고 싶어요. samgyeopsal-eul meokgo sipeoyo — I want to eat samgyeopsal.
  • 뭔가 달콤한 걸 먹고 싶어요. mwonga dalkomhan geol meokgo sipeoyo — I want to eat something sweet.
  • 오늘은 집에서 쉬고 싶어요. oneureun jibeseo swigo sipeoyo — Today I want to rest at home.
