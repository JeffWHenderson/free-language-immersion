시간 표현 — Time Words & Days

Korean time words are straightforward in meaning, but their position in the sentence follows Korean word order: time expressions come after the topic/subject and before the verb. Learning where to place them is as important as learning the words themselves.

Word order: In Korean, time words come after the topic/subject and before the verb.
Pattern: [Topic] + [Time] + [Place] + [Verb]
저는 오늘 학교에 가요. (Jeoneun oneul hakgyoe gayo.) — I go to school today.
Compare English: "I go to school today" vs. Korean word order: "I today school-to go."

Example sentences

  • 저는 오늘 바빠요. (Jeoneun oneul bappayo.) — I'm busy today.
  • 내일 뭐 해요? (Naeil mwo haeyo?) — What are you doing tomorrow?
  • 보통 아침에 커피를 마셔요. (Botong achime keopireul masyeoyo.) — I usually drink coffee in the morning.
  • 금요일에 만나요! (Geumyoire mannayo!) — See you on Friday!
