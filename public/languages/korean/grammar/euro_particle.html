-(으)로 — Direction & Means

The particle -(으)로 has two main uses: (1) indicating direction — the way you are heading or turning, and (2) indicating means or method — how you travel or do something. The rule is simple: use 로 after vowels and after stems ending in ㄹ; use 으로 after all other consonants.

Usage 1 — Direction

KoreanRomanizationEnglish
오른쪽으로 가세요oreunjjogeuro gaseyoGo to the right
왼쪽으로 도세요oenjjogeuro doseyoTurn to the left
이쪽으로 오세요ijjogeuro oseyoCome this way
서울로 가는 버스Seoul-lo ganeun beoseuA bus going to Seoul

Usage 2 — Means / Method of Transport

KoreanRomanizationEnglish
지하철로 가요jihacheolro gayogo by subway
버스로 왔어요beoseuro wasseoyocame by bus
택시로 갈 거예요taeksiro gal geoyeyowill go by taxi
걸어서 가요georeoeseo gayogo on foot (note: 서 not 로)

Attachment Rules

EndingParticleExample
Vowel (지하철)로지하철로
ㄹ (버스)로N/A — 버스 ends in vowel, 서울 ends in ㄹ → 서울로
Other consonant (오른쪽)으로오른쪽으로

Note on 걸어서

"On foot" uses 걸어서 (from 걷다, to walk), not 걸어로. This is because the method of walking is expressed as a sequential clause ("having walked"), not a direction particle. Remember this exception!

Example Sentences

  • 다음 모퉁이에서 왼쪽으로 도세요. daeum motungieseo oenjjogeuro doseyo — Turn left at the next corner.
  • 지하철로 학교에 가요. jihacheolro hakgyoe gayo — I go to school by subway.
  • 매일 걸어서 출근해요. maeil georeoeseo chulgeunhaeyo — I commute to work on foot every day.
