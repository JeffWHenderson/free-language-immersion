Descriptive Verbs as Adjectives

In Korean, adjectives don't exist as a separate category — instead, there are descriptive verbs (형용사, hyeongyongsa) that function like adjectives. They conjugate just like action verbs and can serve as both predicates ("The cat is cute") and noun modifiers ("the cute cat").

This is a key insight: in Korean, saying "The cat is cute" uses 귀엽다 (gwiyeopda) as a verb in the predicate position — not an adjective like in English.

Using descriptive verbs as predicates

Structure: [subject] + descriptive verb (아요/어요)

  • 고양이가 귀여워요. (Goyangi-ga gwiyeowoyo.) — The cat is cute.
  • 이 음식이 맛있어요. (I eumsigi massisseoyo.) — This food is delicious.
  • 날씨가 좋아요. (Nalssiga joayo.) — The weather is good.
  • 가방이 비싸요. (Gabangi bissayo.) — The bag is expensive.

Using descriptive verbs as noun modifiers

To modify a noun ("the cute cat," "a good book"), use the -(으)ㄴ form of the descriptive verb before the noun.

Dictionary formModifier form (stem ending)Example
귀엽다 → 귀여운consonant stem + 은귀여운 고양이 (cute cat)
크다 → 큰vowel/ㄹ stem + ㄴ큰 집 (big house)
작다 → 작은consonant stem + 은작은 강아지 (small puppy)
좋다 → 좋은consonant stem + 은좋은 날씨 (good weather)
비싸다 → 비싼아 stem: 아 → 아ㄴ비싼 가방 (expensive bag)

Examples of modifier forms in sentences

  • 귀여운 고양이가 있어요. (Gwiyeoun goyangi-ga isseoyo.) — There is a cute cat.
  • 좋은 책을 읽어요. (Joeun chaekeul ilgeoyo.) — I'm reading a good book.
  • 맛있는 음식을 먹었어요. (Massinneun eumsigul meogeosseoyo.) — I ate delicious food.
  • 큰 도시에 살아요. (Keun dosie sarayo.) — I live in a big city.

Negative descriptive verbs

Add 안 (an) before the descriptive verb, or use the ~지 않다 form.

  • 안 비싸요. (An bissayo.) — It's not expensive.
  • 좋지 않아요. (Jochi anayo.) — It's not good.

Common mistakes

  • Don't treat Korean adjectives like English adjectives — in Korean they must be conjugated. "Big house" as a full sentence is 집이 커요, not 집이 큰.
  • When modifying a noun, you must use the -(으)ㄴ modifier form, not the dictionary form: 좋은 책 (correct), 좋다 책 (wrong).
  • 맛있다 and 맛없다 are irregular in that they follow the action verb conjugation pattern in modifiers: 맛있는 (not 맛있은).
