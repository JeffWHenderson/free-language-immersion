-아야/어야 해요 — Have To / Must

-아야/어야 해요 expresses obligation — "have to" or "must." It follows the same vowel harmony as the basic polite ending: attach 아야 해요 when the stem's last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ, and 어야 해요 for all other vowels. The 하다 verb becomes 해야 해요. This pattern is very common in medical and health contexts.

Negation — Don't Have To

To say you don't have to do something, use 안 해도 돼요 or -지 않아도 돼요. These literally mean "it's okay even if you don't."

KoreanRomanizationEnglish
안 가도 돼요an gado dwaeyoyou don't have to go

Example Sentences (Medical & Health)

  • 병원에 가야 해요. byeongwone gaya haeyo — I have to go to the hospital.
  • 약을 먹어야 해요. yageul meogeoya haeyo — I must take medicine.
  • 물을 많이 마셔야 해요. mureul mani masyeoya haeyo — I must drink a lot of water.
  • 충분히 쉬어야 해요. chungbunhi swieoaya haeyo — I must rest sufficiently.
  • 일찍 자야 해요. iljjik jaya haeyo — I must go to bed early.
