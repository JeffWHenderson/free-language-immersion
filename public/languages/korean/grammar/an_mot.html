안 vs 못 — Negation

Korean has two main short-form negation words: 안 (an) and 못 (mot). Both are placed directly before the verb, but they carry different meanings.

WordMeaningUse when
안 (an)don't / doesn't (choice / habit)You choose not to, or it's simply not the case
못 (mot)can't (inability)You are unable to — due to skill, situation, or circumstance

Placement: before the verb

Both 안 and 못 go directly before the verb (or before 하다 in compound verbs).

Positive안 (choice)못 (inability)
가요 (go)안 가요 (don't go)못 가요 (can't go)
먹어요 (eat)안 먹어요 (don't eat)못 먹어요 (can't eat)
해요 (do)안 해요 (don't do)못 해요 (can't do)
마셔요 (drink)안 마셔요 (don't drink)못 마셔요 (can't drink)

With 하다 compound verbs

When the verb is a noun + 하다 compound, 안 and 못 go between the noun and 하다.

  • 공부 안 해요. (Gongbu an haeyo.) — I don't study. (choice)
  • 공부 못 해요. (Gongbu mot haeyo.) — I can't study. (inability)
  • 요리 안 해요. (Yori an haeyo.) — I don't cook.
  • 요리 못 해요. (Yori mot haeyo.) — I can't cook.

Examples

  • 저는 고기를 안 먹어요. (Jeoneun gogireul an meogeoyo.) — I don't eat meat. (I choose not to — perhaps vegetarian)
  • 저는 고기를 못 먹어요. (Jeoneun gogireul mot meogeoyo.) — I can't eat meat. (perhaps an allergy or medical reason)
  • 오늘은 못 가요. 약속이 있어요. (Oneureun mot gayo. Yaksogi isseoyo.) — I can't go today. I have an appointment.
  • 저는 술을 안 마셔요. (Jeoneun sureul an masyeoyo.) — I don't drink alcohol. (by choice)
  • 한국어를 못 해요. 아직 배우고 있어요. (Hangugeoreul mot haeyo. Ajik baeugo isseoyo.) — I can't speak Korean (well). I'm still learning.

Long-form negation with 지 않다 and 지 못하다

There are also long-form equivalents that are slightly more formal or emphatic.

Short formLong formMeaning
안 가요가지 않아요 (gaji anayo)don't go
못 가요가지 못해요 (gaji mothaeyo)can't go

Common mistakes

  • Using 못 when you mean "don't" (choice) — this changes the meaning significantly. 고기를 못 먹어요 implies inability, not preference.
  • Using 안 with descriptive (adjective) verbs for basic negation — this is fine (안 좋아요 = not good), but 못 cannot be used with pure descriptive verbs.
  • With 하다 compounds, putting 안/못 before the whole verb instead of splitting it: 안 공부해요 vs. 공부 안 해요. Both are technically used, but the split form (noun + 안/못 + 해요) is more standard.
