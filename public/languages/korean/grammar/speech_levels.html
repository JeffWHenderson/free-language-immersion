Speech Levels — Formal, Polite & Casual

Korean has a built-in system of speech levels that reflect the relationship between speaker and listener. The three most important registers in everyday life are: 합쇼체 (formal), 해요체 (polite), and 해체 (casual / banmal). Choosing the wrong level can seem rude or overly stiff, so understanding context is essential.

The Three Main Registers

RegisterNameEndingWhen to use
Formal합쇼체 (hapssyoche)-습니다 / -ㅂ니다Presentations, announcements, first meetings, job interviews, service staff to customers
Polite해요체 (haeyoche)-아요 / -어요Everyday interactions with strangers, acquaintances, older people, or anyone you're not close to
Casual해체 (haechae) / 반말 (banmal)-아 / -어Close friends, younger siblings, children; never with elders or superiors unless invited

Same Sentence Across All Three Registers

MeaningFormal (합쇼체)Polite (해요체)Casual (해체)
I work here. 여기서 일합니다. 여기서 일해요. 여기서 일해.
I understand. 이해합니다. 이해해요. 이해해.
It's good. 좋습니다. 좋아요. 좋아.
I'm going now. 지금 갑니다. 지금 가요. 지금 가.

Formal Register — 합쇼체 Examples

  • 안녕하십니까? annyeonghashimnikka — How do you do? (formal greeting)
  • 잘 부탁드립니다. jal butakdeurimnida — I look forward to working with you. (formal first meeting)
  • 감사합니다. gamsahamnida — Thank you. (formal)

Polite Register — 해요체 Examples

  • 안녕하세요! annyeonghaseyo — Hello! (polite, all-purpose greeting)
  • 뭐 드실래요? mwo deusillaeyo — What would you like to eat? (polite offer)
  • 잘 지내요? jal jinaeyo — How are you? (polite)

Casual Register — 해체 / 반말 Examples

  • 안녕! annyeong — Hey! / Hi! (casual)
  • 뭐 먹을래? mwo meogeullae — What do you want to eat? (casual)
  • 나 피곤해. na pigonhae — I'm tired. (casual)

Social Context Note

SituationRecommended level
Job interview, business presentation합쇼체 (formal)
Talking to a neighbor, shopkeeper, or new acquaintance해요체 (polite)
Chatting with a close friend of the same age해체 / 반말 (casual)
Talking to a child or younger sibling해체 / 반말 (casual)
Speaking to an elder or senior colleague해요체 or 합쇼체 (never banmal)
