할 수 있어요 — Can / Able To

To express ability or possibility in Korean — "can," "am able to" — use the pattern: verb stem + (으)ㄹ 수 있어요. The negative "cannot" is: verb stem + (으)ㄹ 수 없어요.

수 (su) means "way" or "means," so the literal sense is "there is a way to [verb]" for can, and "there is no way to [verb]" for cannot.

MeaningPatternExample
Can / able tostem + (으)ㄹ 수 있어요갈 수 있어요 (gal su isseoyo) — can go
Cannot / unable tostem + (으)ㄹ 수 없어요갈 수 없어요 (gal su eopseoyo) — cannot go

How to form (으)ㄹ

Look at the final sound of the verb stem (after dropping 다).

Stem ends inAddExampleResult
vowelㄹ가 (go)갈 수 있어요
consonant을먹 (eat)먹을 수 있어요
ㄹ alreadyㄹ (no change)알 (know)알 수 있어요

Examples

  • 한국어를 말할 수 있어요. (Hangugeoreul malhal su isseoyo.) — I can speak Korean.
  • 내일 올 수 있어요? (Naeil ol su isseoyo?) — Can you come tomorrow?
  • 죄송해요, 갈 수 없어요. (Joesonghaeyo, gal su eopseoyo.) — Sorry, I can't go.
  • 도와드릴 수 있어요. (Dowadeuril su isseoyo.) — I can help you.
  • 이것을 먹을 수 있어요? (Igeoseul meogeul su isseoyo?) — Can I eat this?
  • 여기서 수영할 수 있어요? (Yeogiseo suyeonghal su isseoyo?) — Can you swim here?
  • 시간이 없어서 못 가요. (Sigani eopseoseo mot gayo.) — I can't go because I don't have time. [못 = inability, see 안/못 lesson]

할 수 없어요 vs 못 해요

Both express "cannot," but with a different feel. (으)ㄹ 수 없어요 is more formal and deliberate ("it is impossible for me to..."). 못 + verb is more casual and immediate ("I just can't right now"). Both are correct and natural.

Common mistakes

  • Don't forget the space: 수 is a separate word — write 갈 수 있어요, not 갈수있어요.
  • The pattern is (으)ㄹ 수 있어요, not (으)ㄹ 수 있다요. The 있다 stays in its full polite 어요 form.
  • Don't confuse 있어요 (have/exist) with its use here as part of the can-pattern — in 갈 수 있어요, 있어요 doesn't mean "have," it means the possibility exists.
