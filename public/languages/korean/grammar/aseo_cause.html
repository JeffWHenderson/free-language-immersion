-아서/어서 — Because / So

-아서/어서 connects two clauses to show cause and result, or a natural sequence of actions. It means "because" or "and so / and then." The same vowel harmony applies: use 아서 when the stem's last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ, and 어서 for all others. 하다 verbs become 해서.

Key rule: The -아서/어서 clause cannot carry its own tense marker. Tense is only shown on the final verb of the sentence.

Formation

Verb-아서/어서 formMeaning (as connector)
아프다 (to be sick)아파서because (I am) sick
피곤하다 (to be tired)피곤해서because (I am) tired
열이 있다 (to have a fever)열이 있어서because (I) have a fever
씻다 (to wash)씻어서after washing / and then
배고프다 (to be hungry)배고파서because (I am) hungry

Causal Use (Because → So)

  • 아파서 못 가요. apaseo mot gayo — Because I'm sick, I can't go.
  • 피곤해서 일찍 잤어요. pigonhaeseo iljjik jasseoyo — I was tired so I went to bed early.
  • 열이 있어서 쉬었어요. yeori isseoseo swiosseoyo — I had a fever so I rested.

Sequential Use (And Then)

  • 씻어서 잠들었어요. ssiseoseo jamdeureosseoyo — I washed up and fell asleep.
  • 약을 먹어서 나았어요. yageul meogeoseo naasseoyo — I took medicine and got better.
