은/는 vs 이/가 — Topic vs Subject

One of the first and most important distinctions in Korean grammar is between the topic marker (은/는) and the subject marker (이/가). Both attach directly to nouns, but they carry different meanings and feel different to Korean ears.

은/는 (topic marker) sets the stage — it says "as for X, ..." or "speaking of X, ...". It often introduces something the speaker wants to talk about, contrast, or emphasize as a known entity.

이/가 (subject marker) identifies the grammatical subject performing the action or having the quality described. It often introduces new information or focuses attention on who is doing something.

Topic은 (eun)는 (neun)Sets topic; contrast; known info
Subject이 (i)가 (ga)Identifies subject; new info; focus

How to choose: consonant or vowel?

Look at the final sound of the noun. If it ends in a consonant, use 은 or 이. If it ends in a vowel, use 는 or 가.

Examples

  • 저는 학생이에요. (Jeoneun haksaengieyo.) — I am a student. [topic: I — setting the stage about myself]
  • 고양이가 귀여워요. (Goyangi-ga gwiyeowoyo.) — The cat is cute. [subject: cat — new focus, cat is doing/being something]
  • 저는 커피를 좋아해요. 그는 차를 좋아해요. (Jeoneun keopireul joahaeyo. Geuneun chareul joahaeyo.) — I like coffee. He likes tea. [는 used for contrast between I and he]
  • 누가 왔어요? 민준이가 왔어요. (Nuga wasseoyo? Minjuni-ga wasseoyo.) — Who came? Minjun came. [이/가 answers "who" — new information]
  • 한국어는 어려워요. (Hangugeoneun eoryeowoyo.) — (As for) Korean, it's difficult. [은 sets Korean as the topic of discussion]

Topic vs. Subject: the key difference

SentenceMarkerNuance
제가 했어요. (Jega haesseoyo.)이/가"I (specifically) did it." — emphasis on who
저는 했어요. (Jeoneun haesseoyo.)은/는"As for me, I did it." — topic, or possible contrast

Common mistakes

  • Don't mix up 은/는 and 이/가 — they are not interchangeable. Using 이/가 where 은/는 is expected can make a sentence sound oddly emphatic or like you're introducing new information.
  • When answering "who?" questions, always use 이/가 (the answer is new information).
  • When talking about yourself in general statements, 저는/나는 (은/는) is the most natural choice.
  • Note: 제 (je) is the possessive/subject form of 저 — when using 이/가, 저 becomes 제가, not 저가.
