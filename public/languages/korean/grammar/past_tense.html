Past Tense — 았어요/었어요

The past tense in Korean is formed by adding 았어요 or 었어요 to the verb stem. Use 았어요 when the stem's last vowel is ㅏ or ㅗ; use 었어요 for all other vowels. Verbs ending in 하다 become 했어요. These contracted forms are the standard polite past tense used in everyday speech.

Formation Rules

RuleExample stemPast tenseMeaning
Last vowel ㅏ/ㅗ → 았어요가- (가다)갔어요went
Last vowel ㅏ/ㅗ → 았어요오- (오다)왔어요came
Other vowels → 었어요먹- (먹다)먹었어요ate
Other vowels → 었어요보- (보다)봤어요saw
Other vowels → 었어요있- (있다)있었어요was/had
하다 → 했어요공부하- (공부하다)공부했어요studied

Negation in the Past Tense

Add 안 before the verb for "did not," or 못 for "could not." The ending 었어요/았어요 stays on the main verb.

KoreanRomanizationEnglish
안 먹었어요an meogeosseoyodid not eat
못 갔어요mot gasseoyocould not go
안 했어요an haesseoyodid not do

Example Sentences

  • 어제 밥을 먹었어요. eoje babeul meogeosseoyo — I ate rice yesterday.
  • 친구를 만났어요. chingureul mannasseoyo — I met a friend.
  • 오늘 학교에 안 갔어요. oneul hakgyoe an gasseoyo — I didn't go to school today.
  • 숙제를 했어요. sukjereul haesseoyo — I did my homework.
  • 영화를 봤어요. yeonghwareul bwasseoyo — I watched a movie.
