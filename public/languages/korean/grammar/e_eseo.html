에 vs 에서 — Location Particles

Korean uses two different particles to express location: 에 (e) and 에서 (eseo). This distinction trips up many learners because English uses "at," "in," or "on" for both situations. The key is whether you are describing where something exists or where an action takes place.

ParticleCore meaningUse for
에 (e)at / in / toStatic location (existence); direction of movement
에서 (eseo)at / in / fromLocation of an action; point of departure

에 (e) — static location and direction

Use 에 when talking about where something or someone is (no active verb), or when indicating the destination of movement.

  • 저는 집에 있어요. (Jeoneun jibe isseoyo.) — I am at home. [existence]
  • 학교에 가요. (Hakgyoe gayo.) — I go to school. [direction]
  • 책이 테이블 위에 있어요. (Chaegi teibeul wie isseoyo.) — The book is on the table. [existence]
  • 병원에 가야 해요. (Byeongwone gaya haeyo.) — I have to go to the hospital. [direction]

에서 (eseo) — location of action and departure

Use 에서 when an active verb describes what happens at that place, or when indicating where you came from.

  • 카페에서 공부해요. (Kapeeseo gongbuhaeyo.) — I study at the café. [action at place]
  • 도서관에서 책을 읽어요. (Doseogwaneseo chaekeul ilgeoyo.) — I read books at the library. [action]
  • 어디서 왔어요? (Eodiseo wasseoyo?) — Where did you come from? [departure]
  • 미국에서 왔어요. (Migugeseo wasseoyo.) — I came from America. [departure]

Common mistakes

  • The most common error is using 에 where 에서 is needed: 카페에 공부해요 is wrong — you're doing something (studying), so 카페에서 공부해요 is correct.
  • With 있어요 (to be/exist), always use 에, never 에서: 집에 있어요 (correct), 집에서 있어요 (incorrect).
  • With verbs of movement like 가다 (go), 오다 (come), 다니다 (attend), use 에 for destination: 학교에 가요.
