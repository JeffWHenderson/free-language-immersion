-(으)ㄴ/는 편이에요 — Tends To / Rather

-(으)ㄴ/는 편이에요 is used to soften a statement — it means "I tend to," "I'm rather," or "I'm on the [adjective] side." Instead of making a blunt claim, Korean speakers use this pattern to hedge, which is culturally valued. It is very common in conversations about personality, habits, and work style.

Formation Rules

Stem typeEndingExampleMeaning
Action verb (present)는 편이에요잘 하는 편이에요I tend to do well
Descriptive verb / adj (vowel)ㄴ 편이에요바쁜 편이에요I'm on the busy side
Descriptive verb / adj (consonant)은 편이에요조용한 편이에요I'm rather quiet
Past tense-(으)ㄴ 편이었어요바빴던 편이에요I tended to be busy

Common Examples

KoreanRomanizationEnglish
바쁜 편이에요bappeun pyeonieyoI'm on the busy side / rather busy
조용한 편이에요joyonghan pyeonieyoI'm rather quiet
잘 하는 편이에요jal haneun pyeonieyoI tend to do well
열심히 하는 편이에요yeolsimhi haneun pyeonieyoI tend to work hard
느린 편이에요neurín pyeonieyoI'm on the slow side

Why Use This Pattern?

Saying 바빠요 ("I'm busy") is direct and can sound blunt. Saying 바쁜 편이에요 softens it to "I'm on the busy side" — more modest and socially comfortable. Use it whenever you want to describe yourself or others without sounding too absolute.

Example Sentences

  • 저는 내성적인 편이에요. jeoneun naeseongjeog-in pyeonieyo — I tend to be introverted.
  • 우리 팀은 일을 빨리 하는 편이에요. uri timeun ireul ppalli haneun pyeonieyo — Our team tends to work quickly.
  • 월급이 적은 편이에요. wolgeubi jeogeun pyeonieyo — The salary is on the low side.
