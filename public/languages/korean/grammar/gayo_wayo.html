가요 & 와요 — Go & Come

가요 (gayo) means "go" and 와요 (wayo) means "come." These are the polite present-tense forms of 가다 (gada) and 오다 (oda). Like in English, the choice between "go" and "come" depends on the perspective and direction of movement relative to the speaker and listener.

Dictionary formPolite presentMeaningDirection
가다 (gada)가요 (gayo)goAway from speaker
오다 (oda)와요 (wayo)comeToward speaker or listener

Using 에 with direction verbs

The destination of 가다 and 오다 is marked with 에 (e).

  • 학교에 가요. (Hakgyoe gayo.) — I go to school.
  • 집에 와요. (Jibe wayo.) — (Someone) comes home.
  • 여기에 와요. (Yeogie wayo.) — Come here.

Common conjugations

Tense가다 (go)오다 (come)

Examples

  • 지금 어디 가요? (Jigeum eodi gayo?) — Where are you going now?
  • 슈퍼마켓에 가요. (Syupeomakete gayo.) — I'm going to the supermarket.
  • 언제 와요? (Eonje wayo?) — When are you coming?
  • 내일 와요. (Naeil wayo.) — I'm coming tomorrow. / Come tomorrow.
  • 어디서 왔어요? (Eodiseo wasseoyo?) — Where did you come from?
  • 미국에서 왔어요. (Migugeseo wasseoyo.) — I came from America.
  • 같이 가요! (Gachi gayo!) — Let's go together!

go vs. come: perspective matters

In Korean, just as in English, "come" implies movement toward the speaker's current location. If someone calls you and says "와요" (come), they mean come to where they are. If you say "갈게요" (galkkeyo — I'll go), you mean you'll move away from where you currently are toward them.

  • 지금 가고 있어요. (Jigeum gago isseoyo.) — I'm on my way (going now). [said by person traveling]
  • 빨리 와요! (Ppalli wayo!) — Come quickly! [said by person waiting]

Common mistakes

  • Don't use 와요 when you are moving away from where you currently are — use 가요.
  • The past tense of 오다 is 왔어요 (wasseoyo), not 왔요. The full ending 어요 is needed.
  • Remember: destination gets 에, point of origin gets 에서: 학교에 가요 / 학교에서 와요.
