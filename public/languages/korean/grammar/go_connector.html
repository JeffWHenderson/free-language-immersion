-고 — And / Then (Connector)

-고 is one of the most versatile connectors in Korean. It joins two clauses or lists two actions/states, equivalent to "and" in English. Unlike -아서/어서, the -고 connector allows tense to appear on each clause independently, or just on the final verb. It can show simultaneous states or sequential actions.

Uses of -고

UseExampleRomanizationMeaning
Listing states크고 예뻐요keugo yeppeoyobig and pretty
Listing actions일하고 공부해요ilhago gongbuhaeyowork and study
Sequential actions아침을 먹고 출근해요achimeul meokgo chulgeunhaeyoeat breakfast then go to work
After doing (-고 나서)일하고 나서 운동해요ilhago naseo undong haeyoexercise after working

-고 나서 — After Doing

-고 나서 makes the sequence more explicit: "after having done X, then Y." It is commonly used in work and daily routine contexts.

  • 회의하고 나서 점심을 먹었어요. hoeuihago naseo jeomsimeul meogeosseoyo — After the meeting, I ate lunch.
  • 운동하고 나서 샤워해요. undonghago naseo syawohaeyo — I shower after exercising.

Contrast with -아서/어서

-고-아서/어서
Tense can appear on each sideTense only on final verb
Simple listing or sequenceCause-and-result or tight sequence
먹고 잤어요 (ate and slept)먹어서 졸려요 (ate so I'm sleepy)

Example Sentences

  • 저는 요리사이고 그는 웨이터예요. jeoneun yorisaigo geuneun weiteoye yo — I'm a cook and he's a waiter.
  • 노래하고 춤을 춰요. noraehago chumeul chweoyo — I sing and dance.
  • 퇴근하고 나서 독서해요. toegeunhago naseo dokseoyo — I read after finishing work.
