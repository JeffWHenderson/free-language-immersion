입니다 vs 이에요/예요 — To Be

Korean has two main ways to say "is/am/are" (the copula "to be"): 입니다 (imnida) and 이에요/예요 (ieyo/yeyo). Both mean the same thing, but they differ in formality level.

FormFormalityUse
입니다 (imnida)Formal politePresentations, news, official contexts, first meetings
이에요 (ieyo)Informal politeAfter consonant: 학생이에요
예요 (yeyo)Informal politeAfter vowel: 의사예요

How to attach these endings

Both 입니다 and 이에요/예요 attach to nouns. The rule for 이에요 vs 예요 is simple: look at the last syllable of the noun.

NounEnds inInformal politeFormal polite
학생 (haksaeng, student)consonant (ng)학생이에요학생입니다
의사 (uisa, doctor)vowel (a)의사예요의사입니다
선생님 (seonsaengnim, teacher)consonant (m)선생님이에요선생님입니다
커피 (keopi, coffee)vowel (i)커피예요커피입니다

Negative forms

To say "is not," use 이/가 아니에요 (informal polite) or 이/가 아닙니다 (formal polite).

MeaningInformal politeFormal polite
Is not a student학생이 아니에요학생이 아닙니다
Is not a doctor의사가 아니에요의사가 아닙니다

Question forms

To make a question, simply raise your intonation (in speech) or add a question mark. The verb form stays the same.

  • 학생이에요? (Haksaengieyo?) — Are you a student?
  • 학생입니까? (Haksaengiimnikka?) — Are you a student? [formal question form]

Examples

  • 저는 학생이에요. (Jeoneun haksaengieyo.) — I am a student.
  • 저는 의사예요. (Jeoneun uisayeyo.) — I am a doctor.
  • 저는 학생입니다. (Jeoneun haksaengimnida.) — I am a student. [formal]
  • 이것은 책이에요. (Igeoseun chaegieyo.) — This is a book.
  • 저는 학생이 아니에요. 직장인이에요. (Jeoneun haksaengi anieyo. Jikjanginieyo.) — I'm not a student. I'm an office worker.
  • 그녀는 한국 사람이에요. (Geunyeoneun hanguk saramieyo.) — She is Korean.
  • 이게 뭐예요? (Ige mwoyeyo?) — What is this? [뭐 ends in vowel, so 예요]

Common mistakes

  • Don't use 이에요 after a vowel — it should be 예요. So 의사이에요 is wrong; the correct form is 의사예요.
  • 입니다 does not change based on consonant/vowel endings — it always stays as 입니다.
  • Remember: these copula forms only attach to nouns. For adjectives and verbs, use a different ending (아요/어요 form).
  • In very casual speech among close friends, 이야/야 is used (이야 after consonant, 야 after vowel): 학생이야, 의사야.
