-지만 — But / Although

-지만 is the most common contrast connector in Korean, meaning "but" or "although." Attach it directly to the verb or adjective stem without any vowel harmony changes. It connects two clauses that are in contrast with each other. Two related connectors are 그런데 (by the way / however — a softer sentence-level transition) and 그래도 (even so / still).

Formation

Stem+ 지만Meaning
피곤하-피곤하지만tired but…
어렵-어렵지만difficult but…
슬프-슬프지만sad but…
행복하-행복하지만happy but…

-지만 in Sentences

  • 피곤하지만 행복해요. pigonhajiman haengbokhaeyo — I'm tired but happy.
  • 어렵지만 재미있어요. eolyeopjiman jaemiisseoyo — It's hard but interesting.
  • 슬프지만 괜찮아요. seulpeujiman gwaenchanayo — I'm sad but I'm okay.
  • 무섭지만 해볼 거예요. museopjiman haeboel geoyeyo — I'm scared but I'll try.

그런데 & 그래도

KoreanRomanizationMeaning & Use
그런데geureonde"However / by the way" — softer topic shift between sentences
그래도geraedo"Even so / still" — conceding a point but continuing

Contrast: -지만 vs 그런데 vs 그래도

  • 힘들지만 계속할게요. himdeoljiman gyesokal geoyeyo — It's tough but I'll keep going. (-지만 within one sentence)
  • 힘들어요. 그런데 포기하지 않을 거예요. himdeoreoyo. Geureonde pogihaji aneul geoyeyo — It's tough. However, I won't give up. (그런데 between sentences)
  • 힘들어요. 그래도 할 거예요. himdeoreoyo. Geraedo hal geoyeyo — It's tough. Even so, I'll do it. (그래도 — strong resolution)
