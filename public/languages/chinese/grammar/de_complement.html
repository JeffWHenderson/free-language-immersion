得 — Degree Complement

The degree complement uses 得 (de) after a verb to describe how well, how intensely, or to what degree an action is performed. The structure is: Verb + 得 + degree description. This is the same 得 used in potential complements (V得了, V不了), but here it introduces a descriptive phrase.

Structure: Verb + 得 + Description

ChinesePinyinEnglish
睡得好shuì de hǎosleep well
走得很慢zǒu de hěn mànwalk very slowly
说得很流利shuō de hěn liúlìspeak very fluently
恢复得很快huīfù de hěn kuàirecover very quickly
吃得很少chī de hěn shǎoeat very little
呼吸得很顺畅hūxī de hěn shùnchàngbreathe very smoothly

Negation: V + 得 + 不 + description

To negate, place 不 before the description after 得.

  • 昨晚睡得不好。 Zuówǎn shuì de bù hǎo. — I slept badly last night.
  • 他走得不快。 Tā zǒu de bù kuài. — He doesn't walk fast.
  • 她吃得不多。 Tā chī de bù duō. — She doesn't eat much.

Questions with 怎么样

To ask "how well" an action was done, use V + 得 + 怎么样.

  • 你睡得怎么样？ Nǐ shuì de zěnmeyàng? — How did you sleep?
  • 他恢复得怎么样？ Tā huīfù de zěnmeyàng? — How is his recovery going?
  • 你说中文说得怎么样？ Nǐ shuō zhōngwén shuō de zěnmeyàng? — How well do you speak Chinese?
