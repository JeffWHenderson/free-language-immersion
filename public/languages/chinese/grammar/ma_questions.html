吗 (ma) — Yes/No Questions

The simplest way to form a yes/no question in Chinese is to add 吗 to the end of any statement. The word order does not change.

Structure

Statement + 吗？

  • 你好。 You are well. (statement)
  • 你好吗？ Are you well? (question)
  • 你去。 You are going.
  • 你去吗？ Are you going?

Answering yes/no questions

YesNo
是 / 对 / 是的不是 / 不
Yes / Correct / Yes, that's rightNo / Not right

Alternative questions — A 还是 B?

For "A or B?" questions, use 还是 (háishì) — don't add 吗:

  • 你喝茶还是咖啡？ Do you want tea or coffee?
  • 你去还是不去？ Are you going or not?

Have-you-eaten? — a cultural note

你吃了吗？ literally means "Have you eaten?" but functions as a common greeting, like "How are you?"
