从...到... — From...To...

从 (cóng) means "from" and 到 (dào) means "to" or "until." Together they form a versatile pattern used for both place (from location A to location B) and time (from time A to time B). The pattern always precedes the main verb in the sentence.

Place: From Location to Location

ChinesePinyinEnglish
从这里到地铁站怎么走？Cóng zhèlǐ dào dìtiě zhàn zěnme zǒu?How do I get from here to the subway station?
从北京到上海要多长时间？Cóng Běijīng dào Shànghǎi yào duō cháng shíjiān?How long does it take from Beijing to Shanghai?
从酒店到机场不远。Cóng jiǔdiàn dào jīchǎng bù yuǎn.It's not far from the hotel to the airport.
从公园到图书馆走路要十分钟。Cóng gōngyuán dào túshūguǎn zǒulù yào shí fēnzhōng.It takes ten minutes to walk from the park to the library.

Time: From Time to Time

ChinesePinyinEnglish
从早上到晚上cóng zǎoshang dào wǎnshangfrom morning to evening
商店从九点开到九点。Shāngdiàn cóng jiǔ diǎn kāi dào jiǔ diǎn.The shop is open from nine to nine.
他从星期一工作到星期六。Tā cóng xīngqīyī gōngzuò dào xīngqīliù.He works from Monday to Saturday.

到 Alone: Arrival

到 can also be used alone, as a verb meaning "to arrive at" or as a result complement meaning the destination was reached.

  • 到了！ Dào le! — We've arrived! / Here we are!
  • 你到了吗？ Nǐ dào le ma? — Have you arrived?
  • 我走到车站了。 Wǒ zǒu dào chēzhàn le. — I walked to the station.
