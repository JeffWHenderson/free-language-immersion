Word Patterns — Spotting How Chinese Works

Chinese builds new words by attaching small, predictable pieces to existing ones. Learn a handful of these patterns and you'll be able to guess the meaning of hundreds of words you've never seen before.

1. 化 (huà) — Turning Nouns into "to become X" Verbs

Add 化 to a noun or adjective and you get a verb meaning to become X or to make X. It works just like English -ize or -ify.

Base word+ 化Meaning
现代 (xiàndài) modern现代化 (xiàndàihuà)modernize / modernization
国际 (guójì) international国际化 (guójìhuà)internationalize
自动 (zìdòng) automatic自动化 (zìdònghuà)automate / automation
美 (měi) beautiful美化 (měihuà)beautify
简单 (jiǎndān) simple简单化 (jiǎndānhuà)simplify
  • 我们需要现代化这个系统。(Wǒmen xūyào xiàndàihuà zhège xìtǒng.) We need to modernize this system.
  • 工厂正在自动化。(Gōngchǎng zhèngzài zìdònghuà.) The factory is automating.
化 is a very productive suffix — if you can name a concept, you can probably stick 化 on it to say "make it that way."

2. 者 (zhě) — Person Who Does X

Add 者 after a verb or concept and you get a person who does or embodies that thing. Think of it as Chinese -er or -ist.

Base word+ 者Meaning
作 (zuò) create/write作者 (zuòzhě)author
学 (xué) learn/study学者 (xuézhě)scholar
读 (dú) read读者 (dúzhě)reader
记 (jì) record/note记者 (jìzhě)journalist / reporter
工作 (gōngzuò) work工作者 (gōngzuòzhě)worker
  • 这本书的作者是谁？(Zhè běn shū de zuòzhě shì shéi?) Who is the author of this book?
  • 记者在采访。(Jìzhě zài cǎifǎng.) The reporter is doing an interview.
者 often appears in formal or written Chinese. Its everyday counterpart is 人 (rén) — e.g., 工人 (gōngrén, worker) vs. 工作者 (gōngzuòzhě, worker — more formal).

3. 性 (xìng) — Abstract Quality or Nature

Add 性 to an adjective or concept to create an abstract noun — the quality or degree of something. Like English -ity or -ness.

Base word+ 性Meaning
可能 (kěnéng) possible可能性 (kěnéngxìng)possibility
重要 (zhòngyào) important重要性 (zhòngyàoxìng)importance
安全 (ānquán) safe安全性 (ānquánxìng)safety
创造 (chuàngzào) creative/create创造性 (chuàngzàoxìng)creativity
灵活 (línghuó) flexible灵活性 (línghuóxìng)flexibility
  • 这件事的重要性不能忽视。(Zhè jiàn shì de zhòngyàoxìng bù néng hūshì.) The importance of this matter cannot be ignored.
  • 还有一定的可能性。(Hái yǒu yīdìng de kěnéngxìng.) There is still a certain possibility.
性 is more formal than everyday speech. You're more likely to see it in writing, news, and academic contexts — but it's everywhere once you start looking.

4. 不 / 非 / 无 — Negative Prefixes

Chinese has three main negative prefixes. They overlap but each has its own flavor:

不 (bù) — "not / un-"

The most common negator. Directly contradicts the base word.

  • 可能 (kěnéng) → 不可能 (bù kěnéng) impossible
  • 正常 (zhèngcháng) → 不正常 (bù zhèngcháng) abnormal
  • 方便 (fāngbiàn) → 不方便 (bù fāngbiàn) inconvenient
非 (fēi) — "non- / un-" (formal)

More formal; often implies a category violation — something that falls outside the norm.

  • 正式 (zhèngshì) → 非正式 (fēi zhèngshì) informal / unofficial
  • 正常 (zhèngcháng) → 非正常 (fēi zhèngcháng) abnormal
  • 法 (fǎ) → 非法 (fēifǎ) illegal
无 (wú) — "without / -less" (formal/literary)

Means "lacking" or "without." Common in set phrases and formal writing.

  • 限制 (xiànzhì) → 无限制 (wú xiànzhì) unlimited
  • 条件 (tiáojiàn) → 无条件 (wú tiáojiàn) unconditional
  • 聊 (liáo) → 无聊 (wúliáo) boring / pointless (nothing to chat about)
PrefixRegisterNuance
不EverydayDirect contradiction — "not X"
非FormalCategory negation — "non-X, outside of X"
无Formal / literaryAbsence — "without X, X-less"

5. Reduplication — Softening and Emphasis

Repeating a word or syllable is a built-in feature of Chinese grammar. The effect depends on the word type.

Adjective AA — vivid, gentle feeling

Repeating an adjective makes it more vivid or gentle, often used with 的 or 地.

  • 红红的 (hónghóng de) reddish / vividly red
  • 慢慢地 (màn màn de) slowly, leisurely (take your time)
Compare: 红 (hóng) = red; 红红的 (hónghóng de) = a warm, vivid red — like describing autumn leaves or a child's cheeks.

Verb ABAB — casual, brief action

Repeating a two-syllable verb makes the action feel casual, light, or brief — "do it a little."

  • 学习学习 (xuéxí xuéxí) study a bit / have a little study session
  • 讨论讨论 (tǎolùn tǎolùn) have a little discussion / talk it over briefly

Measure word — distributive "one by one"

Combine 一 + measure + measure to mean "one by one" or "day by day."

  • 一个个 (yī gè gè) one by one
  • 一天天 (yī tiān tiān) day by day
Summary of reduplication effects:
  • Adjective AA → vivid, warm, or soft quality
  • Verb ABAB → brief, casual, low-pressure action
  • 一 + MW + MW → gradual, one-at-a-time progression
