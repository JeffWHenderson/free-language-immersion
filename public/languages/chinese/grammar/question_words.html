Question Words — 什么, 谁, 哪里...

Chinese question words (疑问词, yíwèn cí) stay in the same position as the information they're asking about. Unlike English, you do not move the question word to the front of the sentence. The word order of a statement and a question is the same — only the question word changes.

Question Words Stay in Place

Compare the statement and the question — the structure is identical. The question word simply replaces the unknown information in the same position.

  • 你吃什么？ Nǐ chī shénme? — What are you eating? (NOT 什么你吃?)
  • 那个人是谁？ Nàge rén shì shéi? — Who is that person?
  • 你住在哪里？ Nǐ zhù zài nǎlǐ? — Where do you live?
  • 你什么时候来？ Nǐ shénme shíhou lái? — When are you coming?
  • 你为什么不去？ Nǐ wèishénme bù qù? — Why aren't you going?
  • 这个多少钱？ Zhège duōshao qián? — How much does this cost?
  • 你要哪个？ Nǐ yào nǎge? — Which one do you want?
  • 你怎么去？ Nǐ zěnme qù? — How are you getting there?
