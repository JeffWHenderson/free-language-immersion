Job Title Patterns — 师 员 家 生 人

Chinese job titles end in a small set of suffixes that signal what kind of role a person has. Recognize the suffix and you can guess what an unfamiliar title means.

师 (shī) — skilled practitioner / master

Implies trained expertise or mastery of a craft. Used for roles requiring serious skill.

  • 老师 lǎoshī — teacher (老 = respected/elder)
  • 工程师 gōngchéngshī — engineer (工程 = engineering)
  • 厨师 chúshī — chef (厨 = kitchen)
  • 律师 lǜshī — lawyer (律 = law/rule)
  • 设计师 shèjìshī — designer (设计 = design)

员 (yuán) — member / staff

A worker who is part of an organization or performs a defined function. More about the role than mastery.

  • 服务员 fúwùyuán — waiter / service staff (服务 = service)
  • 售货员 shòuhuòyuán — sales clerk (售货 = sell goods)
  • 运动员 yùndòngyuán — athlete (运动 = sports/exercise)
  • 飞行员 fēixíngyuán — pilot (飞行 = fly)

家 (jiā) — expert / specialist in a field

A person recognized as an authority in a field — often intellectual, artistic, or creative.

  • 作家 zuòjiā — writer / author (作 = write/create)
  • 音乐家 yīnyuèjiā — musician (音乐 = music)
  • 艺术家 yìshùjiā — artist (艺术 = art)
  • 科学家 kēxuéjiā — scientist (科学 = science)
  • 政治家 zhèngzhìjiā — politician (政治 = politics)

生 (shēng) — student / person in training

Someone who is learning, studying, or working under supervision. Also used in some professional titles.

  • 学生 xuésheng — student (学 = study/learn)
  • 医生 yīshēng — doctor (医 = medicine) — one who practices medicine
  • 实习生 shíxíshēng — intern (实习 = internship/practice)
  • 研究生 yánjiūshēng — graduate student (研究 = research)

人 (rén) — person doing something

The most general suffix — simply "person who does X." Often informal or descriptive.

  • 商人 shāngrén — businessman (商 = commerce/trade)
  • 工人 gōngrén — worker / laborer (工 = work)
  • 中国人 zhōngguórén — Chinese person (中国 = China)
  • 主持人 zhǔchírén — host / presenter (主持 = host/chair)
