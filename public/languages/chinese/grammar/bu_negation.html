不 & 没 — Negation

Chinese has two main negation words. Choosing the right one depends on the verb and the time frame.

不 (bù/bú)没 (méi) / 没有 (méiyǒu)
Used withmost verbs, adjectives, present/future有 (to have), completed past actions
Meaningdon't / won't / notdidn't / haven't / don't have

不 — general negation

  • 我不去。 I'm not going. / I won't go.
  • 我不喜欢。 I don't like it.
  • 这不好。 This is not good.

没 — negating 有 and past actions

  • 我没有钱。 I don't have money.
  • 他没吃。 He didn't eat.
  • 我没去过。 I've never been there.

不 vs. 没 — the key difference

ChineseEnglishWhy
我不去。I won't go. (future)不 = intention/choice
我没去。I didn't go. (past)没 = completed action didn't happen

Tone change

不 is normally 4th tone (bù), but changes to 2nd tone (bú) before another 4th-tone syllable: 不要 → bú yào, 不是 → bú shì.
