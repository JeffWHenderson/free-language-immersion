Direction Complements — 上来, 出去, 回来

Direction complements are added to movement verbs to show the direction of movement relative to the speaker. They are two-syllable combinations: a direction word (上/下/进/出/回/过/起) plus either 来 (lái, toward the speaker) or 去 (qù, away from the speaker).

来 vs 去

来 = the movement is toward the speaker's current location. 去 = the movement is away from the speaker's current location.

Common Direction Complements

ComplementPinyinMeaning
上来shàng láicome up (toward speaker, upward)
上去shàng qùgo up (away from speaker, upward)
下来xià láicome down (toward speaker, downward)
下去xià qùgo down (away from speaker, downward)
进来jìn láicome in (toward speaker, inward)
出去chū qùgo out (away from speaker, outward)
回来huí láicome back (return toward speaker)
回去huí qùgo back (return away from speaker)
过来guò láicome over (toward speaker)
过去guò qùgo over (away from speaker)

Examples in Context

  • 请进来！ Qǐng jìn lái! — Please come in!
  • 他出去了。 Tā chū qù le. — He went out.
  • 你什么时候回来？ Nǐ shénme shíhou huí lái? — When are you coming back?
  • 快过来！ Kuài guò lái! — Come over here quickly!
  • 他爬上去了。 Tā pá shàng qù le. — He climbed up (away from me).
  • 菜端上来了。 Cài duān shàng lái le. — The food has been brought up / served.
