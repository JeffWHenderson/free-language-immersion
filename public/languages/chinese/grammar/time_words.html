Time Words — 时间词 (Shíjiān Cí)

Chinese time words are simple vocabulary items, but their position in the sentence follows a specific rule that differs from English.

Core time words

Position rule — time comes BEFORE the verb

In Chinese, time expressions come before the verb — either at the very start of the sentence or right after the subject. They never go at the end.

correct 我今天去。/ 今天我去。 I'm going today. (time before verb)
incorrect 我去今天。 time at the end — ungrammatical in Chinese

Frequency words

  • 每天 měitiān — every day
  • 有时候 yǒushíhou — sometimes
  • 经常 jīngcháng — often / frequently
  • 从来不 cónglái bù — never

Examples

  • 我昨天很累。 I was very tired yesterday.
  • 明天我们去哪里？ Where are we going tomorrow?
  • 她每天早上喝咖啡。 She drinks coffee every morning.
  • 你星期几有空？ What day of the week are you free?
