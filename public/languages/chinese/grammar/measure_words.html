Measure Words (量词)

In Chinese, you cannot put a number directly before a noun. You must use a measure word (量词, liàngcí) in between: Number + Measure Word + Noun.

Structure

  • 一杯茶 one cup of tea   (一 + 杯 + 茶)
  • 两碗米饭 two bowls of rice   (两 + 碗 + 米饭)

Common food and drink measure words

Measure wordPinyinUsed forExample
个gègeneral / people / many objects一个鸡蛋 — one egg
杯bēicups and glasses一杯咖啡 — a cup of coffee
碗wǎnbowls一碗面条 — a bowl of noodles
瓶píngbottles一瓶水 — a bottle of water
盘pánplates / dishes一盘饺子 — a plate of dumplings
片piànslices / flat pieces一片面包 — a slice of bread

Two vs. 二

Use 两 (liǎng) — not 二 (èr) — when saying "two" before a measure word:

  • 两碗米饭 two bowls of rice ✓
  • 二碗米饭 two bowls of rice ✗ (incorrect)
