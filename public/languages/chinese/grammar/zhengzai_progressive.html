正在 — Progressive Aspect

Chinese expresses ongoing actions using 正在 (zhèngzài), 在 (zài), or 呢 (ne) — or a combination. These mark that an action is happening right now, at this moment. Unlike English "-ing," these are optional markers — context often makes it clear without them.

Three Ways to Mark Progressive

PatternExampleEnglish
正在 + V我正在工作。I am (right now) working.
正在 + V + 呢我正在工作呢。I'm working (right now — added emphasis).
在 + V他在开会。He is in a meeting / having a meeting.
V + 呢他睡觉呢。He's sleeping (implied: don't disturb him).

More Examples

  • 她正在学习呢。 Tā zhèngzài xuéxí ne. — She's studying right now.
  • 他们在练习唱歌。 Tāmen zài liànxí chànggē. — They are practicing singing.
  • 我正在写作。 Wǒ zhèngzài xiězuò. — I'm writing right now.
  • 她在画画呢。 Tā zài huàhuà ne. — She's painting.

Negation: 没(有)在 + V

To say someone is not currently doing something, use 没有在 or 没在 before the verb.

  • 他没有在工作。 Tā méiyǒu zài gōngzuò. — He's not working (right now).
  • 我没在睡觉。 Wǒ méi zài shuìjiào. — I'm not sleeping.
  • 他们没有在开会。 Tāmen méiyǒu zài kāihuì. — They aren't in a meeting.
