如果...就... — If / Then Conditionals

如果 (rúguǒ) means "if" and 就 (jiù) means "then." Together they form the standard conditional pattern: 如果 + condition, 就 + result. The subject usually appears before 如果 if it applies to the whole sentence, or inside the clause if it differs. In casual speech, 就 is often dropped — the sentence still sounds natural, but including both makes the logical link clearer.

Full Pattern: 如果...就...

如果你难过，就告诉我。Rúguǒ nǐ nánguò, jiù gàosù wǒ.If you're sad, tell me.

Informal Alternative: 要是...就...

要是 (yàoshi) is a conversational equivalent of 如果. It has the same meaning and structure, but feels slightly more relaxed. Native speakers use both freely.

  • 要是你不开心，就来找我。 Yàoshi nǐ bù kāixīn, jiù lái zhǎo wǒ. — If you're not happy, come find me.
  • 要是有问题，就问我。 Yàoshi yǒu wèntí, jiù wèn wǒ. — If there's a problem, ask me.

Omitting 就 in Speech

In everyday speech, 就 is often left out. The meaning stays the same — the 如果 clause signals that what follows is the result.

  • 如果你害怕，告诉我一声。 Rúguǒ nǐ hàipà, gàosù wǒ yīshēng. — If you're scared, let me know.
  • 如果没时间，不用来。 Rúguǒ méi shíjiān, bùyòng lái. — If you don't have time, you don't need to come.

Contrast: 只要...就... (As Long As)

如果...就... expresses a simple if/then condition. 只要...就... (zhǐyào...jiù...) is stronger — it means "as long as / provided that," implying that the one condition is all that's needed.

  • 如果你努力，就会成功。 Rúguǒ nǐ nǔlì, jiù huì chénggōng. — If you work hard, you will succeed.
  • 只要你努力，就一定会成功。 Zhǐyào nǐ nǔlì, jiù yīdìng huì chénggōng. — As long as you work hard, you will definitely succeed.
