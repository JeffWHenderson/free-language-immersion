有 & 没有 — Have / Don't Have / There Is

有 (yǒu) means both "to have" and "there is/are." 没有 (méiyǒu) is its negation — "don't have" or "there isn't."

有 = to have

  • 我有预约。 I have an appointment.
  • 你有药吗？ Do you have medicine?
  • 我没有保险。 I don't have insurance.

有 = there is / there are

  • 附近有医院。 There is a hospital nearby.
  • 这里有药店吗？ Is there a pharmacy here?
  • 这里没有医生。 There is no doctor here.

Always use 没有 — never 不有

有 is the only common verb negated with 没 instead of 不:

  • 我没有钱。 ✓ I don't have money.
  • 我不有钱。 ✗ (incorrect)

有没有 — asking either/or

Instead of adding 吗, you can ask "do you have or not?" with 有没有:

  • 你有没有药？ Do you have medicine (or not)?
  • 附近有没有医院？ Is there a hospital nearby?
