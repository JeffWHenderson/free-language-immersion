因为...所以... — Because...So...

因为 (yīnwèi) means "because" and 所以 (suǒyǐ) means "so" or "therefore." In Chinese, both connectors are usually used together in the same sentence — unlike English, where you choose either "because" or "so," not both. This is one of the most noticeable structural differences between Chinese and English causal sentences.

Full Pattern: 因为...所以...

ChinesePinyinEnglish
因为很累，所以我很烦躁。Yīnwèi hěn lèi, suǒyǐ wǒ hěn fánzào.Because I'm very tired, so I'm irritable.
因为他迟到了，所以大家都很生气。Yīnwèi tā chídào le, suǒyǐ dàjiā dōu hěn shēngqì.Because he was late, everyone was angry.
因为天气很好，所以我很开心。Yīnwèi tiānqì hěn hǎo, suǒyǐ wǒ hěn kāixīn.Because the weather is nice, I'm happy.

因为 Alone (giving a reason)

因为 can appear alone to explain a reason, often in answer to a 为什么 question.

  • 因为下雨，我们取消了计划。 Yīnwèi xià yǔ, wǒmen qǔxiāo le jìhuà. — Because it rained, we cancelled our plans.
  • 因为工作太多，我很焦虑。 Yīnwèi gōngzuò tài duō, wǒ hěn jiāolǜ. — Because there's too much work, I'm anxious.

所以 Alone (stating the result)

所以 can be used alone to introduce the consequence when the cause is already known.

  • 他迟到了，所以我很生气。 Tā chídào le, suǒyǐ wǒ hěn shēngqì. — He was late, so I was angry.
  • 我没睡好，所以今天很累。 Wǒ méi shuì hǎo, suǒyǐ jīntiān hěn lèi. — I didn't sleep well, so I'm very tired today.
  • 她很难过，所以哭了。 Tā hěn nánguò, suǒyǐ kū le. — She was very upset, so she cried.
