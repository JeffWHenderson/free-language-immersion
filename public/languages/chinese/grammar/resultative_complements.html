Resultative Complements — 吃完, 做好

A resultative complement is a second morpheme added directly to a verb to express the result of the action. Together they form a compound verb. This is one of the most productive and important structures in Chinese — once you learn the pattern, you can combine many verbs and results.

Common Resultative Complements

CompoundPinyinMeaning
吃完chī wánfinish eating (eat to completion)
做好zuò hǎofinish making / do it right / ready
喝光hē guāngdrink it all up (drink until empty)
买到mǎi dàosuccessfully buy / manage to buy
找到zhǎo dàofind successfully / locate
切好qiē hǎocut properly / finish cutting

Examples in Context

  • 我吃完了。 Wǒ chī wán le. — I've finished eating.
  • 饭做好了！ Fàn zuò hǎo le! — The food is ready!
  • 他把汤喝光了。 Tā bǎ tāng hē guāng le. — He drank up all the soup.
  • 我在市场买到了新鲜的鱼。 Wǒ zài shìchǎng mǎi dào le xīnxiān de yú. — I managed to buy fresh fish at the market.

Negation with 没

To negate a resultative complement, use 没 before the whole compound verb. The 了 is dropped.

  • 我没吃完。 Wǒ méi chī wán. — I didn't finish eating.
  • 我没找到菜刀。 Wǒ méi zhǎo dào càidāo. — I couldn't find the cleaver.

Potential Form: V + 得/不 + Result

Insert 得 (de) to say "can" or 不 to say "can't" accomplish the result.

  • 你吃得完吗？ Nǐ chī de wán ma? — Can you finish eating it?
  • 我吃不完。 Wǒ chī bu wán. — I can't finish it.
  • 这么多，我做不好。 Zhème duō, wǒ zuò bù hǎo. — There's so much, I can't do it properly.
