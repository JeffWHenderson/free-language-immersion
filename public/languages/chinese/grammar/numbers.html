Numbers — 数字 (Shùzì)

Chinese numbers are highly logical. Once you learn 0–10, you can build almost any number by combining them.

Building larger numbers

The system is purely additive and multiplicative — no irregular forms:

  • 十一 11 (ten-one)
  • 十二 12 (ten-two)
  • 二十 20 (two-ten)
  • 二十一 21 (two-ten-one)
  • 三十 30 (three-ten)
  • 一百 100 (one-hundred)

两 (liǎng) vs. 二 (èr)

Use 两 (liǎng) instead of 二 when counting people or things with a measure word. 二 is used in math, phone numbers, and building larger numbers.

两 + measure word 两个人 two people — correct
二 + measure word 二个人 two people — sounds unnatural
  • 两本书 two books (两 + 本 measure word for books)
  • 两杯水 two cups of water (两 + 杯 measure word for cups)

Telling time — X点

Use X点 (diǎn) for "X o'clock." Add 半 (bàn) for half past:

  • 两点 2:00 (note: 两, not 二)
  • 三点半 3:30
  • 七点十五分 7:15

Prices — X块 / X元

块 (kuài) is the spoken unit of currency (like "buck"); 元 (yuán) is more formal (like "dollar"):

  • 五块钱 5 yuan / 5 RMB
  • 二十块 20 yuan

Practical sentences

  • 我要两杯咖啡。 I want two coffees.
  • 现在几点？三点半。 What time is it now? 3:30.
  • 这个多少钱？十五块。 How much is this? 15 yuan.
  • 我们班有二十个学生。 Our class has 20 students.
