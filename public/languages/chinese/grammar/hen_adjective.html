很 (hěn) + Adjective

In Chinese, adjectives can serve directly as predicates (no verb "to be" needed). But in positive statements, 很 is almost always required before the adjective — even when you don't mean "very."

Structure

Subject + 很 + adjective

  • 我很高兴。 I am happy. (not necessarily "very" happy)
  • 他很忙。 He is busy.
  • 食物很好吃。 The food is delicious.

Why 很 is required

Without 很, the sentence implies a comparison:

  • 他忙。 He is busy (compared to others). ← sounds like a contrast
  • 他很忙。 He is busy. ← neutral statement

Negation — replace 很 with 不

  • 不贵。 It's not expensive.
  • 我不累。 I'm not tired.
