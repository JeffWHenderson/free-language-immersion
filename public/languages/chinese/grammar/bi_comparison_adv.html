比 — Advanced Comparisons

You already know the basic 比 (bǐ) pattern: A 比 B + adjective means "A is more [adjective] than B." This lesson builds on that foundation with 更 and 还 for extra emphasis, the superlative 最, negative comparisons with 没有...那么, and degree phrases that show by how much something differs.

Adding 更 or 还 for Emphasis

更 (gèng) and 还 (hái) both mean "even more" when placed before the adjective in a 比 sentence. 还 is slightly more colloquial and often implies surprise.

ChinesePinyinEnglish
今天比昨天更冷。Jīntiān bǐ zuótiān gèng lěng.Today is even colder than yesterday.
他比我还努力。Tā bǐ wǒ hái nǔlì.He works even harder than me.
这份工作比上一份还有趣。Zhè fèn gōngzuò bǐ shàng yī fèn hái yǒuqù.This job is even more interesting than the last one.

Superlative: 最 (The Most)

最 (zuì) means "the most" and is placed directly before the adjective. No 比 is needed in superlative sentences.

  • 他是我们团队里最有经验的人。 Tā shì wǒmen tuánduì lǐ zuì yǒu jīngyàn de rén. — He is the most experienced person on our team.
  • 这是我做过最有意思的项目。 Zhè shì wǒ zuò guò zuì yǒu yìsi de xiàngmù. — This is the most interesting project I've ever done.
  • 我最喜欢周末画画。 Wǒ zuì xǐhuān zhōumò huàhuà. — What I like most is painting on weekends.

Negative Comparison: 没有...那么 (Not As...As)

A 没有 B + adjective means "A is not as [adjective] as B." You can also add 那么 (nàme) or 这么 (zhème) before the adjective to soften it slightly.

ChinesePinyinEnglish
这份工作没有那份轻松。Zhè fèn gōngzuò méiyǒu nà fèn qīngsōng.This job isn't as relaxed as that one.
他没有你那么有耐心。Tā méiyǒu nǐ nàme yǒu nàixīn.He isn't as patient as you.
今天没有昨天那么忙。Jīntiān méiyǒu zuótiān nàme máng.Today isn't as busy as yesterday.

Degree Phrases: By How Much

To say "a little more" or "much more," add a degree word after the adjective in a 比 sentence. Common degree phrases: 一点 (yīdiǎn — a little), 多了 (duō le — a lot more), 得多 (de duō — much more).

  • 他比我忙一点。 Tā bǐ wǒ máng yīdiǎn. — He's a little busier than me.
  • 这家公司比那家大多了。 Zhè jiā gōngsī bǐ nà jiā dà duō le. — This company is much bigger than that one.
  • 新工作比旧工作好得多。 Xīn gōngzuò bǐ jiù gōngzuò hǎo de duō. — The new job is much better than the old one.
