Location Word Patterns — 店 馆 场 院 站

Chinese place names are built from recognizable suffixes. Once you know these five characters, you can decode hundreds of unfamiliar location names.

店 (diàn) — shop / store

Sells something. The product or service comes first.

  • 咖啡店 kāfēi diàn — coffee shop
  • 书店 shū diàn — bookstore (书 = book)
  • 药店 yào diàn — pharmacy (药 = medicine)
  • 面包店 miànbāo diàn — bakery (面包 = bread)

馆 (guǎn) — hall / public establishment

A more formal, often public or cultural space — not just for buying things.

  • 图书馆 túshū guǎn — library (图书 = books/documents)
  • 博物馆 bówù guǎn — museum (博物 = broad knowledge of things)
  • 饭馆 fàn guǎn — restaurant (饭 = food/rice)
  • 体育馆 tǐyù guǎn — gymnasium (体育 = sports/physical education)

场 (chǎng) — field / ground / area

An open or large-scale functional space, often for transportation or activity.

  • 机场 jī chǎng — airport (机 = machine/plane, short for 飞机)
  • 广场 guǎng chǎng — plaza / public square (广 = broad/wide)
  • 停车场 tíngchē chǎng — parking lot (停车 = parking)
  • 操场 cāo chǎng — sports field / playground

院 (yuàn) — courtyard / institution

A formal, enclosed institution — often official, medical, or academic.

  • 医院 yī yuàn — hospital (医 = medicine/healing)
  • 电影院 diànyǐng yuàn — cinema (电影 = movie, lit: electric shadow)
  • 法院 fǎ yuàn — court of law (法 = law)
  • 学院 xué yuàn — college / academy (学 = study)

站 (zhàn) — station / stop

A transit point — where you catch or transfer transportation.

  • 火车站 huǒchē zhàn — train station (火车 = train, lit: fire vehicle)
  • 地铁站 dìtiě zhàn — subway station (地铁 = subway, lit: underground iron)
  • 公交站 gōngjiāo zhàn — bus stop
  • 加油站 jiāyóu zhàn — gas station (加油 = add fuel)
