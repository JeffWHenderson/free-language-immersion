了 — Completed Action & Change of State

了 (le) has two main uses. The first is placed directly after a verb to mark a completed action. The second is placed at the end of a sentence to signal that something has changed or a new situation now exists. These two uses are distinct, and understanding them will unlock a huge amount of everyday Chinese.

Use 1: Completed Action (verb + 了)

Place 了 immediately after the verb to show the action was completed. This is not quite the same as English past tense — 了 is about completion, not just time.

ChinesePinyinEnglish
我吃了。Wǒ chī le.I ate. / I've eaten.
他来了。Tā lái le.He came. / He has arrived.
我买了一本书。Wǒ mǎi le yī běn shū.I bought a book.
她喝了一杯水。Tā hē le yī bēi shuǐ.She drank a glass of water.

Use 2: Change of State (sentence-final 了)

了 at the end of a sentence signals that a new situation exists now — something has changed. This is sometimes called the "change of state" 了.

ChinesePinyinEnglish
下雨了。Xià yǔ le.It's raining now. (It started raining.)
我明白了。Wǒ míngbai le.I understand now. (I get it.)
太晚了。Tài wǎn le.It's too late now.
我饿了。Wǒ è le.I'm hungry now. (I've gotten hungry.)

Negation: Use 没(有), Not 不

To negate a completed action, use 没 or 没有 before the verb — never use 不 with 了. The 了 is dropped in the negative.

  • 我没吃。 Wǒ méi chī. — I didn't eat. / I haven't eaten.
  • 他没来。 Tā méi lái. — He didn't come.
  • 我没买书。 Wǒ méi mǎi shū. — I didn't buy a book.
