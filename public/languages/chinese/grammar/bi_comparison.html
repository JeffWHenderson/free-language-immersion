比 — Comparisons

To compare two things in Chinese, use the pattern A 比 B + adjective. This means "A is more [adjective] than B." The adjective goes at the end — do not add 更 (gèng, more) unless adding extra emphasis. For the opposite — "A is not as [adjective] as B" — use A 没有 B + adjective.

A 比 B + Adjective (A is more... than B)

ChinesePinyinEnglish
这个比那个好吃。Zhège bǐ nàge hǎochī.This is tastier than that.
红烧肉比沙拉辣。Hóngshāo ròu bǐ shālā là.Red-braised pork is spicier than salad.
苹果比巧克力甜。Píngguǒ bǐ qiǎokèlì tián.Apples are sweeter than chocolate.
这家餐厅比那家贵。Zhè jiā cāntīng bǐ nà jiā guì.This restaurant is more expensive than that one.

A 没有 B + Adjective (A is not as... as B)

ChinesePinyinEnglish
这个没有那个贵。Zhège méiyǒu nàge guì.This is not as expensive as that.
这道菜没有那道菜辣。Zhè dào cài méiyǒu nà dào cài là.This dish is not as spicy as that one.
超市的蔬菜没有市场的新鲜。Chāoshì de shūcài méiyǒu shìchǎng de xīnxiān.The supermarket vegetables are not as fresh as the market's.

Adding Degree: 一点, 多了, 得多

To say "a little more" or "much more," add a degree word after the adjective.

  • 这个比那个甜一点。 Zhège bǐ nàge tián yīdiǎn. — This is a little sweeter than that.
  • 这家比那家便宜多了。 Zhè jiā bǐ nà jiā piányí duō le. — This place is much cheaper than that one.
  • 这个味道比那个好得多。 Zhège wèidào bǐ nàge hǎo de duō. — This flavor is much better than that one.
