虽然...但是... — Although...But...

虽然 (suīrán) means "although" and 但是 (dànshì) means "but." Just like 因为...所以..., both halves of this concessive pair are usually used together in Chinese. In English you choose one — "although X, Y" or "X, but Y." In Chinese, you typically say both: 虽然X，但是Y.

Full Pattern: 虽然...但是...

ChinesePinyinEnglish
虽然很难，但是我很开心。Suīrán hěn nán, dànshì wǒ hěn kāixīn.Although it's difficult, I'm happy.
虽然累，但是很满足。Suīrán lèi, dànshì hěn mǎnzú.Although tired, I feel very satisfied.
虽然他很忙，但是他总是帮助别人。Suīrán tā hěn máng, dànshì tā zǒngshì bāngzhù biérén.Although he's very busy, he always helps others.
虽然天气不好，但是我们还是出去了。Suīrán tiānqì bù hǎo, dànshì wǒmen háishi chūqù le.Although the weather was bad, we still went out.

Softer Alternatives: 不过 and 可是

不过 (búguò) and 可是 (kěshì) both mean "but" and are slightly softer or more conversational than 但是.

  • 我很想去，不过有点累。 Wǒ hěn xiǎng qù, búguò yǒudiǎn lèi. — I really want to go, but I'm a bit tired.
  • 她说很开心，可是看起来很难过。 Tā shuō hěn kāixīn, kěshì kàn qǐlái hěn nánguò. — She said she was happy, but she looked upset.

Emotion Contexts

  • 虽然很伤心，但是我理解。 Suīrán hěn shāngxīn, dànshì wǒ lǐjiě. — Although I'm very sad, I understand.
  • 虽然压力很大，但是他没有放弃。 Suīrán yālì hěn dà, dànshì tā méiyǒu fàngqì. — Although the pressure was great, he didn't give up.
