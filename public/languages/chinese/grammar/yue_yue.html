越来越 & 越...越... — More and More / The More...The More

You already know 越来越 (yuè lái yuè) from the Human Body topic: it means "more and more" and describes something that is gradually increasing over time. This lesson introduces the parallel pattern 越A越B — "the more A, the more B" — where two changes escalate together. The two structures look similar but work differently.

Quick Review: 越来越 + Adjective/Verb

越来越 expresses a single thing that grows progressively. It describes a trend with no paired condition.

ChinesePinyinEnglish
她越来越开心。Tā yuè lái yuè kāixīn.She's getting happier and happier.
他越来越担心。Tā yuè lái yuè dānxīn.He's getting more and more worried.
天气越来越冷了。Tiānqì yuè lái yuè lěng le.The weather is getting colder and colder.

越A越B — The More A, The More B

This pattern links two clauses: as A increases, B increases along with it. The first 越 introduces the condition; the second introduces the consequence. Both A and B can be adjectives, verbs, or verb phrases. The subject can appear before the first 越 or be shared across both clauses.

ChinesePinyinEnglish
越想越难过。Yuè xiǎng yuè nánguò.The more I think about it, the sadder I feel.
越说越开心。Yuè shuō yuè kāixīn.The more we talk, the happier I get.
这首歌越听越好听。Zhè shǒu gē yuè tīng yuè hǎotīng.The more you listen to this song, the better it sounds.
他越跑越快。Tā yuè pǎo yuè kuài.The more he runs, the faster he gets.

Emotions with 越A越B

This pattern is especially common when describing emotional escalation — feelings that spiral as a thought or situation develops.

  • 她越哭越难受。 Tā yuè kū yuè nánshòu. — The more she cried, the worse she felt.
  • 越等越着急。 Yuè děng yuè zháojí. — The longer I wait, the more anxious I get.
  • 我们越聊越投机。 Wǒmen yuè liáo yuè tóujī. — The more we chatted, the better we got along.

Side by Side: Structural Comparison

  • 她越来越紧张。 Tā yuè lái yuè jǐnzhāng. — She's getting more and more nervous. (one trend, over time)
  • 她越想越紧张。 Tā yuè xiǎng yuè jǐnzhāng. — The more she thinks about it, the more nervous she gets. (two linked changes)
