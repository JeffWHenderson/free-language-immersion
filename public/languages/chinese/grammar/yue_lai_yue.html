越来越 — More and More

越来越 (yuè lái yuè) followed by an adjective or verb means "more and more" or "increasingly." It expresses a progressive change over time. A related pattern, 越...越..., links two clauses: "the more [X], the more [Y]."

越来越 + Adjective/Verb

ChinesePinyinEnglish
越来越好yuè lái yuè hǎogetting better and better
越来越疼yuè lái yuè ténghurting more and more
越来越累yuè lái yuè lèigetting more and more tired
越来越健康yuè lái yuè jiànkānggetting healthier and healthier

Examples with 越来越

  • 我的身体越来越好了。 Wǒ de shēntǐ yuè lái yuè hǎo le. — My health is getting better and better.
  • 我的腿越来越疼。 Wǒ de tuǐ yuè lái yuè téng. — My legs are hurting more and more.
  • 走了这么远，我越来越累。 Zǒu le zhème yuǎn, wǒ yuè lái yuè lèi. — After walking so far, I'm getting more and more tired.

越...越... (The more..., the more...)

This paired pattern links a condition and a result that escalate together. The first 越 introduces the condition, the second introduces the consequence.

  • 越吃越想吃。 Yuè chī yuè xiǎng chī. — The more I eat, the more I want to eat.
  • 越走越累。 Yuè zǒu yuè lèi. — The more I walk, the more tired I get.
  • 越休息越好。 Yuè xiūxi yuè hǎo. — The more rest you get, the better.
  • 他的病越来越重，医生越来越担心。 Tā de bìng yuè lái yuè zhòng, yīshēng yuè lái yuè dānxīn. — His illness is getting more and more serious; the doctor is getting more and more worried.
